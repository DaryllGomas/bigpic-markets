# Enterprise Security

*Sector analysis for cybersecurity investment thesis | February 2026*

---

## Market Overview

The global cybersecurity market stands at approximately $235-272 billion in 2025, depending on scope definitions, with projections pointing to $350-470 billion by 2030-2031 at a 9-13% CAGR. McKinsey estimates that AI is expanding the total addressable market for cybersecurity providers to $2 trillion when including currently unaddressed risks. U.S. and Western Europe account for over 70% of global information security spending.

The enterprise security subsegments covered here -- endpoint/XDR, next-gen firewalls, SIEM/SOAR, MDR, vulnerability management, and AI-powered threat detection -- represent the core operational security stack that every organization must maintain. Together they constitute roughly $60-80 billion of the total market and are growing faster than the overall cybersecurity average due to AI adoption, regulatory mandates (SEC disclosure rules, cyber insurance requirements), and an escalating threat landscape where AI-powered attacks demand AI-powered defense.

Cybersecurity M&A activity in 2025 was extraordinary: over 420 deals were announced with total disclosed value exceeding $84 billion. Eight acquisitions surpassed $1 billion individually, accounting for nearly $75 billion of that total. This consolidation wave signals the market's maturation and the growing dominance of platform vendors.

## Endpoint Security & XDR

**Market Size:** The EDR market is expected to reach $5.1 billion in 2025, growing at a 24.8% CAGR to $15.5 billion by 2030. The broader endpoint security market is $21-27 billion in 2025 (depending on definition), growing at 7-11% CAGR. XDR, which integrates endpoint, network, and cloud telemetry, is growing at 20-21% CAGR and is rapidly becoming the default deployment model.

### CrowdStrike (CRWD) -- The Platform Leader

**Market Cap:** ~$105 billion | **FY2025 Revenue:** $3.95 billion (+29% YoY) | **Q3 FY2026 Ending ARR:** $4.92 billion (+23% YoY)

CrowdStrike is the dominant pure-play endpoint/XDR vendor and increasingly the operating system of enterprise security. The Falcon platform's single-agent architecture provides a natural consolidation point, and the company's land-and-expand model continues to deliver strong module adoption metrics.

**Platform Metrics (Q3 FY2026, October 2025):**
- Module adoption: 49% with 6+ modules, 34% with 7+ modules, 24% with 8+ modules, all trending upward quarter-over-quarter
- Falcon Flex subscription model ARR: $1.35 billion, growing 200%+ YoY
- Record Q3 net new ARR of $265 million despite a difficult post-July 2024 outage comparison period

**Charlotte AI:** CrowdStrike's AI assistant has evolved from a chatbot into what the company calls "Mission-Ready Agentic AI" handling alert triage, incident response, and threat hunting autonomously. Charlotte AI is a key differentiator for SOC productivity and a significant attach-rate driver.

**Path to $10B ARR:** Management has articulated a clear roadmap to $10 billion in ending ARR, driven by Falcon Flex demand and innovation across next-gen SIEM, cloud, identity, and exposure management. The stock gained approximately 38% in 2025 despite the July 2024 outage overhang.

**Investment Thesis:** CrowdStrike is the best-positioned platform for security consolidation with the highest module adoption velocity and strongest brand in endpoint. The key risk is valuation -- at ~$105 billion market cap (~21x forward ARR), the stock requires continued execution at scale. The July 2024 outage provided a stress test of customer loyalty, and the recovery has been strong, but it remains a brand risk overhang.

### SentinelOne (S) -- AI-Native Challenger

**Market Cap:** ~$5.2 billion | **FY2025 ARR:** ~$860 million | **Q3 FY2026 ARR:** $1.055 billion (+23% YoY)

SentinelOne positions itself as the AI-native alternative to CrowdStrike, with the Singularity platform and Purple AI representing genuine technical differentiation. The company crossed a significant inflection in FY2025 by achieving its first full year of positive net income and free cash flow.

**Key Metrics:**
- Revenue growth: 23% YoY in Q3 FY2026
- International revenue now 40% of total, growing 34% YoY
- Non-endpoint solutions represent over 50% of bookings -- a critical milestone showing platform diversification
- Operating margin improved to 7% with 6% free cash flow margin
- Remaining Performance Obligations: $1.3 billion (+35% YoY)

**Purple AI:** The company's AI-powered security analyst delivers triple-digit growth with 30%+ attach rates. The "Athena" release introduced deep security reasoning, agentic detection and response, and hyperautomation workflows compatible with any SIEM or data source. Purple AI achieved FedRAMP-High authorization -- the first cybersecurity AI analyst to reach that level.

**PinnacleOne Advisory:** SentinelOne acquired Krebs Stamos Group in late 2023 and rebranded it as PinnacleOne Strategic Advisory Group, led by Chris Krebs and Alex Stamos. The unit provides strategic risk analysis and advisory services, though Krebs departed in April 2025.

**Investment Thesis:** At ~$5.2 billion market cap (~5x forward ARR), SentinelOne is significantly cheaper than CrowdStrike and offers higher relative upside if the company continues its profitability trajectory and Purple AI gains traction. The risk is that CrowdStrike's scale advantages and Charlotte AI could limit SentinelOne's addressable market. The path to sustained profitability is the key unlock.

## Next-Generation Firewalls & Network Security

**Market Size:** The NGFW market is $5.9-6.7 billion in 2025, growing at 11-13% CAGR to $12-17 billion by 2030-2034. Despite predictions of the firewall's demise, demand remains robust, driven by the ongoing FortiGate refresh cycle, hybrid cloud requirements, and the convergence of NGFW with SASE and SD-WAN.

### Palo Alto Networks (PANW) -- The Platformization Architect

**Market Cap:** ~$138 billion | **FY2025 Revenue:** ~$9.2 billion (+15% YoY) | **FY2026 Guidance:** $10.48-10.53 billion (+14% YoY)

Palo Alto Networks has become the largest pure-play cybersecurity company globally. The "platformization" strategy -- consolidating customers onto the Strata (network), Prisma (cloud/SASE), and Cortex (SOC) pillars -- is working, but valuation remains demanding.

**Platform Metrics:**
- Next-Gen Security ARR: $5.9 billion (+29% YoY)
- SASE ARR growing 35% YoY, ~6,300 SASE customers
- 60%+ of Q4 FY2025 Network Security bookings from SASE and software
- AI ARR: ~$545 million, up 2.5x+ YoY

**Cortex XSIAM:** The fastest-growing product in company history, now in its third major iteration. Approximately 400 XSIAM customers with average ARR per customer exceeding $1 million. Over 60% of XSIAM customers achieve mean time to resolution (MTTR) under 10 minutes. XSIAM is positioned as the next-gen SIEM replacement and SOC operating platform.

**Precision AI:** Palo Alto's proprietary ML system detects and blocks threats in real-time with near-zero latency. This underpins product differentiation across all three platform pillars.

**M&A Strategy:**
- CyberArk acquisition ($25 billion, pending regulatory close in H2 FY2026) -- adds identity security and privileged access management, making PANW a one-stop-shop for network + SOC + identity
- Chronosphere acquisition ($3.35 billion, expected FY2026 close) -- adds cloud-native observability with $160M+ ARR
- Protect AI (closed July 2025) -- AI/ML pipeline security
- IBM QRadar assets integration ongoing

**Investment Thesis:** PANW is the most ambitious consolidation play in cybersecurity. The CyberArk acquisition, if completed, creates a platform spanning network security, cloud security, SOC operations, and identity -- nearly the entire enterprise security stack. At ~$138 billion market cap (~15x FY2025 revenue), the stock is priced for continued execution. Key risks include integration complexity of multiple acquisitions, the CyberArk regulatory approval process, and the possibility that platformization benefits are already priced in.

### Fortinet (FTNT) -- The Volume Leader

**Market Cap:** ~$59-60 billion | **FY2025 Revenue:** $6.80 billion (+15% YoY) | **FY2026 Guidance:** $7.50-7.70 billion (+12% YoY)

Fortinet holds the #1 position in firewalls by unit volume with 55% unit market share. The company operates with a unique vertically integrated model (custom ASICs) that delivers superior price-performance, making it the default choice for mid-market and cost-conscious enterprise buyers.

**FortiGate Refresh Cycle:** The multi-year FortiGate hardware refresh cycle is a major near-term catalyst. As of Q4 2025, 40-50% of the major 2026 refresh is complete, with ongoing upgrade activity expected to continue driving growth through 2026-2027. This refresh creates natural upsell opportunities for SASE and SecOps modules.

**SASE Performance:**
- Unified SASE billings grew 40% in Q4 2025
- SASE now represents 27% of total billings
- Unified SASE & SecOps billings grew 24% for FY2025
- Growing adoption across both Sovereign and Public SASE use cases

**Investment Thesis:** Fortinet is a high-quality compounder with the strongest margin profile in the industry (operating margins consistently above 25%). The FortiGate refresh cycle provides visibility into 2026-2027 growth, while SASE expansion opens a large incremental TAM. At ~$60 billion market cap (~8.5x FY2025 revenue), Fortinet offers a more reasonable valuation than PANW with strong cash generation. Key risk: SASE competition from Zscaler, Palo Alto, and Netskope, and the eventual end of the refresh cycle tailwind.

### Check Point Software (CHKP) -- The Steady Compounder

**Market Cap:** ~$19-20 billion | **TTM Revenue:** ~$2.68 billion | **Growth:** Mid-single digits YoY

Check Point is the original enterprise firewall vendor, and while it lacks the growth profile of PANW or FTNT, it remains a profitable, cash-generating business with a loyal installed base.

**Infinity Platform:** Check Point's AI-powered, cloud-delivered platform built on a hybrid mesh network architecture with SASE at its core. The Infinity platform unifies management across on-premises, cloud, and workspace environments, enabling consolidation.

**Investment Thesis:** Check Point is a value play within cybersecurity. At ~$19 billion market cap (~7x revenue), it trades at a significant discount to peers despite consistent profitability. Every Wall Street analyst covering CHKP as of early 2026 has a buy-equivalent rating, suggesting limited downside. The risk is market share erosion to PANW, FTNT, and Zscaler over time. Best suited for investors seeking cybersecurity exposure with lower volatility.

## SIEM, SOAR & Security Operations

**Market Size:** The SIEM market reached approximately $9.6 billion in 2024, growing at a 12.2% CAGR to approximately $17 billion by 2029. The market is undergoing a fundamental transformation driven by AI, cloud migration, and the emergence of security data pipelines that decouple data ingestion from analytics.

### Cisco/Splunk -- The Integration Play

Cisco completed its $28 billion acquisition of Splunk in March 2024. This was the largest cybersecurity acquisition in history at the time and transformed Cisco from a networking company with security products into a combined networking-security-observability platform.

**Integration Status:** Cisco has been integrating Splunk's AI-powered SIEM capabilities with its networking and security portfolio. Additional acquisitions including SnapAttack (late 2024) and NeuralFabric (intent to acquire, November 2025) support the move toward AI-native security. The key question is whether Cisco can retain Splunk's customer base and innovate fast enough to compete with cloud-native alternatives.

### Elastic (ESTC) -- Open-Source SIEM Disruptor

**Market Cap:** ~$7.6-7.9 billion | **FY2025 Revenue:** $1.483 billion | **FY2026 Guidance:** ~$1.72 billion (midpoint, ~16% growth)

Elastic has evolved from a search company into a credible security/SIEM vendor. The company won a landmark $26 million base-year contract with CISA to build a unified FedRAMP-certified SIEM-as-a-Service platform on Elastic Cloud, with potential extension to $130 million over five years.

**Key Metrics:**
- Elastic Cloud revenue: $688 million in FY2025, +26% YoY
- Q2 FY2026 total revenue: $423.5 million, +15.9% YoY
- Recognized as a Leader in the IDC MarketScape for XDR Software (2025)
- Strong traction in federal/government sector

**Investment Thesis:** Elastic offers a differentiated, open-source-foundation approach to SIEM that appeals to cost-conscious and technically sophisticated buyers. The CISA contract provides a reference account for federal expansion. At ~$7.7 billion market cap (~4.5x FY2026 revenue), ESTC trades at a reasonable multiple for its growth rate. Risks include competition from Microsoft Sentinel and Google Chronicle, which bundle SIEM with their broader cloud platforms.

### Exabeam/LogRhythm -- The Merged Challenger

Exabeam and LogRhythm completed their merger in July 2024, creating the largest independent SIEM provider under the Exabeam name. The combined company standardized on Exabeam's New-Scale cloud SIEM platform and retired LogRhythm's competing Axon offering. Operating under Thoma Bravo ownership (private), the company focuses on AI-driven SIEM and user/entity behavior analytics (UEBA). This is relevant primarily as a competitive dynamic -- Exabeam provides a pure-play SIEM alternative for organizations unwilling to commit to PANW, CRWD, or hyperscaler SIEM offerings.

### Microsoft Sentinel & Google Chronicle/SecOps

The hyperscaler SIEM offerings are increasingly competitive threats to standalone SIEM vendors:

**Microsoft Sentinel** benefits from deep integration with the Microsoft 365 and Azure ecosystem. Microsoft is now bundling Security Copilot with all Microsoft 365 E5 customers, dramatically expanding its security footprint. Sentinel can query archived logs in Azure storage, reducing data ingestion costs. SOC analysts using the Phishing Triage Agent detect malicious emails up to 550% faster. Identity admins achieve up to 204% greater accuracy identifying missing Zero Trust policies.

**Google Chronicle** (now Google SecOps) is recognized as a Leader in Gartner's Magic Quadrant for SIEM, with AI as a core competency. Google's $32 billion acquisition of Wiz (expected to close 2026) will dramatically strengthen its security platform and cloud-native detection capabilities.

**Impact:** The hyperscaler bundling strategy puts enormous pressure on standalone SIEM vendors. Organizations already invested in Microsoft or Google Cloud face strong pull toward these integrated offerings, potentially compressing margins and growth for pure-play SIEM vendors.

## AI-Powered Threat Detection & SOC Automation

AI is simultaneously the most significant growth driver and the greatest risk in cybersecurity. Every major vendor is integrating AI capabilities, while attackers are using AI to generate more sophisticated, faster, and harder-to-detect threats.

### Defender-Side AI

**LLM-Powered Security Copilots:**
- **CrowdStrike Charlotte AI:** Agentic AI handling alert triage, incident response, and threat hunting. Evolving from a chatbot to autonomous SOC operations
- **Microsoft Security Copilot:** Bundled with M365 E5, achieving 550% faster phishing detection. Agentic AI agents for phishing triage, conditional access optimization, and vulnerability remediation
- **Palo Alto XSIAM:** AI-native SOC platform with ~400 customers averaging $1M+ ARR each. 60%+ achieving MTTR under 10 minutes
- **SentinelOne Purple AI:** Triple-digit growth, 30%+ attach rates, FedRAMP-High authorized. Athena release brings deep security reasoning and hyperautomation

**Autonomous Response:** In 2026, more companies are deploying AI assistants capable of autonomous containment -- isolating compromised endpoints or disabling suspected malicious accounts within seconds without human approval. This represents a paradigm shift from human-in-the-loop to human-on-the-loop security operations.

### Attacker-Side AI

AI-powered attacks are accelerating:
- Deepfake fraud is now a material enterprise risk. The Arup incident (September 2025) saw $25 million stolen via a video call populated entirely by AI-generated deepfakes of the CFO and financial controller
- Generative AI has achieved near-flawless real-time replication, making deepfakes indistinguishable from reality
- Autonomous AI agents introduce cascading failure risks -- research shows a single compromised agent can poison 87% of downstream decision-making within 4 hours
- AI-powered phishing, social engineering, and malware generation are increasing attack volume and sophistication

### Investment Implications

The AI arms race in security creates a structural tailwind for vendors with proprietary data advantages (CrowdStrike's telemetry from millions of endpoints, Palo Alto's network traffic data). Vendors that can demonstrate measurable SOC productivity improvements (faster MTTR, reduced analyst burden) will command premium pricing. The risk is that AI becomes table stakes and fails to differentiate, but early evidence (XSIAM ARR, Charlotte AI engagement, Purple AI attach rates) suggests meaningful monetization is underway.

## Managed Detection & Response (MDR)

**Market Size:** The global MDR market is projected to reach $8.4-11.8 billion by 2029-2030, growing at a 21-24% CAGR from 2025. The related SOC-as-a-Service market is projected to reach $14.7 billion by 2030 at a 12.2% CAGR.

MDR is the fastest-growing segment within enterprise security, driven by the acute shortage of security talent (estimated 3.5+ million unfilled cybersecurity positions globally) and the increasing complexity of managing multi-vendor security stacks. MDR providers offer outsourced 24/7 monitoring, detection, and response capabilities, effectively providing a SOC as a service.

### Key Players

**Sophos/Secureworks (Private):** Sophos completed its $859 million acquisition of Secureworks (from Dell Technologies) in February 2025. The combined entity, operating under Thoma Bravo ownership, is now the largest pure-play MDR provider supporting over 28,000 organizations. Secureworks' common stock has ceased trading on Nasdaq.

**Arctic Wolf (Private):** Valued at $4.4 billion (though secondary market data suggests below $4 billion as of Q1 2025), Arctic Wolf is the largest independent MDR specialist with over $400 million in ARR. The company was the first cloud-native MDR vendor to exceed $1 billion valuation. An IPO has been discussed but no filing has been made; CEO Nick Schneider has cited waiting for improved investor appetite.

**eSentire (Private):** Rated a Strong Performer in the Forrester Wave Q1 2025 and recognized as one of only two Leaders in the Forrester MDR Wave for Europe Q3 2025. A strong mid-market competitor.

**CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, Palo Alto Networks:** All three major platform vendors have built or acquired MDR capabilities, increasingly competing with pure-play MDR providers. This dual pressure (platform vendors moving downstream, MDR vendors moving upstream) creates consolidation risk for standalone MDR players.

### Investment Implications

Most major MDR players are private. For public market investors, MDR growth accrues primarily to the platform vendors (CRWD, PANW, S) that offer MDR as part of their platform bundle. Arctic Wolf remains the most significant IPO candidate in the space, though timing is uncertain. Zscaler's acquisition of Red Canary for ~$675 million validates MDR as a strategic capability worth acquiring.

## Vulnerability Management

**Market Size:** The vulnerability management market is estimated at $16-18 billion in 2025, growing at a 6.5-8% CAGR to $24 billion by 2030. The top 5 vendors (Tenable, Qualys, Rapid7, Synopsys, IBM Security) collectively hold 56% market share.

### Qualys (QLYS) -- The Profitable Specialist

**Market Cap:** ~$4.6 billion | **FY2025 Revenue:** ~$670 million | **FY2026 Guidance:** $717-725 million (+7-8% YoY)

Qualys is the most profitable company in the vulnerability management space, consistently generating 40%+ operating margins. The cloud-native platform provides vulnerability management, compliance, and web application scanning.

**Key Metrics:**
- Q4 2025 revenue: $175.3 million
- Non-GAAP EPS: $1.87 (Q4), FY2026 guidance of $7.17-$7.45 (raised vs consensus ~$6.59)
- $200 million share repurchase increase
- P/E ratio: 24.77, Beta: 0.53

**Investment Thesis:** Qualys is a high-margin, low-growth cash cow. The 7-8% revenue growth rate limits upside, but the company's profitability and capital return program provide downside protection. At ~$4.6 billion market cap (~6.5x FY2026 revenue), valuation is reasonable for the quality of earnings. Key risk: market share loss to Tenable's more aggressive expansion and CrowdStrike/PANW entering the exposure management space.

### Tenable (TENB) -- The Growth-Oriented Incumbent

**Market Cap:** ~$2.7 billion | **FY2025 Revenue:** $999.4 million (+11% YoY) | **FY2026 Guidance:** $1.065-1.075 billion

Tenable remains independent and publicly traded (despite earlier speculation about potential acquisition). The company is the broadest exposure management platform, recently acquiring Vulcan Cyber for $147 million (February 2025) to add AI-powered risk prioritization and automated remediation.

**Investment Thesis:** At ~$2.7 billion market cap (~2.5x FY2026 revenue), Tenable is the cheapest of the vulnerability management vendors. The company is crossing the $1 billion revenue milestone and has clear catalysts in AI security demand and platform adoption. However, slower growth relative to endpoint/XDR peers and potential M&A target risk (both upside and downside) are considerations.

### Rapid7 (RPD) -- The Turnaround Candidate

**Market Cap:** ~$1.0-1.2 billion | **TTM Revenue:** ~$859 million (+3% YoY) | **TTM ARR:** ~$838 million (+2% YoY)

Rapid7 is in turnaround mode. Growth has decelerated sharply to low-single digits, and the company is restructuring its cost base (including a new operations center in India) to set the stage for profitability reacceleration in 2026.

**Investment Thesis:** Rapid7 is the deep-value play in the vulnerability management space. At ~$1 billion market cap (~1.2x TTM revenue), the stock is extremely cheap if the turnaround succeeds. InsightVM 6.0 (released January 2026) introduced improved integration, enhanced risk reduction, expanded cloud support, and NIST CSF 2.0 compliance. However, the risk of continued market share loss to Tenable, CrowdStrike, and PANW is significant. Best suited for contrarian investors with high conviction in the turnaround thesis.

## Market Consolidation & Platform vs. Best-of-Breed

### The Platformization Trend

The cybersecurity industry is undergoing a generational shift from best-of-breed point products to integrated platforms. A 2025 Gartner survey found that 62% of companies are actively consolidating security vendors, with another 36% planning to do so within three years. By 2028, 45% of organizations are projected to use fewer than 15 cybersecurity tools, up from just 13% in 2023.

**Key Drivers:**
- Centralized data streams reduce MTTD and MTTR
- Total cost of ownership savings from reduced vendor management
- AI effectiveness improves with unified data lakes (more telemetry = better models)
- Regulatory pressure (SEC 4-day disclosure rule requires faster detection and response)

**Key Risks:**
- Consolidation can create the illusion of integration without genuine architectural unity
- Lock-in risk for customers
- Platform vendors often lack best-in-class depth in any single category
- Dual-vendor strategies remain common in practice

### Investment Implications

Platform vendors (PANW, CRWD) are the primary beneficiaries of the consolidation trend. Best-of-breed specialists (QLYS, TENB, RPD, ESTC) face either acquisition or market share pressure. The survivor pool is narrowing. Investors should position in platform leaders and/or specialists with strong enough differentiation to avoid displacement (e.g., QLYS's margin profile, ESTC's open-source moat in government).

## Key Macro Trends

### SEC Cybersecurity Disclosure Rules

The SEC's rules (effective December 2023) require public companies to disclose material cybersecurity incidents within four days of materiality determination. This has created structural demand for faster detection and response capabilities, benefiting SIEM/XDR vendors and MDR providers. The rules also require annual disclosure of cybersecurity risk management practices, driving board-level investment in security programs. Insurers are now scrutinizing these disclosures, creating additional pressure to maintain robust cybersecurity postures.

### Zero Trust Adoption

Zero trust has moved from concept to implementation. Approximately 63% of organizations have partially or fully implemented zero trust, with 81% planning to implement within 12 months. However, only 10% of large enterprises are projected to have a fully mature zero trust program by 2026 (up from less than 1% in 2023). The zero trust architecture market is estimated at $38.4 billion in 2025, growing at 17.7% CAGR to $86.6 billion by 2030.

Key challenge: 48% of organizations cite cost and resource constraints as the primary barrier, while 22% report internal team resistance. This adoption gap creates a multi-year tailwind for security vendors offering zero trust solutions as part of their platforms.

### Cyber Insurance as a Demand Driver

Cyber insurance underwriting requirements are increasingly dictating minimum security standards. Organizations seeking coverage must demonstrate specific security controls (EDR, MFA, backup practices, incident response plans), effectively mandating the purchase of enterprise security products. The SEC disclosure rules further tighten this loop: inadequate security practices disclosed publicly create both D&O liability exposure and potential insurance coverage denials, creating powerful incentives to invest in best-in-class security tooling.

## Risk Factors

1. **Valuation Risk:** CRWD and PANW trade at premium multiples that require sustained execution. Any growth deceleration would compress multiples significantly
2. **AI Commoditization:** If AI-powered security features become table stakes, the pricing premium for AI copilots (Charlotte AI, Purple AI, Security Copilot) may erode
3. **Hyperscaler Competition:** Microsoft Sentinel and Google Chronicle/SecOps benefit from deep integration with cloud platforms and bundling leverage that pure-play vendors cannot match
4. **Integration Risk:** PANW's aggressive M&A (CyberArk, Chronosphere, Protect AI) creates significant integration complexity and potential culture clash
5. **Macro Sensitivity:** While cybersecurity spending is generally resilient in downturns, budget scrutiny can delay deals and compress growth
6. **Regulatory/Geopolitical:** U.S.-China tensions, export controls, and differing international regulatory frameworks create operational complexity for global vendors
7. **Talent Shortage:** The 3.5M+ unfilled cybersecurity positions creates execution risk for all vendors, though it also drives MDR and AI adoption
8. **AI Attack Escalation:** If AI-powered attacks outpace defensive capabilities, customer trust in existing security tools could erode, creating market share volatility
