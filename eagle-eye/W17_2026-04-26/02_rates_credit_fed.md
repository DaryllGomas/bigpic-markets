# Eagle Eye W17 (2026-04-26) — Rates, Credit & Fed

*Snapshot as of Sunday, April 26, 2026 — covers trading week ending Friday April 24, 2026, plus weekend developments through this morning. FOMC blackout in effect (Apr 18 – Apr 30); Apr 28-29 FOMC meeting decision Wednesday at 2:00 PM ET.*

## Treasury Yields

**Friday April 24, 2026 closing levels (US Treasuries):**

| Tenor | 4/24 Close | 4/17 Close | Weekly Change |
|-------|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| 2-Year | 3.78% | 3.70% | +8 bps |
| 5-Year | ~3.95% (est.) | ~3.85% | ~+10 bps |
| 10-Year | 4.31% | 4.244% | +~7 bps |
| 30-Year | 4.91% | ~4.83% | ~+8 bps |

The week was dominated by **risk-on/risk-off whiplash around the Iran–Strait of Hormuz crisis**. Yields drifted higher mid-week (Wed/Thu) as oil pushed back above $105/bbl and U.S. naval enforcement extended beyond the Strait, reigniting inflation fears. The 10-year hit ~4.325% on Thursday before retracing modestly into Friday's close.

**Friday afternoon drama:** The DOJ formally dropped its criminal probe of Chair Powell. Front-end yields fell sharply on the news — the 2Y dropped 5 bps in the afternoon session — as markets reassessed the path to Kevin Warsh's confirmation as the next Fed Chair (he is perceived as materially more dovish than Powell).

For context, the 10Y was at ~3.97% in late February 2026 before the Iran war broke out — yields are roughly 30+ bps higher since the conflict began, almost entirely an inflation-risk-premium story rather than a real-yield move.

| 3m10y | +64 bps | Comfortably positive after the late-Feb brief reinversion |

**Curve narrative.** The 2s10s sits at +53 bps — roughly the steepest level since the curve emerged from its 2022-2024 inversion. Two forces are playing tug-of-war:

1. **Steepening force (long end up):** Iran/Hormuz oil shock, sticky 3.3% headline CPI, weak Treasury auctions reflecting growing US debt burden, and rising term premium.
2. **Bull-flattening force (front end down):** Friday's DOJ news raised odds of a Warsh-led, more-dovish Fed. Front-end fed funds futures repriced lower.

Net effect Friday: front-end rallied harder than the long end, creating mild bull-steepening that capped what would otherwise have been a more aggressive bear-steepening week.

## Fed Watch

### April 28-29 FOMC Meeting (Wednesday)

**Pricing (CME FedWatch as of April 24 close):**
- Hold at 3.50–3.75%: **97.9%** probability
- Cut 25 bps: ~2%
- Hike: nil

Polymarket implied 99.7% no-change. The decision is functionally already priced in — **the market focus will be entirely on the statement language and Powell's press conference**, particularly given the Iran-driven inflation impulse and the Powell/Warsh succession backdrop.

**End-2026 pricing (April 24 close):**
- 78.2% probability the target range stays at 3.50–3.75% through December
- 15.4% probability of one 25 bps cut by year-end
- After the DOJ news Friday, **fed funds futures repriced from 23% to 38% odds of a cut by year-end** — a meaningful intraday shift driven by the Warsh succession path
- September meeting: ~48% cut probability ("genuinely no idea" territory)

### Fed Speakers — Pre-Blackout (Apr 14-17)

The blackout window started **Saturday April 18 at 12:00 AM ET**, so all FOMC participant comments from this past week (Apr 21-25) are off-limits. The most consequential pre-blackout speech:

**Governor Christopher Waller, April 17, 2026** (NABE Economic Policy Conference):
- **Tone: Decidedly hawkish given the Iran shock.** Underlying ex-tariff inflation was running near 2% through February, but March headline CPI hit 3.3% and core 2.6%, with PCE risk rising toward 3.5%.
- On Q1 GDP: ~2.4% annualized, "modest" growth, with data center capex offsetting consumer softness.
- On labor: Net immigration collapsed from 2.3M (2024) to near-zero, structurally lowering breakeven payroll growth to ~0/month. Recent NFP volatility (+178k to -133k swings) reflects this regime change rather than cyclical weakness.
- **Key quote framework:** "If Hormuz reopens, I would be cautious about rate cuts now... if conflict persists, maintaining the policy rate at the current target range may be necessary if inflation risks outweigh labor market concerns." A "sequence of shocks" warning invoked the 2021-2022 unanchoring playbook.
- Translation: **No cut imminent in his view; rate hikes not explicitly ruled out under prolonged conflict.**

Vice Chair Jefferson (April 7) and Powell himself (Phoenix appearance pre-blackout) struck somewhat more balanced tones, but Waller's hawkish framing carries weight given his past dovish lean and his reputation as a leading indicator on dot-plot shifts.

### FOMC Minutes (March 17-18 meeting) — Released Apr 8

Confirmed the 10-2 hold vote; "patience" language; balance sheet runoff officially halted on the Treasury side (since Dec 1, 2025), MBS runoff continues at ~$35B/mo.

### Powell Succession Backdrop — THE Major Story

- DOJ (US Attorney Pirro, DC) **dropped the criminal probe** into Powell on Friday April 24 — the probe centered on Fed HQ renovation cost overruns. The matter has been referred to the Fed's Inspector General.
- Sen. Thom Tillis (R-NC) had been blocking Warsh's confirmation pending end of the probe; **on Sunday April 26 (today) Tillis announced he is dropping his hold**, effectively clearing the Senate path for Warsh.
- **Warsh is perceived as materially more dovish than Powell** in the current setup — he prefers trimmed-mean and median inflation measures (which read lower than Powell's preferred core PCE) and has publicly argued rates should be lower.
- Market read was unambiguous Friday: **dollar -0.28% (DXY 98.55), 2Y -5 bps, year-end cut odds 23% → 38%, gold higher.**
- Powell's term as Chair runs to May 2026; Warsh confirmation timing now hinges on Senate scheduling, with the **April 29 FOMC meeting almost certainly Powell's penultimate or final** as Chair.

- BBB: ~100 bps | AA: ~51 bps | AAA: ~40 bps
- All-in IG yield: 5.00–5.50% — most attractive carry in over a decade.
- **Weekly change: ~flat to marginally tighter** — IG essentially shrugged off the Iran headlines despite the rate volatility, reflecting strong corporate balance sheets and yield-buyer demand.

### High Yield
- **ICE BofA US HY OAS: ~285-328 bps** range, with the most recent monthly print at ~328 bps and YTW ~7.44%.
- US HY widened approximately **+16 bps over the past month** on the Iran shock — but US HY meaningfully outperformed European HY (which blew out from 287 → 349 bps) due to US energy independence.
- Energy-heavy issuers benefited from oil at $100+; longer-duration HY suffered from the Treasury sell-off.

### Treasury Auction Results (April 2026)
The week saw **three weak auctions** that exacerbated the back-up in yields:
- **2-Year ($69B):** Particularly weak, characterized as "going poorly."
- **5-Year ($70B):** Bid-to-cover 2.29 (vs. 2.36 avg); primary dealer absorption 16% (vs. 11% avg); **tail of 1.4 bps** (vs. 0.3 avg) — a notable concession.
- **7-Year ($44B):** Tail 0.8 bps (vs. 0.3 avg) — also weak but improving from the 5Y.

CRFB and other watchers flagged the soft auctions as confirming "risks of our growing debt burden." With deficit-to-GDP at 5.9% (FY25) and Bessent targeting "3% by end of administration," term premium pressure is structural.

- **SLOOS:** April 2026 release not yet out as of this writing (typically released around the FOMC meeting). The January 2026 SLOOS showed continued (modest) net tightening of C&I standards. Watch for the April release shortly after the Apr 29 FOMC.

## Weekend Developments (April 25-26)

This is a CRITICAL section given the 72 hours into the FOMC meeting.

1. **Tillis drops Warsh hold (Sunday Apr 26).** Senator Tillis confirmed on Sunday morning that with the DOJ probe ended, he is ready to move forward with confirming Warsh. **This effectively means Powell's successor is essentially confirmed, pending only Senate floor scheduling.** Expect this to dominate Sunday futures opening.

2. **Strait of Hormuz on/off again.** Per Bloomberg/maritime trackers: the Strait was briefly reopened Friday-Saturday, then Iran closed it again Saturday in response to the U.S. blockade of Iranian ports. **As of April 25, only 19 vessels transited** (5 in/14 out — vs. normal baseline of 120-140/day). The two-week ceasefire expires Tuesday — directly on the eve of the FOMC decision.

3. **U.S. naval action expanding.** April 25 saw U.S. forces intercept the sanctioned LPG SEVAN in the central Arabian Sea — an extension of enforcement well beyond the Strait itself.

4. **Brent crude at ~$105/bbl** as of Saturday close, having peaked above $120 earlier in the crisis. Hormuz status will dominate Monday's open.

5. **No Fed commentary expected** — blackout runs through Thursday April 30. The next official Fed voice will be Powell at the press conference Wednesday at 2:30 PM ET.

6. **Treasury Secretary Bessent** was on the wires earlier in April with a pivot from "wait and see" (Apr 13) to "I understand if the Fed wants to wait" (Apr 14) — reflecting administration acknowledgement that the Iran shock complicates the cut narrative. No new Bessent remarks over the weekend in our search.

**Net positioning into Monday open:** The Warsh-cleared-path news is bullish bonds (especially front end) and bearish dollar; the renewed Hormuz closure is bearish bonds (inflation impulse) and bullish oil/gold. Treasuries should open with a flatter front end and continued long-end pressure. The 2Y at 3.78% has clear room lower toward 3.65-3.70% if Warsh confirmation accelerates this week.
