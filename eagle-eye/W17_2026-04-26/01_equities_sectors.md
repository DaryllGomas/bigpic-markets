## Market Overview

US equities posted a fourth consecutive weekly gain, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite hitting fresh all-time highs on Friday despite an active geopolitical backdrop (ongoing 2026 Iran war, fragile Israel-Lebanon truce). The week was characterized by a sharp rotation: AI/semiconductors ripped on a blockbuster Intel print while enterprise software cratered on IBM and ServiceNow disappointments. Earnings breadth was solid — roughly 25% of S&P 500 companies have reported Q1 2026 with ~80% beating EPS — but consumer sentiment hit a record low.

The Nasdaq-100 was the standout, lifted by an 18-day semiconductor winning streak that ran into Friday. SMH (VanEck Semiconductor ETF) closed at $506.44, +5.10% on Friday alone, +33% since March 30, and +40.67% YTD. SMH absorbed a record $3.4B of April inflows. Weekly inflows into global equity funds reached $48.72B through April 22 — the largest weekly sum since November 2024.

Oil was the other major story: Brent settled at $105.30/bbl and WTI at $94.40/bbl after a fifth straight day of gains. Brent rose ~16% on the week and WTI ~14%, both up >40% since the Iran conflict began. The IEA characterized the cumulative Strait of Hormuz disruption as the "largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market." Goldman Sachs raised its 12-month US recession probability to 30%.

## Weekend Developments

The weekend brought escalating geopolitical risk that may pressure Sunday-night futures.

- **Iran peace talks collapse on Saturday:** President Trump abruptly canceled the planned trip of Special Envoy Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner to Pakistan, telling Fox News "We have all the cards. They can call us anytime they want, but you're not going to be making any more 18-hour flights to sit around talking about nothing." Iranian FM Abbas Araghchi is now headed to meet Putin in Moscow on Monday — a sign Tehran is pivoting toward Russia rather than de-escalating with Washington.
- **Strait of Hormuz hardline:** Iran's Deputy Parliament Speaker Ali Nikzad said on Saturday the Strait will "under no circumstances" return to its previous state, citing an order from Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei. He framed Hormuz + Bab al-Mandab disruption as affecting "25% of the world's economy."
- **Israel-Lebanon ceasefire fraying:** Despite a US-brokered three-week extension, Israeli strikes killed at least 6 people in Lebanon on Saturday (Lebanese Health Ministry). Cumulative Lebanese deaths since March 2 stand at 2,509 with 7,755 wounded.
- **Implication for Sunday futures:** Specific Sunday-night ES/NQ levels were not yet available at the time of this report, but the talks-collapse + Hormuz rhetoric set up a likely gap-down on Monday open and renewed bid in oil/energy. Watch for risk-off rotation that mirrors last week's mid-week IBM/ServiceNow drawdown.

The Friday rally largely reflected hope of a continued ceasefire — Saturday's news fundamentally reverses that narrative.
