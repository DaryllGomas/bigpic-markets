## Headline: Iran War / Strait of Hormuz Dominates Everything

The 2026 Iran war (US-Israel air war launched Feb 28; Iranian closure of the Strait of Hormuz, US naval blockade since April 13) remains the single dominant macro driver. Approximately ~11 mb/d of crude production has been removed from global supply, Middle East Gulf seaborne exports have fallen from ~15 mb/d to an effective ~7 mb/d, and the IEA has called it "the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market." Brent has rallied ~55% since the war began, peaking near $120/bbl, with WTI up ~69% and Brent up ~75% YTD. Despite Friday's pullback on hopes for renewed Pakistan-mediated talks, oil booked another double-digit weekly gain. Critically, those Pakistan talks fell apart over the weekend (Witkoff/Kushner trip scrapped Saturday), pushing Brent back above $107 in Sunday-evening futures trading.

- WTI fell more than 1% on Friday after early-session gains evaporated on a CNN report that Trump would dispatch envoys to Pakistan for a second round of Iran talks. The day prior Brent had touched ~$107 intraday and capped a five-session winning streak.
- IEA April Oil Market Report: OPEC+ production fell 9.4 mb/d MoM to 42.4 mb/d in March, with OPEC alone collapsing 27% from 28.7 mb/d to 20.8 mb/d as Gulf Arab states cannot export through the Strait. Physical crude has reportedly traded near $150/bbl in some bilateral deals — well above futures — underscoring the physical-paper disconnect.
- The OPEC+ "Eight" had agreed in early April to begin a phased unwind of 1.65 mb/d in voluntary cuts starting April 2026, but the framework is explicitly conditional and "can be paused or reversed" — effectively moot while Hormuz remains closed.

## Weekend Developments (CRITICAL — ongoing as of Sun April 26)

### Iran Diplomacy Collapses Again
- **Saturday April 25**: Trump scrapped the planned Witkoff/Kushner Pakistan trip, said negotiations "could happen by phone" instead. Pakistan-mediated second round of US-Iran peace talks effectively dead in the water.
- **Saturday-Sunday**: Iran "re-imposed tighter control" over Strait of Hormuz within hours of reopening it. Reports of gunfire on tankers, vessels turning back. Iran's military stated it will retaliate after US Navy seized an Iranian-flagged cargo vessel.

### Sunday Futures Open (April 26 evening)
- **Brent rose >2% to a 3-week high of $107.97/bbl** in Sunday-night Asian trading.
- Equity futures: Dow -0.2% (~-130 pts), S&P 500 -0.3%, Nasdaq 100 -0.3%.
- USD likely to extend safe-haven bid into Monday Asia.
- Gold: no clean weekend OTC print available, but the geopolitical re-escalation should at minimum arrest the prior week's ~3% slide; Sunday-night reaction will be a key tell.

### Russia/Ukraine
- **April 23**: EU adopted 20th sanctions package — basis for future maritime services ban on Russian crude/products; mandatory due diligence on tanker sales targeting shadow fleet; LNG terminal services to Russian entities banned from January 2027; sanctions on Murmansk and Tuapse ports plus Karimun (Indonesia) terminal.
- Ukraine has intensified strikes on Russian ports/refineries since March 21; April Russian oil exports could fall to lowest since 2023 (-300 kb/d crude transhipments, -200 kb/d products in March).
- US OFAC has Rosneft and Lukoil designated.

### Implications for Monday Open
1. **Energy**: Brent likely opens around $107-108, WTI mid-to-high $90s; rally could extend on any further Iran provocation.
2. **Gold**: After last week's profit-take, weekend headlines are bullish; watch $4,700 reclaim.
3. **DXY**: Continued safe-haven support, especially vs. EUR (Europe most exposed to Hormuz oil).
4. **JPY**: Risk-off normally bullish JPY but BoJ next week complicates; USD/JPY likely choppy near 159-160.
5. **Equities**: Risk-off bias confirmed by Sunday futures; energy stocks the clear winner, refiners/airlines/transports the losers, broad market vulnerable to multiple compression as inflation expectations re-anchor higher.
