**Key narratives driving the week:**
- **FOMC held at 3.50-3.75%** (11-1 vote; Gov. Miran dissented in favor of a cut). The longer-run neutral rate drifted up to 3.1%.
- **Iran conflict (Day 21):** Strait of Hormuz effectively closed for 19 days, choking ~20% of global oil supply. Brent crude at ~$107/bbl. Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum to Iran to reopen the Strait or face strikes on power plants. No ceasefire negotiations underway.
- **Triple Witching + S&P Rebalance:** $5.7T in options contracts expired. S&P announced a significant index overhaul on March 6 to reflect AI infrastructure/communications sector dominance. Historically, triple witching Friday has a 25% win rate (median S&P return of -0.36%).
- **Super Micro (SMCI) smuggling charges:** Co-founder and two associates charged with diverting $2.5B in Nvidia AI servers to China. SMCI cratered 28.4% on Friday to $22.06. Broader AI supply chain integrity concerns weighed on the Nasdaq.
