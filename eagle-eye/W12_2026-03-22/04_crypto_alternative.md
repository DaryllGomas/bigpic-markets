## Regulation & Institutional

### Landmark SEC/CFTC Joint Classification (March 17, 2026)

The most significant US crypto regulatory development in years: the SEC and CFTC issued a joint 68-page interpretation classifying **16 crypto assets as "digital commodities"** — not securities:

**The Clean 16:** Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, XRP, Cardano, Chainlink, Avalanche, Polkadot, Stellar, Hedera, Litecoin, Dogecoin, Shiba Inu, Tezos, Bitcoin Cash, Aptos, and Algorand.

**Key provisions:**
- Staking, mining, and airdrops classified outside securities law
- Coherent token taxonomy established: digital commodities, digital collectibles, digital tools, stablecoins, and digital securities
- Supersedes all prior staff statements and ends the "regulation by enforcement" era
- A March 11 MOU established a Joint Harmonization Initiative between SEC and CFTC

This is a watershed moment for US crypto regulation and should provide long-term structural support for the classified assets.

### Institutional Adoption

- **Morgan Stanley:** Filing spot BTC ETF (MSBT), Ethereum and Solana trusts; planning E*Trade retail crypto trading (BTC, ETH, SOL) in H1 2026; applied for OCC National Trust Bank Charter for digital asset custody; planning proprietary digital wallet for H2 2026
- **Mastercard:** Launched Crypto Partner Program with 85+ partners including Binance, PayPal, and Ripple
- **JPMorgan:** Processing $1B+ daily on-chain through Kinexys
- **Broadridge:** Integrated Crypto.com into NYFIX order routing network for institutional brokers
- **Japan FSA:** Reclassifying crypto from payments law to Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (banks/insurers can treat crypto like stocks)
