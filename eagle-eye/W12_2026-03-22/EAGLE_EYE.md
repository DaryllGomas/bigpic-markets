## This Week's Take

The Iran war is now the dominant variable in every asset class. The Strait of Hormuz has been effectively closed for 19 days, choking ~20% of global oil supply and pushing Brent above $107/bbl (+95% in two months). On Saturday, Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum threatening to "obliterate" Iran's power plants if the Strait is not reopened — a major escalation that sets up extreme event risk at Monday's open. Iranian missiles struck Israel Saturday, both sides rejected ceasefire talks, and weekend oil futures are already pushing Brent toward $113. The Dallas Fed estimates the closure will shave 2.9 annualized percentage points off Q2 GDP. This is a stagflationary shock of a magnitude the market hasn't faced since the 1970s.

Against this backdrop, the FOMC delivered a hawkish hold on Wednesday, keeping rates at 3.50-3.75% while raising the 2026 inflation forecast to 2.7%. The dot plot is deeply split — 7 members want zero cuts, 7 want one — and the market is now pricing a 48% probability of no cuts at all in 2026, with some participants pricing rate hikes. The 2Y yield spiked 15 bps to 3.88% (highest since July 2025), the 10Y hit 4.39%, and the 30Y is flirting with 5%. J.P. Morgan has called for zero cuts in 2026 and a hike in 2027.

The equity damage is mounting: the S&P 500 closed at 6,506 (-1.5% for the week, fourth consecutive weekly decline), the Russell 2000 entered correction territory (-10.9% from ATH), and the S&P broke below its 200-day moving average. Triple Witching + the S&P rebalance on Friday amplified volatility with $5.7T in options expiring. In individual names, Micron delivered a blowout quarter (EPS $12.20 vs $9.31 est., revenue +196% YoY) validating the AI infrastructure thesis, while Super Micro cratered 28% after its co-founder was charged with smuggling $2.5B in Nvidia AI servers to China. Gold suffered a historic ~9% weekly crash to ~$4,624 as hawkish Fed rhetoric and a surging dollar overwhelmed safe-haven demand. Bitcoin slid to $68,951 over the weekend from $70,423 Friday, with whale distribution at its highest since 2015 — but a landmark SEC/CFTC ruling classifying 16 crypto assets as digital commodities provides a structural positive.

## Fed & Rates Outlook

**FOMC March 18 — Hawkish Hold at 3.50-3.75%**

The committee is deeply divided: 7 members want zero 2026 cuts, 7 want one cut. The median dot held at 3.4% year-end (one 25 bps cut), but the center of gravity shifted hawkish. Core PCE forecast raised to 2.7% (from 2.5%), reflecting the oil shock pass-through.

**Key quotes:**
- Powell: "If we don't see progress [on inflation], you won't see that rate cut."
- Powell on stagflation: "It's a very difficult situation, but it's nothing like the 1970s."
- Bowman (3/20): Penciled in 3 cuts to support labor market.
- Waller (3/20): "I just want to wait and see." Does not think hikes are needed.
- J.P. Morgan (3/19): Zero cuts in 2026, rate hike in 2027.

**Rate expectations:** Fed funds futures price ~21 bps of cuts by year-end. 48% probability of zero cuts. First cut not fully priced until December, if at all. The 2Y yield's +53 bps rise since the Iran war began is now pricing in the possibility of hikes.

**Yield curve:** 2s10s at +46-51 bps, solidly uninverted. Bear-flattening impulse as the short end reprices faster. The 30Y at 4.96% is pushing mortgage rates to 6.5%.

**Credit:** IG spreads widened to ~85-90 bps (from ~78 bps at Q4 2025 close). HY OAS at ~298-320 bps, widening from ~280 bps. Quality bifurcation underway — BB tightening, CCC widening. Pre-revenue and capital-intensive watchlist names (CRWV, QS, OKLO, SMR, RIVN, LAC) face higher cost of capital.

**Liquidity:** ON RRP at zero. TGA volatile ($800-940B). QT ended Dec 2025. March 31 quarter-end is the first major reserve adequacy test in the post-QT, post-RRP regime.

## Positioning & Thesis Update

**The macro regime has shifted to stagflation risk.** The Iran war / Hormuz closure is a supply shock that simultaneously raises inflation and threatens growth. The Fed is trapped between fighting inflation (which argues for holding or hiking) and supporting growth (which argues for cutting). With the committee split 7-7 and the dot plot showing only one cut, optionality has collapsed. The weekend escalation (Trump ultimatum, Israel strikes) only deepens the uncertainty.

**Actionable takeaways:**

1. **De-risk into Monday's open.** The 48-hour ultimatum creates binary event risk. Weekend Brent at $113+ signals the market is pricing escalation. The post-Triple Witch week is historically weak, and breadth is already deteriorating (only 44% of S&P above 200-day MA).

2. **Energy is the clear winner.** XLE outperformed all sectors. The thesis favors staying long energy exposure while Hormuz remains closed. Nuclear names (CEG, VST) sold off with the broader utility sector Friday on rates — this looks like a buying opportunity given structural demand is unchanged.

3. **AI infrastructure thesis strengthened.** Micron's blowout (revenue +196% YoY, HBM4 in production, Q3 guide $33.5B) is the strongest data point yet for the AI capex supercycle. The SMCI smuggling charges are a company-specific event but raise export control enforcement risk for the broader AI supply chain.

4. **Cybersecurity selloff is a potential opportunity.** The Anthropic Claude Security AI scare drove CRWD -11% and hit the group. Analyst consensus calls this an overreaction — AI tools complement rather than replace cybersecurity platforms. The Iran conflict should actually accelerate cyber spending.

5. **Credit quality bifurcation matters for watchlist.** Pre-revenue and capital-intensive names (CRWV, QS, OKLO, SMR, RIVN, LAC) face rising cost of capital as HY spreads widen. Favor companies with contracted revenue and strong backlogs (VRT, ETN, BWXT, FLNC, SYM, RKLB).

6. **Gold's flash crash is unusual.** A ~9% weekly loss during a major geopolitical crisis is paradoxical. The dollar/rates channel dominated safe-haven demand. If the Iran situation worsens materially (Strait remains closed, military escalation), gold could reclaim $5,000+ once the forced liquidation cycle completes.

7. **Crypto regulatory tailwind.** The SEC/CFTC digital commodity classification is the most significant US regulatory clarity event for crypto. This is structurally bullish for the classified assets (BTC, ETH, SOL, XRP, etc.) but weekend prices reflect the broader risk-off. BTC at $69K with Extreme Fear (15) and institutional accumulation is a divergence worth monitoring.

8. **Watch March 31 quarter-end.** With ON RRP at zero and bank reserves potentially tight, the quarter-end could produce funding market stress. This is the first major test of reserve adequacy in the post-QT regime.
