# Commodities & Forex — Week Ending May 8, 2026

*Agent 3 / Eagle Eye W19 | Snapshot as of Saturday, May 10, 2026*

> **Macro overdrive:** Every commodity and FX market this week was distorted by the ongoing **2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis**. The strait has been effectively closed since Feb 28, 2026 (US-Israel air war on Iran, killing of Supreme Leader Khamenei). The IEA calls this "the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market." **UAE formally exited OPEC/OPEC+ on May 1, 2026** mid-week. Iran-US one-page ceasefire/nuclear memo negotiations whipsawed prices daily. Both WTI and Brent printed weekly losses of >6% from peace-deal hopes, but remain structurally elevated due to the supply shock. Gold and silver continued ripping; USD/DXY broke under 98 to a 10-week low even with strong NFP.

- **Weekly arc:** Brent printed a 2026-high early in the week (post-OPEC+ output bump and "Project Freedom" launch to reopen Hormuz), then plunged on Tuesday-Thursday on reports the US-Iran are closing in on a **one-page memo** to end the war (Axios). Brent fell to ~$100.06 Thursday before bouncing Friday. WTI traded a similar pattern, ~$95 close [Fortune](https://fortune.com/article/price-of-oil-05-08-2026/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/08/oil-prices-today-wti-brent-us-iran-fire-war-hormuz-ceasefire.html).
- **Strait of Hormuz status:** Effectively closed since Feb 28. Tanker traffic down ~70%; ~20,000 mariners and ~2,000 ships stranded in the Persian Gulf per IMO. Iran established the "Persian Gulf Strait Authority" on May 5 to regulate transit [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis), [House of Commons Library](https://commonslibrary.parliament.uk/research-briefings/cbp-10636/).
- **Tanker incidents this week:** US fired on/disabled two Iranian tankers attempting to evade the US naval blockade [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/08/g-s1-121061/iran-war-updates). On May 5, CMA CGM San Antonio reportedly struck by cruise missile in the strait (8 crew injured). On May 8, Iran seized the **Ocean Koi** tanker. The UAE air defenses engaged two Iranian ballistic missiles and three drones Friday [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/08/oil-prices-today-wti-brent-us-iran-fire-war-hormuz-ceasefire.html).
- **OPEC+ May 3 meeting:** Seven remaining OPEC+ countries (Saudi, Russia, Iraq, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman) agreed to a **188,000 bpd output increase for June** — first meeting without the UAE [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/03/opec-announces-188000-barrels-per-day-output-increase-.html), [Interfax](https://interfax.com/newsroom/top-stories/117396/). Saudi/Russia each +62kbpd; Iraq +26; Kuwait +16; Algeria +6.
- **UAE OPEC exit (Apr 28 announcement, effective May 1):** UAE was OPEC's #3 producer. Cites need for "freedom of action" to hit 5 mb/d capacity by 2027 and frustration over Iranian attacks on its territory/shipping [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/28/uae-leaves-opec-and-opec), [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/04/28/uae-opec-iran-hormuz-trump-saudi/), [WAM](https://www.wam.ae/en/article/bzxzuh7-uae-announces-decision-exit-opec-opec+).
- **EIA inventories:** Latest report (week ending May 1, released May 6) showed US crude stocks at **461.6 million bbls**, ~0.1% above the 5-year average. Next report May 13 [EIA WPSR](https://www.eia.gov/petroleum/supply/weekly/).
- **Strategic Petroleum Reserve:** ~**392.7 million barrels** as of week ending May 1, down from 415M in March prior to the IEA-coordinated 400M-barrel release [EIA SPR](https://www.eia.gov/dnav/pet/hist/LeafHandler.ashx?n=PET&s=WCSSTUS1&f=W), [DOE](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-initiates-strategic-petroleum-reserve-emergency-exchange-stabilize).

## Weekend Developments (May 9-10, 2026)

**This is the dominant story going into Monday — peace-deal odds are the single biggest commodity macro variable right now.**

1. **Trump on Iran enriched uranium (Sun May 10, Full Measure interview):** Trump warned the US will "target any Iranian trying to reach the country's highly enriched uranium," saying nuclear material is under constant US military surveillance. He downplayed urgency on the buried uranium and said there's a **"very good chance"** of a deal — but admitted he's felt that confidence before [Al Jazeera May 10](https://www.aljazeera.com/amp/news/2026/5/10/trump-says-us-will-not-allow-iran-to-reach-enriched-uranium), [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/u-s-and-iran-exchange-new-proposals-to-end-war).

2. **One-page memo framework (Axios scoop, May 6, still active over weekend):** US and Iran "closing in on" a one-page memo to end the war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Sticking points: 20-year US ask on Iranian enrichment freeze vs. Iran's 5+5 counter; transfer of HEU out of Iran [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/06/iran-us-deal-one-page-memo).

3. **Iran's response to ceasefire proposal (May 8):** Iran's Mohsen Rezaei (Expediency Council) rebuffed parts of the proposal, demanding US "reparations" for damage to Iran — pushed Friday oil bid higher [Al Jazeera May 8](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/5/8/what-we-know-about-irans-response-to-the-latest-us-ceasefire-proposal).

4. **Project Freedom paused (May 6) — still paused:** Trump suspended the US Navy operation to forcibly reopen the strait, citing "great progress" toward a deal [Fox News live](https://www.foxnews.com/live-news/iran-war-news-trump-strait-hormuz-oil-prices-project-freedom-may-6).

5. **Trump-Xi summit scheduled May 14-15:** Yuan/risk-asset positioning building into this. Tariff truce headlines possible [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3336453/chinas-currency-comeback-yuan-seen-strong-68-2026-breaking-key-dollar-level).

6. **No futures open weekend, but proxy signals:** Oil ETF leveraged products, Bitcoin (some correlation to oil-shock dynamics), and overnight Asian session Sunday will price the weekend deal-flow. Watch the **Sunday CME crude open (6pm ET)** — gap risk is enormous in either direction:
   - **Deal headline scenario:** Brent could gap −$5 to −$10 on Hormuz-reopening optimism.
   - **Deal collapses / Iran rebuff hardens:** Brent could gap **+$5-$15** as Project Freedom restarts and tanker incidents escalate.

7. **OPEC+ post-mortem from May 3 meeting:** 188 kbpd June increase modest given the supply gap; the cartel is signaling discipline rather than rushing to fill the void. UAE's exit means OPEC+ is now effectively a Saudi-Russia coordination mechanism with marginal members.

8. **UAE additional missile/drone attacks Friday (continued through weekend?):** UAE air defense engaged 2 ballistic missiles + 3 drones from Iran on May 8 — the second time that week. No fresh weekend reports of attacks, but the live escalation risk persists.
