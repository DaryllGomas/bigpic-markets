*Compiled Saturday, May 10, 2026 | Agent 2 | Eagle Eye Weekly Macro*

## Headline Takeaway

The week ending Friday May 8, 2026 was defined by a building leadership transition at the Fed: **Chair Powell's term expires Thursday May 15, the Senate Banking Committee advanced Kevin Warsh's nomination 13-11 on a party-line vote (April 29), and the full Senate is teed up to vote the week of May 11 — with cloture/floor action signaled for Monday-Tuesday May 11-12.** Powell announced he will stay on the Board of Governors after his chairmanship ends — a strategic move that denies Trump a fourth governor seat and a working majority. Treasury yields finished the week modestly lower (10Y at 4.36-4.38%, -3 to -4 bps Fri), with the 2s10s curve holding a positive ~48 bp slope and the 3m10y at +69 bps. Credit spreads remain near 25-year tights (IG OAS ~71 bps; HY OAS ~279-290 bps). Powell on April 29 said Fed independence is "at risk" and "we've had to fight for it."

## Fed Watch

### Most Recent FOMC: April 28-29, 2026
- **Decision:** Held federal funds target at **3.50%-3.75%** for the third consecutive meeting
- **Dissents:** Three hawkish members dissented on forward-guidance language pushing back on the "easing bias"
- **SEP:** Showed a single 25 bp cut in 2026, expected after June (i.e., earliest July 29, more likely September 16)
- **Minutes release:** Scheduled for ~May 20, 2026 (three weeks post-meeting)
- **Powell presser language:** Cited persistent inflation risks tied to March CPI at 3.3% (highest since May 2024) and energy from the Iran conflict — read as a "hawkish hold"

### Fed Speakers — Week of May 4-8

- **John Williams (NY Fed) — May 4, "There Is No Try", Cynosure Group Spring Symposium, NYC.** Base case: inflation ~3% in 2026, declining to 2% in 2027 as tariff/energy effects fade. GDP growth 2-2.25% in 2026/2027. Unemployment stays 4.25-4.5%. Supports holding at 3.50-3.75% given "elevated inflation, mixed labor signals, heightened Middle East uncertainty." **Lean: neutral-to-hawkish hold.**
- **Mary Daly (SF Fed), Christopher Waller (Gov), Michelle Bowman (Vice Chair Supervision), Austan Goolsbee (Chicago Fed)** — appeared on a panel at the **Hoover Institution Monetary Policy Conference 2026** (May 8-9 timeframe). Waller delivered the keynote-adjacent remarks at Hoover. **Lean:** Waller previously (April 17 speech) flagged willingness to cut if disinflation resumes — closest to a dove on the Board.

### Fed Chair Nomination — Kevin Warsh

This is the single biggest near-term Fed risk catalyst.

- **April 21:** Senate Banking Committee confirmation hearing. Warsh said Trump never demanded rate cuts; defended his finances.
- **April 29:** Committee voted **13-11 along party lines** — first fully partisan Fed chair committee vote in history.
- **May 11-15 window:** Full Senate floor vote expected — calendar notes show **Tuesday May 12** as the most likely date. Republicans hold a 53-seat majority; only simple majority needed. Sen. John Fetterman (D-PA) has indicated he will vote yes — likely the lone Democratic crossover.
- **May 15:** Powell's chairmanship expires. Warsh is widely expected to be sworn in around this date.
- **Powell stays on Board:** April 29 announcement — Powell will remain as Governor (term as governor runs to January 2028). Says Trump's "unprecedented" legal attacks "left me no choice." Strategic effect: denies Trump a 4th Fed governor seat / working board majority.
- **Market read:** Warsh is more hawkish on the dollar / Fed credibility but has signaled openness to faster cuts if data permits. Net: ambiguous; the *Fed-independence* narrative is the bigger price driver than Warsh's policy preferences.

**Weekly change:** Spreads were broadly stable, with no notable widening despite the Iran/Hormuz headlines and Fed-leadership uncertainty. ETF flows: HYG yielding above 6%, LQD ~5.5%.

**Risks flagged in research:**
- 2026 issuance projected at ~$2.25T (+35% YoY), much of it AI infrastructure capex — could finally tip technicals
- Historically, a move from current tight levels back to 500 bps coincides with HYG drawdowns of 5%+
- Tight spreads leave little cushion if defaults rise or recession risk reprices

From the 9 sector thesis files, rate-sensitive watchlist names to monitor:

- **Critical minerals (MP, FCX, ALB, LAC):** Higher long rates pressure development-stage names (LAC, USAR). FCX/SCCO carry copper-deficit thesis through cycle.
- **Cybersecurity (PANW, CRWD, NET, ZS):** Premium multiples (15-36x revenue) most sensitive to long-rate moves; 10Y at 4.36% is digestible but not friendly.
- **Energy storage / EV (TSLA, FLNC, EOSE):** ITC/IRA economics intact; rate cuts would help, but credit availability (tight HY) is supportive.
- **Nuclear (CEG, VST, TLN, CCJ, LEU):** Fleet operators benefit from rate normalization through utility comps; pre-revenue (OKLO, SMR) more rate-sensitive.
- **Quantum (IONQ, IBM, NVDA):** Pure-plays trade at 100-2,700x revenue — extreme rate sensitivity. Quantinuum IPO pricing (S-1 filed Jan 2026) most exposed to risk-off / rate volatility.
- **Robotics (ISRG, SYK, SYM, TSLA):** Quality margin compounders relatively rate-insensitive; humanoid pre-revenue names (TSLA Optimus thesis) carry duration risk.
- **Space (PL, LUNR, RKLB, LMT):** Defense primes insulated; pre-revenue SPACs (BKSY, SATL) carry rate beta. SpaceX IPO pricing in mid-2026 will be heavily rate-influenced.
