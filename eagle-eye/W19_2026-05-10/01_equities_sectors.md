## Market Overview

The week of May 4–8 extended the U.S. equity rally to **six consecutive weekly gains** — the longest winning streak since 2024. The Nasdaq Composite and S&P 500 each set fresh all-time highs as a stronger-than-expected April jobs report (+115K vs. +65K expected), blowout earnings from AMD and Palantir, and renewed optimism on an Iran ceasefire framework overshadowed continued military exchanges in the Strait of Hormuz.

| Index | Friday Close (May 8) | Weekly Change | YTD Change |
|-------|---------------------|---------------|------------|
| S&P 500 (SPX) | 7,398.93 | **+2.3%** | ~+7.6% (SPY total return ~+8.5%) |
| Nasdaq Composite | 26,247.08 | **+4.5%** | ~+11.2% |
| Dow Jones (DJIA) | 49,609.16 | ~flat (+0.02% Fri) | ~+1.8% |
| Russell 2000 (RUT) | ~2,890 (record on May 6: 2,886.77) | ~+1.5–2% | **+16.3%** |

The S&P 500 advanced 0.84% Friday to a record close; Nasdaq surged 1.71% Friday on broad chip strength. **Small caps lead 2026 YTD** at +16.3% vs. SPX +7.6% — a notable rotation regime as capital tilts toward domestic-cyclical and regional-bank exposure tied to lower yields and easing tariff/conflict pressure.

**Narrative drivers:**
1. **Jobs surprise** — Nonfarm payrolls +115K (vs. +65K consensus); unemployment steady at 4.3%. Eases recession concerns despite the active Iran war.
2. **AI capex thesis re-validated** — AMD reported Data Center revenue +57% to $5.8B with Q2 guide $11.2B (vs. $10.52B consensus). AMD crossed **$700B market cap**, joining the Mag-7 plus NVDA elite tier.
3. **Iran ceasefire optimism + tail risk** — Most of the week's gains came in a single session on reports of progress on the U.S.–Iran ceasefire framework (delivered via Pakistan). Counterweight: U.S. disabled two Iranian tankers (Sea Star III, Sevda) Friday and Iran fired missiles at UAE earlier in the week.
4. **Mega-cap tech leadership** — Nvidia hit an intraday all-time high of $217.80 Friday before closing $215.20, +4.8% on the week. Micron and SanDisk surged sharply on memory pricing.

3. **Russia–Ukraine ceasefire signals.** Putin Saturday said war "coming to an end"; Trump announced a 3-day ceasefire (Sat–Mon) to mark Soviet WWII Victory Day. Potential constructive headline risk for Monday open.

4. **Risk for Monday futures:** With Iran's rejection late Sunday and continued Hormuz friction (tanker incidents, UAE attacks), expect modest gap-down risk in oil-sensitive cyclicals and a bid in defense + energy if the rhetoric escalates further before the U.S. open. Sunday-night futures levels not yet available at time of writing.
