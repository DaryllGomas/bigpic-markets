The holiday-shortened week ended with a rotation-driven tape: the **Dow printed a fresh all-time high** on Thursday July 2 while **mega-cap tech and small-caps sold off** into the long weekend. ## Weekend Developments (through 2026-07-05)

1. **OPEC+ approved another output hike (announced Sun July 5, 2026).** The group added **+188,000 bpd effective August 2026** — the third consecutive monthly increment (seven core members have raised quotas ~800,000 bpd April→July). One more similar increase is expected at the **August 2 meeting**, which would essentially complete the unwind of the 2023 production cuts. **Brent traded near $72/bbl as of Friday ~7/4**, down from spring peaks >$120 and back to pre-conflict (Feb 28) levels. Strait of Hormuz shipping is recovering (OPEC+ output had fallen to 33.13M bpd in May) but remains below pre-war levels. **Bias: bearish for oil/energy equities into Monday's open**; UBS flags Hormuz tanker traffic and Chinese demand recovery as the near-term swing factors.
2. **S&P 500 futures held Friday's gain** across the closed session. Prediction markets implied ~**62% odds the S&P opens higher Monday July 6**, but with low conviction. Flagged weekend risks: geopolitical escalation or an adverse US-China trade headline could gap ES futures down Sunday night.
3. **Michael Burry AI-bubble short (disclosed June 30, 2026)** continued to circulate over the weekend (Motley Fool coverage dated July 4) — a bearish sentiment overhang for Monday (see Breadth & Sentiment).
4. **Korean won began 24-hour trading** — first session noted as a market-structure item; de-minimis US equity impact.
5. No new major geopolitical shock (Iran/Middle East) was reported over the July 4–5 weekend; the late-Feb US/Israel–Iran conflict remains under an uneasy ceasefire (since April 7) and is H1 context rather than a fresh catalyst.
