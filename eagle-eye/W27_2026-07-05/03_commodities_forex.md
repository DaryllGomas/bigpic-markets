## Energy

**The dominant energy story of the week: crude kept unwinding the "Iran-war" risk premium as Strait of Hormuz shipping recovered.** Oil fell for a third straight session/week to its lowest level since Feb 27, 2026, effectively returning to "pre-war" levels.

- **WTI crude:** Settled **~$68.78/bbl on Friday July 3, 2026** (+$0.09 / +0.13% on the day). On Thursday July 2 it dropped ~2% intraday to a low near **$67.14** before recovering to ~$68.5 into the long weekend; that was the lowest since Feb 27, 2026. WTI opened July 2 at $68.11. (Source: Forbes Advisor Jul 2; fxdailyreport Jul 3; TradingEconomics.)
- **Brent crude:** ~**$70.57/bbl (Jul 2, 2026)**, down ~0.8% on the day; traded ~$71.1–71.5 earlier July 2. Versus the prior Friday's ~$73.7 area (Jun 26), Brent was down roughly **~4% on the week**. (Source: Forbes Advisor; Fortune Jul 2 & Jun 26; TradingEconomics.)
- **Weekly move:** Both benchmarks fell for the week. The multi-week drawdown is dramatic — WTI has round-tripped from a war-spike high of ~$95.9 (early June) back to the high-$60s (a ~28% peak-to-trough decline over the June 4–July 4 window). (Source: LiteFinance.)
- **Driver — Hormuz normalization:** Maritime traffic through the Strait of Hormuz "rapidly expanded." The UAE restored exports to **>3.9 million b/d** and Saudi Arabia ramped exports to Asia; on June 27 the US-Navy-overseen Joint Maritime Information Center (JMIC) announced a **widened transit route near Oman**. Al Jazeera (Jul 2, 2026) framed the question as "has the oil shortage turned into a glut?" (Source: TradingEconomics/fxdailyreport Jul 3; Al Jazeera Jul 2; Commons Library.)
- **EIA inventories (week ended June 26, 2026, released July 1):** Commercial crude **-3.775 million bbl** (vs -2.9 mmbbl forecast; prior week -6.088 mmbbl) — a third straight sizable draw. Total crude stocks **441.7 million bbl, ~2% below the 5-year average** for this time of year. (Source: EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report; Investing.com.) Next report: July 8.
- **Natural gas (Henry Hub):** **~$3.20/MMBtu on July 2, 2026**, supported by strong LNG export feedgas flows and record-setting power demand from a major heat wave (temps approaching 100°F from the Plains to the Atlantic through July 4). (Source: EIA/TradingEconomics.)

**Thesis linkage (nuclear/uranium — see nuclear/THESIS.md):**
- **Uranium spot U3O8:** Holding **~$85/lb (late June 2026)**, range-bound since April after erasing the early-2026 surge toward the ~$100/lb Jan 2026 peak. TradeTech's **Long-Term** indicator hit **$93.00/lb on Mar 31, 2026** (+$6.50 since year-end 2025). (Source: TradingEconomics; TradeTech via InvestingNews.)
- **Cameco (CCJ):** ~**$108.89 on June 23, 2026**, +56% over 12 months; Q1 2026 average realized uranium price **$66.21/lb** (+6% YoY); ~28 M lbs/yr committed deliveries 2026–2030. RBC raised its target citing uranium strength, sovereign buyers, and government support for AP1000 new-builds. Consistent with the thesis's structural 30–40 M lb/yr deficit view. (Source: Globe and Mail; Investing.com/RBC.)

## Metals

**Precious metals rallied hard into the long weekend on a weak US jobs print** (see Forex/macro below). Gold logged its first weekly gain since May.

- **Gold:** **$4,170.25/oz on July 3, 2026** (+1.15% on the day), after breaking back above $4,100 on July 2 (+~1.6–2.5% intraday to ~$4,094) on the soft payrolls report. Prior Friday (Jun 26) close was **$4,071.95**, so gold gained **~+2.2% to +2.4% on the week** (Bloomberg: "first weekly gain since May," +2.2%). Context: this is still well below the all-time high of **$5,597.23 set Jan 29, 2026**. (Source: Forbes Advisor Jul 3; Bloomberg/CNBC Jul 2–3; PriceGold Jun 26.)
- **Silver:** **$62.40/oz on July 3, 2026** (+2.41% on the day), up from $60.96 (Jul 2) and $58.74 (Jul 1) — a sharp two-day surge on the same rate-cut-repricing impulse. Silver is **down ~15.5% over the trailing month** (off its own spring highs) but **up ~69% year-over-year**. Gold/silver ratio compressed to ~66.9. (Source: Forbes Advisor; Fortune Jul 1–2; goldsilver.com; bitcoin.com.)
- **Copper:** **COMEX ~$6.17/lb on July 3, 2026** (+0.90% on the day); **LME ~$13,315–13,334/MT** (very elevated by historical standards). Copper is **down ~5.3% over the trailing month** but **up ~23% year-over-year**. S&P Global analysts (Jan 2026) flagged the elevated-price regime as "overextended," though the critical-minerals thesis (150k–330k MT 2026 deficit; Grasberg restart) remains the structural bull case. ## Forex

**Macro backdrop reframed:** Through mid-2026 the dollar had firmed on **war-driven inflation and a hawkish Fed** — the DXY hit its highest since May 2025 (~100.7 on June 19). But the **June jobs report (released Thu July 2): nonfarm payrolls +57,000 vs. ~110–115k expected**, the weakest in months, pared bets on a September Fed **hike**. CME FedWatch odds of a September hike fell from ~65% pre-report to **~53.5%**, with the Fed widely expected to hold at the July 28–29 meeting. That dovish-at-the-margin repricing capped the dollar and lifted gold/silver. (Source: Bloomberg/CNBC Jul 2–3; goldsilver.com.)

- **DXY (US Dollar Index):** **~101.4 on Thursday July 2, 2026**, little changed on the day; ended June at 101.28. Roughly flat-to-slightly-firmer on the week, softening after the payrolls miss. (Source: TradingEconomics; Barchart/Statista.)

## Weekend Developments (post-Friday-close, through 2026-07-05)

**1) OPEC+ raised August output (+188k b/d) — decision made TODAY, Sunday July 5, 2026.** Seven OPEC+ producers (Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman) met online July 5 and agreed to increase collective output by **188,000 b/d for August 2026** — the fourth/fifth consecutive monthly hike, continuing the unwind of the April-2023 voluntary cuts. They will keep meeting monthly. Note the important caveat: with Middle East supply logistics only just normalizing post-Hormuz, prior hikes were partly "theoretical"; roughly ~567k b/d of the original cut remained to return as of early July, with full unwind possible by end-September if monthly hikes continue. This adds to the bearish supply narrative already pressuring crude. (Source: Muscat Daily Jul 5; IndexBox Jul 5; CNBC/The National Jun 7.)

**2) Strait of Hormuz — fragile, not resolved (July 4, 2026 headlines).** The US-Iran interim peace deal (signed June 17, 2026; 60-day free-transit window) is being tested:
   - **Iran will "definitely" charge Hormuz transit fees** after the 60-day free period, per Iran's ambassador to China (July 4) — an idea Washington rejects; "friendly" nations would get "special treatment." Post-60-day arrangements remain unclear. (Source: Times of Israel liveblog Jul 4; RFE/RL.)
   - **Iran warned the UK and France** that the strait "is not a theater for the military display of extra-regional powers," after Macron and Starmer floated a **multinational naval mission** to secure freedom of navigation. (Source: Times of Israel Jul 4.)
   - **Backdrop:** The US/Israel air war on Iran began Feb 28, 2026; Iran blockaded Hormuz; a 60-day ceasefire MOU was signed June 17; JMIC widened the transit route June 27. This is the key two-way risk for oil — continued normalization is bearish, any re-escalation or a punitive fee regime is bullish. (Source: Wikipedia "2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis"; Commons Library; Al Jazeera Jul 2.)

**3) Continued precious-metals momentum / dollar softness** carried into the July 3 shortened commodity session on the jobs-driven Fed repricing (covered above). No new weekend metals catalyst beyond the macro follow-through.
