## Weekend Developments (Jun 19 close → Sun Jun 21) — MOST IMPORTANT FOR MONDAY

The dominant Monday-open driver is the **2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis**, which deteriorated over the weekend after appearing to be resolving earlier in the week:

- **Jun 17:** Trump and Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian **signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU)** to end the >3-month 2026 Iran war, signed at a dinner with French President Macron at the **Palace of Versailles** following the G7 summit. Terms: extend the U.S.–Iran ceasefire ~60 days; Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz; U.S. to lift its blockade of Iranian ports. Pakistan (PM Sharif) was the lead mediator. [VPM/NPR, PBS, Britannica]
- **Jun 18:** Pakistan announced the MOU "implies Tehran will promptly reopen the Hormuz Strait" and the U.S. blockade would cease immediately. Markets read this as de-escalation; oil fell to multi-month lows; energy stocks dropped ~2%.
- **Jun 19 (market holiday):** Trump announced a **renewed Israel–Hezbollah ceasefire** (brokered with Qatar and Iran). BUT **U.S.–Iran talks at Bürgenstock, Switzerland were postponed** — Switzerland's foreign ministry confirmed; the White House said VP **JD Vance was no longer traveling** to Switzerland, citing "unresolved logistical issues." **Oil rose** on the renewed uncertainty (Brent back to ~$80).
- **Jun 20 (Saturday):** **Iran announced a re-closure of the Strait of Hormuz**, citing alleged Israeli violations of the agreement; the **U.S. military denied** the allegations. The Strait carries ~20 million bbl/day (~20% of seaborne oil trade).
- **Sun Jun 21:** Per VP Vance, Iran **did not fire on any ships** in the Strait for a second straight night — a tentative de-facto calm despite the announced closure. Situation described as **highly volatile / unresolved**.

**Net for Monday June 22:** Expect **oil and energy equities to gap higher / stay bid** on the re-closure and cancelled talks, with **broad risk sensitivity to any tanker incident**. The setup is whipsaw-prone: a genuine reopening would slam oil back down; any kinetic escalation would spike it. This weekend development outweighs last week's rotation in importance.

## Breadth & Sentiment

**The FOMC (June 17) was the week's defining event and a genuine regime signal:**
- Fed **held at 3.50–3.75%** on a **unanimous 12-0 vote** — Kevin Warsh's **first meeting as Fed Chair**.
- **Dot plot flipped hawkish:** median 2026 rate projection rose to **~3.8%** (i.e., implying **hikes**), a flip from March when the median implied a cut. **9 of 18** participants project **at least one hike** by end-2026; **6 project two 25bp hikes**.
- **Inflation risk skew:** 17 of 18 participants see **upside** inflation risk; PCE inflation projection raised to **3.6%** YE 2026 (from 2.7% in March); unemployment **4.3%**.
- **Warsh style shift:** declined to give his own dot; the FOMC statement was **~130 words** (≈⅓ the length of the April statement). This hawkish surprise drove Wednesday's broad selloff.
