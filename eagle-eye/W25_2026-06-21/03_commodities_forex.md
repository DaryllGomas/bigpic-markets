# Eagle Eye W25 — Agent 3: Commodities & Forex
**Point-in-time snapshot as of Sunday 2026-06-21.** Covers the trading week ending Friday **2026-06-19** plus breaking developments through the weekend.

> **Headline:** The US-Iran war ended (on paper). A memorandum of understanding (MoU) was **signed Wed June 17, 2026** at Versailles (post-G7), not Friday — and the separate **Geneva signing ceremony scheduled for Fri June 19 was abruptly postponed**, injecting fresh uncertainty. Crude collapsed ~8-10% on the week as Strait of Hormuz shipping began recovering, then bounced over the weekend after **Iran "declared" the Strait closed again on June 20** (a claim CENTCOM denied — oil kept flowing). Meanwhile a **hawkish FOMC** (split dot plot, half of members projecting hikes) drove the **DXY to ~101, its highest since May 2025**, crushing gold (3rd straight weekly drop) and pinning **USD/JPY near a 40-year low (~160.8)** despite a BoJ hike to 1%.
>
> **Note on the holiday:** Friday June 19 was **Juneteenth**. CBOT grains were closed (no Friday grain settlement); US cash/futures liquidity was thin and several "Friday close" prints reflect Thursday settles or pre-holiday levels. Weekend/Monday-open proxies are labeled "as of <date>."

## Energy

### Crude Oil — the dominant story
The week was defined by the unwinding of the Iran war risk premium, then a partial weekend re-rating.

| Benchmark | Friday 2026-06-19 (approx) | Weekly change | Current (as of 2026-06-21) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brent | ~$80–81/bbl | **≈ −8.5%** | **$81.42/bbl** (TradingEconomics, +1.03% d/d) |
| WTI | ~$77/bbl (intraday ~$77.54) | **≈ −10%** | ~$78–79/bbl (opened week +1.5%) |

- **Timeline that matters (verified in-window):**
  - **Sun June 14:** Initial framework announced; WTI gapped down >4.5% to ~$80, Brent ~$83 at Sunday-evening open — lowest since early March. ([NBC](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/deal-reached-united-states-iran-war-rcna350039))
  - **Wed June 17:** Trump and Iranian President Pezeshkian **signed the MoU** during the G7 dinner at the Palace of Versailles. Terms: 60-day ceasefire extension, reopening of the Strait to commercial shipping **toll-free for 60 days**, lifting of the US naval blockade of Iranian ports, discussion of up to ~$25B in frozen-asset release. ([NBC](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/iran/strait-hormuz-reopen-us-lift-iran-sanctions-14-point-deal-seeking-end-rcna350513))
  - **Fri June 19:** The separate **Geneva (Bürgenstock) signing ceremony was abruptly postponed**, and US-Iran talks in Switzerland were called off, underscoring that the framework is not yet a final peace. Brent actually **rose ~0.9% on the day to ~$80.57** on this renewed uncertainty even as it logged a steep weekly loss. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/19/oil-prices-wti-brent-crude-us-iran-deal-strait-hormuz-shipping-recovery.html))
- **Why the weekly crash:** Tankers carrying previously stranded crude began **exiting the Strait on Thursday June 18**; US CENTCOM lifted restrictions on traffic to/from Iranian ports; **Kuwait said it would begin increasing production.** Crude erased nearly all gains since the war began Feb 28. A separate **Israel–Hezbollah ceasefire** (set to begin Friday) added to the de-risking. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/19/oil-prices-wti-brent-crude-us-iran-deal-strait-hormuz-shipping-recovery.html))
- **Strait of Hormuz status — ANNOUNCED reopening, NOT a clean physical reopening.** Iran has effectively controlled the Strait (~20% of seaborne oil) since the war began **Feb 28, 2026**. The MoU commits Iran to "best efforts" safe passage toll-free for 60 days (Article 5). This is a ceasefire-era reopening still being tested — full normalization is a forward (~Q3 2026) process, not complete. ([Energy Connects](https://www.energyconnects.com/news/oil/2026/june/us-iran-peace-deal-what-happens-to-strait-of-hormuz-supplies/), [CFR](https://www.cfr.org/articles/trumps-iran-deal-reopens-the-strait-much-remains-to-be-done))

### EIA inventories
- Week ending **June 12, 2026** (released June 17): **US crude inventories −8.262M bbl** to 418.2M bbl — a far larger draw than the ~4.6M consensus. Refinery utilization +1.4pp; gasoline stocks −0.906M bbl to 214.2M. ([EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report](https://www.eia.gov/petroleum/supply/weekly/))

### OPEC+
- At its **May 3, 2026** meeting (first without the UAE, which exited), the OPEC+ seven (Saudi, Russia, Iraq, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman) approved a modest **+188,000 bpd** adjustment for June. Saudi and Russia each took the largest +62,000 bpd share, targeting 10.291M and 9.762M bpd respectively. Read as a symbolic/disciplined increase during the Hormuz crisis. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/03/opec-announces-188000-barrels-per-day-output-increase-.html), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/5/3/opec-announces-symbolic-oil-output-rise-during-strait-of-hormuz-closure))

### Natural Gas
- Henry Hub remains structurally soft on a supply glut. Spot averaged **$2.94/MMBtu in May** (+$0.17 vs April); EIA projects ~**$3.34/MMBtu for 2H26**. Production growth continues to outpace demand; no Iran-war spike given US gas is not Hormuz-exposed. ([EIA STEO](https://www.eia.gov/outlooks/steo/report/natgas.php))

Driver: the **June 17 FOMC** left rates unchanged (3.50–3.75%) but **signaled tighter policy ahead — roughly half of FOMC members project at least one 2026 hike** (a split, hawkish dot plot). ## Weekend Developments (post-Friday 2026-06-19)

1. **Iran "declares" the Strait of Hormuz closed again — June 20, 2026.** Iran's military cited continued **Israeli operations as a violation of the truce/MoU**; the US military **denied the claim**. Crucially, **CENTCOM (Capt. Tim Hawkins) stated "Iran does not control the Strait of Hormuz"** and confirmed **>17 million barrels of crude transited Hormuz over the June 21–22 weekend**, with laden supertankers still moving per AIS tracking. So far this is a **rhetorical/declared closure, not a physical one.** ([Wikipedia: 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis), [OilPrice](https://oilprice.com/Latest-Energy-News/World-News/Oil-Prices-Spike-as-Iran-Declares-Strait-of-Hormuz-Closed.html), [Discovery Alert](https://discoveryalert.com.au/strait-hormuz-oil-flows-iran-closure-2026-market-data/))
2. **Trump (June 20):** reaffirmed **no tolls for Hormuz passage during or after the 60-day ceasefire.** ([NBC](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/iran/strait-hormuz-reopen-us-lift-iran-sanctions-14-point-deal-seeking-end-rcna350513))
3. **Oil re-rated higher into the new week (proxy):** Brent **opened the week of June 22 up >1.5%, ~$81.42 (as of 2026-06-21)** on renewed skepticism about the durability of the peace and the Iran closure rhetoric — but the move is **muted** vs. the war-peak ($95+ intraday earlier in the conflict), because physical flows continue. ([TradingEconomics Brent](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/brent-crude-oil))
4. **Net read:** Markets are treating the conflict as **de-escalating but fragile**. The base case is continued normalization (bearish oil, bullish risk/USD), but a genuine physical Hormuz re-closure or collapse of the Geneva process is the key tail risk to re-arm the war premium. The Geneva ceremony postponement + Iran's June 20 closure claim are the two fresh datapoints arguing for caution.
