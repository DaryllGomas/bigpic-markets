## Executive Summary

The defining event was the **June 17 FOMC** — Chair **Kevin Warsh's debut meeting**. The Fed **held at 3.50–3.75% (unanimous 12-0)** as expected, but the **dot plot flipped hawkish**: the median 2026 year-end rate jumped to **3.8%** (from 3.4% in March), and **9 of 18** participants now project at least one **hike** in 2026. Market-implied odds of a **hike by December 2026 surged to ~77%**, up from ~24% a month earlier. Warsh delivered a radically shortened **130-word statement** (vs 341 in April), declined to submit his own dot, and announced **five task forces** to overhaul Fed operations including the balance sheet and the SEP/forward-guidance framework.

Despite the hawkish FOMC day, **the full trading week was a net bond rally / bull-steepener** — yields fell across the curve over the week as oil tumbled, May core CPI came in softer than feared, and the US-Iran ceasefire framework cooled the inflation scare from the prior week's sharp selloff. **Note: Friday June 19 was a full market holiday (Juneteenth)** — US cash bond, equity, and futures markets were closed (SIFMA full close). The last full session was **Thursday June 18**, and the FOMC-day prints are dated **June 17**.

**Weekend wildcard:** The US-Iran peace-deal **signing ceremony scheduled for Friday June 19 in Switzerland was postponed** after Iran delayed its delegation over ongoing Israeli strikes in Lebanon; VP Vance's flight was cancelled Thursday night. A digital ceasefire MoU had reportedly been signed June 17, and Iran continued to honor the Strait of Hormuz commitment, but the durable deal remains unsigned as of this snapshot.

## Fed Watch

### June 17, 2026 FOMC — the decision
- **Rate decision:** Held the federal funds target range at **3.50%–3.75%**. **Unanimous 12-0 vote.** Warsh's first meeting as Chair. Hold was ~99% priced.
- **Statement:** Slashed to **~130 words** (vs 341 on April 29). **Removed prior language signaling a bias toward future cuts.** Warsh's stated preference is simpler, less forward-guidance-heavy communication.
- **Balance sheet / reserves:** The Fed continued **reserve-management T-bill purchases at $10B/month** for the June–July cycle (down from the initial $40B/month pace announced Dec 2025; stepped to $25B in April, $10B in May). Cumulative purchases >$284B since Dec 12, 2025. Fed balance sheet ~$6.6–6.7T (peaked ~$9T in 2022). Warsh formed a **task force to review the ~$6.7T balance sheet** (one of five new task forces: communications, balance-sheet policy, data sources, productivity/jobs, and the inflation framework).

### Dot plot (the hawkish pivot — the market's focus)
- **Median fed funds projections:** 2026 **3.8%** (up from 3.4% in March) | 2027 **3.6%** | 2028 **3.4%** | longer run **3.1%**.
- **2026 dot distribution (18 submitted; Warsh did not submit a dot):** roughly **9 above** the current midpoint (hikes), **8 at** the midpoint (hold), **1 below** (cut). Breakdown reported as: 1 dot at +75bp, ~5 at +50bp, ~3 at +25bp, 8 at no change, 1 at −25bp. **Six** of the hike-camp support **two** 25bp increases.
- **Inflation risk skew:** **17 of 18** participants saw inflation risks to the **upside**, 1 balanced, 0 to the downside.

### SEP (Summary of Economic Projections)
| Metric | 2026 | 2027 | 2028 | Longer run |
|--------|------|------|------|------------|
| Fed funds (median) | 3.8% | 3.6% | 3.4% | 3.1% |
| Real GDP | 2.2% (↓ from 2.4%) | 2.3% | 2.2% | ~2.0% |
| Unemployment | 4.3% (↓ from 4.4%) | 4.3% | 4.2% | — |
| Headline PCE | **3.6% (↑ from 2.7%)** | 2.3% | 2.0% | 2.0% |
| Core PCE | **3.3% (↑ from 2.7%)** | ~2.x | ~2.0% | — |

The **+0.9pp upward revision to 2026 headline PCE (to 3.6%)** is the engine behind the hawkish dots — the oil-spike/CPI inflation impulse forced the Committee to abandon its cut bias.

### Warsh press conference (debut)
- Said **"inflation remains well ahead of the 2% goal"** and called persistently high prices **"a burden,"** but cautioned **"the recent past need not be a prologue"** (i.e., not pre-committing to hikes).
- Did not provide his own rate projection (a long-standing critic of the dot plot and forward guidance).
- Framed the five task forces as a structural overhaul of how the Fed communicates and operates.

### Market pricing reaction (CME FedWatch / fed funds futures)
- **Odds of a hike by December 2026 jumped to ~77%**, up from ~24% a month earlier.
- Day-after distribution for year-end 2026: roughly **~14% hold**, **~36% one 25bp hike**, **~34% 50bp of hikes** (vs ~40% hold the day before). The cut trade for 2026 was effectively **killed**. Some traders eyed a first hike as early as **October**.
- **Context vs CALENDAR.md:** the local calendar (last updated Feb 2026) had assumed **3 cuts (75bp) priced by year-end** with funds at 3.50–3.75%. That dovish baseline is now **completely inverted** — the market has gone from pricing cuts to pricing hikes within one quarter.

### Fed speakers
Post-meeting the FOMC is in its customary **post-decision communications window**; the dominant signal this week was the statement, SEP, and Warsh's presser rather than individual speakers. No material in-window (Jun 20–21) individual Fed speeches surfaced beyond the meeting itself. **FOMC minutes** for the June 17 meeting are not yet released (typically ~3 weeks later, i.e., early-to-mid July).

Sources: CNBC, Fox Business, Chase, TradingKey, StockTitan, Yahoo Finance, Bondsavvy, U.S. Bank, PBS, Yahoo (Warsh balance-sheet task force).

## Liquidity

- **Fed balance sheet:** ~**$6.6–6.7T** (down from ~$9T peak in 2022). Reserve-management **T-bill purchases continue at $10B/month** for the June–July cycle (de-escalated from $40B/mo in Dec 2025). Cumulative purchases >$284B since Dec 12, 2025. This ongoing bill-buying is adding reserves and supporting an **ample-reserves** posture even as QT-era runoff has ended.
- **Warsh balance-sheet review:** new **task force to review the ~$6.7T balance sheet** — a potential structural swing factor for liquidity policy (e.g., questions of scarce vs ample reserves under Warsh).
- **TGA / ON RRP:** Mechanically, increases in the **Treasury General Account** and **ON RRP** drain reserves one-for-one. ON RRP has been drained to low levels through 2024–2025 as the facility's role shrank; the TGA's swings around tax dates and debt issuance remain the key reserve-drain variable. (Specific daily Jun 18 TGA/RRP dollar levels were not cleanly retrievable in-window from a single authoritative cite; flagged as a data gap — see Treasury Daily Treasury Statement / FRED RRPONTSYD / WTREGEN for the point-in-time figures.)
- **Settlement note:** Juneteenth (Jun 19) paused Fed payment rails and DTC settlement, making **Monday June 22** the key cash-settlement date for the week's trades.

Sources: Federal Reserve H.4.1, Goldman Sachs Asset Management (Fed balance sheet/money markets), Yahoo Finance (Warsh task force), FRED, SIFMA.

## Weekend Developments

**1. US-Iran deal signing POSTPONED (Fri Jun 19) — the big one.**
- The peace-deal **signing ceremony scheduled for Friday June 19** at the Bürgenstock resort near Lucerne, Switzerland **was postponed/called off**. The Swiss Foreign Ministry confirmed the talks would not proceed as planned.
- **Cause:** Iran delayed sending its delegation over ongoing **Israeli military operations in southern Lebanon** (strikes on June 18–19 reportedly killed ~18). Iran's chief negotiator said any talks remained bound by Tehran's "red lines," demanding a halt to Israeli attacks on Lebanon.
- **VP Vance:** had prepared an overnight flight to Switzerland but the trip was **abruptly cancelled Thursday night (Jun 18)**; he remained in Washington and publicly criticized Israeli hardliners ("If I were in the cabinet of the Israeli government, I might not be attacking the only powerful ally that I have anywhere left").
- **What IS in place:** A **digital ceasefire MoU was reportedly signed June 17** (a "14-point" interim framework: codifies Iran/Lebanon ceasefires, removes the US naval blockade, promises to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, 60-day ceasefire window). The thorny issues — nuclear enrichment, sanctions relief, up to **$25B in frozen Iranian assets** — are deferred to a **second phase**. The durable, signed peace deal does **not** exist as of this snapshot.
- **Hormuz / oil:** Iran **continued to honor the Strait of Hormuz commitment** ("for the second night in a row, did not shoot at any ships" — Vance). **Oil rose modestly on the postponement** (Brent +0.9% to **$80.57**, WTI ~+1.2% to ~$77.54 on Jun 19) but was **still down sharply for the week** on the broader ceasefire optimism. A late Israel–Hezbollah ceasefire briefly pulled prices back down.

**2. Rates/credit weekend read.** US markets were closed Friday (Juneteenth) and over the weekend. No in-window (Jun 20–21) individual Fed speech or policy change surfaced beyond the FOMC itself. The key open risk into the Monday Jun 22 reopen: **if the Iran talks collapse and Israeli strikes escalate, the oil/inflation-premium re-prices higher**, which — against a Fed that just turned hawkish and a market pricing ~77% odds of a 2026 hike — would be the cleanest path to a renewed front-end Treasury selloff and credit-spread widening. Conversely, continued Hormuz calm and resumed talks would extend the bull-steepening relief rally.

**3. Convergence context (from CALENDAR.md).** This was the **Jun 16–26 convergence window**: FOMC (Jun 17) + Triple Witch / S&P rebalance (Jun 19) + Russell reconstitution (Jun 26). The Juneteenth holiday landing on triple-witch Friday (Jun 19) is an unusual structural quirk — witching-related flows were compressed into Thursday Jun 18.
