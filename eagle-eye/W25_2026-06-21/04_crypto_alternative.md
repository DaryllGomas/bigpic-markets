## Regulation & Institutional

**Market structure — CLARITY Act (the big 2026 catalyst):**
- The **Digital Asset Market CLARITY Act** has passed the House and cleared **both Senate committees** — the **Senate Banking Committee advanced it 15-9 on May 14, 2026**. Still needed: a merged Senate bill, an ethics provision, and **60 floor votes**.
- Framework: a **three-bucket system** — *digital commodities* (BTC, ETH, SOL → CFTC), *investment-contract assets* (startup-equity-like tokens → SEC), and **stablecoins as a separate category** with joint SEC/CFTC oversight (direct yield on idle holdings restricted; activity-linked rewards allowed).
- Treasury Secretary Bessent has framed passage as a **spring 2026 target**; Ripple CEO Garlinghouse pegs odds at **80–90%**. The market treats CLARITY as one of the largest regulatory catalysts of the year — a positive overhang/catalyst rather than an in-window event this week.

**Stablecoins — GENIUS Act:** Now in the **implementation/rulemaking phase**, with Treasury, OCC, the Fed, FDIC, and FinCEN all issuing rules. This is the regulatory backdrop for the record ~$315–321B stablecoin supply.

**Institutional / corporate treasuries — under stress:**
- The **June 2026 crypto rout erased ~$62B in combined market cap** from public companies holding BTC as a treasury asset — the corporate-treasury ("MicroStrategy model") trade is being openly questioned.
- **Strategy (MSTR)** held **846,842 BTC at a ~$66,384.56 average cost (as of 6/15/2026)** — meaning at ~$63.6K spot, **Strategy is now underwater on its average purchase price.** Critically, in June 2026 Strategy disclosed it **sold 32 BTC**, a rare break from its "never sell" stance. (For January 2026 context, MSTR had accounted for ~97.5% of net new corporate BTC purchases — so a pivot here matters for the whole corporate-bid thesis.)
- Net: the institutional bid that powered 2024–25 (ETF inflows + corporate treasuries) is currently **neutral-to-negative**, even as longer-term structural rails (regulated ETFs, GENIUS/CLARITY, record stablecoin supply) keep building.

**Cross-thesis note (Quantum):** The BigPic quantum thesis flags **~$718B of BTC in quantum-vulnerable P2PK addresses** and a 5–10 year BIP-360 upgrade path — a long-tail structural risk, not a 2026 trading factor, but worth tracking as quantum milestones accumulate.

## Weekend Developments (2026-06-21)

**The weekend is dominated by US–Iran headlines and the Strait of Hormuz — crypto is acting as the 24/7 risk barometer.**

- **Prices firmed over the weekend.** After Friday's flush, BTC recovered toward **$64,000** and sits **~$63,600** Sunday; ETH ~$1,709; SOL ~$72.7. Net weekly change ≈ flat for BTC, modestly positive for ETH.
- **Switzerland talks underway.** The **first round of US–Iran talks concluded in Switzerland on June 21** (mediated by Qatar and Pakistan; **VP JD Vance** participating; technical-level implementation talks scheduled in **Bürgenstock**). Day-one focus: **frozen assets and oil-sanctions relief.**
- **Strait of Hormuz wildcard.** **Iran said it closed the Strait of Hormuz again** in response to Israeli strikes in Lebanon. **VP Vance countered there is "no evidence" the Strait is closed.** This contradiction is the key weekend swing factor: the Strait carries ~1/5 of world oil/LNG, so a genuine closure would spike energy → inflation → fewer rate cuts → risk-off. Crypto is trading this headline-by-headline.
- **No major weekend liquidation cascade reported** as of this snapshot — Friday's move was an orderly risk-off rather than a forced-liquidation event, and weekend price action was stabilizing/recovering. (Context: 2026 has seen multiple $500M–$1B liquidation days, and a late-May Hormuz scare drove ~$897M in long liquidations — so the setup for a violent move exists if Hormuz headlines escalate.)

**Net weekend read:** Crypto is in a fragile, headline-driven equilibrium. The bullish case (de-escalation + eventual rate-cut relief + CLARITY passage + record stablecoin dry powder) and the bearish case (hawkish Fed, Hormuz tail risk, ETF/treasury outflows, BTC ~40% below ATH) are finely balanced around the **$63–64K BTC** level. A confirmed Hormuz closure is the most likely catalyst for a sharp downside break; a clean de-escalation + dovish Fed re-pricing is the upside trigger.
