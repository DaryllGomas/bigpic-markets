**Key Weekly Narrative:** The dominant macro force this week was the **2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis**. Following the U.S.-Israeli "Operation Epic Fury" airstrikes on Iran (launched Feb 28), Iran's IRGC declared the Strait effectively closed on March 4, threatening not "a litre of oil" would pass. Brent crude surpassed $100/bbl for the first time since 2022, peaking at ~$126/bbl intra-week before settling as the IEA announced a coordinated 400 million barrel strategic reserve release on March 12. WTI settled at $98.71 on Friday (+3.1% on the day). The energy shock rippled through every asset class — industrials, consumer staples, and materials were hit hardest, while energy and defense outperformed.

### Rotation Analysis

The week saw a clear **risk-off rotation into energy, commodities, and defense** while growth, industrials, and consumer sectors underperformed. The Seeking Alpha 1-Minute Market Report characterized the selling as "orderly" with investors "repositioning to lower risk profiles and taking advantage of rapidly rising energy prices." Large-cap growth stocks, especially semiconductors and software, faced strong headwinds. Developed foreign markets and mid-cap growth saw the steepest declines. Defense stocks surged — the iShares U.S. Aerospace & Defense ETF is up +14% YTD in 2026 as spending expectations accelerate around the Iran conflict.
