## Regulation & Institutional

### SEC-CFTC Historic Memorandum of Understanding (Mar 11)

The most significant U.S. crypto regulatory development in years: the SEC and CFTC signed a formal MOU on March 11 that **officially classifies Bitcoin and Ethereum as digital commodities under CFTC jurisdiction**, ending years of interagency turf wars. CFTC Chairman Michael Selig called it a step toward eliminating "duplicate and burdensome regulatory requirements." SEC Chairman Paul Atkins stated this could mark a "new era of regulatory clarity."

**Key Framework Details:**
- BTC, ETH, LTC, and other "sufficiently decentralized" infrastructure tokens classified as **digital commodities** (CFTC oversight)
- Tokens issued through ICOs and certain centralized governance tokens remain under **SEC jurisdiction**
- The SEC separately submitted interpretive guidance to the White House on March 3 explaining how federal securities laws apply to crypto assets

### CBDC Ban (Mar 12)

The U.S. Senate passed a **Central Bank Digital Currency prohibition** by an overwhelming 89-10 bipartisan vote, while the SEC's Investor Advisory Committee recommended limited exemptions for tokenized securities.

## Quantum Vulnerability Watch

**BIP-360 Progress:** BIP-360 (Pay-to-Merkle-Root / P2MR) has been added to the official Bitcoin repository, representing Bitcoin's first concrete step toward quantum resistance. The proposal removes the quantum-vulnerable key path spending option from Taproot, committing solely to a Merkle root script tree.

**Scale of Risk:** An estimated ~$718B in Bitcoin sits in quantum-vulnerable P2PK (Pay-to-Public-Key) addresses. BIP-360 does not retroactively protect these -- users must manually migrate funds to P2MR outputs, a process that depends on user behavior and could take years.

**Timeline:** Full PQC (post-quantum cryptography) migration -- including adoption of lattice-based (Dilithium/ML-DSA) or hash-based (SPHINCS+) signature schemes -- is estimated at 5-10 years. BIP-360 is a necessary but insufficient first step. The quantum threat remains a cross-cutting theme for Bitcoin's long-term security model.
