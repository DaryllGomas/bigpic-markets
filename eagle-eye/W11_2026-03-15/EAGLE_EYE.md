## This Week's Take

**The Iran war is no longer a tail risk — it is the macro regime.** Fifteen days into Operation Epic Fury, the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed, Brent crude settled above $100 for the second consecutive session on Friday, and the weekend brought *escalation*, not de-escalation. The U.S. struck Kharg Island — Iran's main oil export terminal handling 90% of exports — and Trump openly discussed hitting Iran's oil infrastructure directly. Iran retaliated with missile and drone strikes on U.S. bases and Israel, explicitly rejecting ceasefire talks. A Fortune strategist forecasts "peak war panic" within 1-3 weeks. Sunday night futures show oil gapping higher (WTI $100.37, Brent $105.36) and equity futures pointing down. This is the backdrop for what was already the most event-dense week of 2026.

That week-ahead calendar is staggering: NVIDIA's GTC keynote Monday, the FOMC's first 2026 dot plot Wednesday (where the Fed must acknowledge a stagflation scenario — oil-driven inflation surging while Q4 GDP was just revised down to 0.7%), Micron earnings Wednesday evening, and the convergence of Triple Witching + S&P 500 quarterly rebalance on Friday with $5-6T in notional expiration. The VIX sits at 27.29, market breadth is at its narrowest since the 2024 AI mania peak, and the put/call ratio at 0.58 suggests traders are dangerously complacent about downside risk. The S&P 500 posted its third consecutive losing week and sits at its lowest close of 2026. Position sizing and risk management are paramount.

Beneath the geopolitical noise, the structural investment theses are evolving rapidly. The rare earth export-vs-domestic price spread in China has blown out to 63% (NdPr at $104/kg domestic vs. $184/kg FOB China), validating the critical minerals reshoring thesis as MP Materials ships its first commercial NdFeB magnets. Defense names are surging (LMT +34.7% YTD, NOC +29.8%) while the Vertiv S&P 500 inclusion (+8.6% on the add) confirms AI infrastructure's transition from growth theme to index staple. Bitcoin, remarkably, is outperforming stocks and gold since the war began — up 7% vs. gold's -2% — even as the Fear & Greed Index hits COVID-era lows. Whales accumulated 270K BTC ($19.3B) in 30 days, the largest net purchase in 13 years. The SEC-CFTC MOU officially classifying BTC and ETH as digital commodities may prove the most consequential regulatory development of the year.
