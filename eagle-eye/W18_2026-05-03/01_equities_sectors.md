**Narrative:** Two dominant forces shaped the week. First, the most concentrated megacap earnings season in memory produced outsized beats across the Magnificent Seven stack, with Alphabet and Amazon setting the tone on Wednesday night and Apple delivering the final punctuation mark on Thursday. Q1 2026 blended earnings growth now stands at 27.1% — the highest since Q4 2021 — with 84% of S&P 500 reporters beating EPS estimates, the highest beat rate since Q2 2021. Second, geopolitical relief: oil prices pulled back from the $100–$102/bbl range after Iran submitted a new diplomatic proposal to Washington, easing near-term fears about a prolonged Strait of Hormuz supply disruption.

The week was not without volatility. Monday saw a sharp AI sentiment shock when the Wall Street Journal reported OpenAI fell short of both user and revenue growth targets, snapping an 18-day winning streak for semiconductor stocks and sending NVDA, AMD, and AVGO sharply lower intraday before they recovered. The Fed held rates at 3.5–3.75% on Wednesday in what was almost certainly Jerome Powell's final press conference as Chair (term ends May 15), with an unusual 8–4 split vote — the highest dissent in three decades.

## Weekend Developments

**Geopolitical: Iran Signals Openness to Deal.** The most significant weekend development is the Iran diplomatic channel. On Sunday May 3, President Trump posted on Truth Social announcing "Project Freedom," describing U.S. plans to help free cargo ships stranded by the Strait of Hormuz closure. Trump's post followed Iran's new agreement proposal submitted to Washington. U.S. crude fell toward $102/bbl on Friday on the news; futures pointed to continued softening Monday if talks advance.

**Strait of Hormuz Context:** The Strait has been effectively closed since late February 2026, when the U.S.-Israel air campaign against Iran prompted Iran to formalize control over maritime traffic. The IEA has called this the "largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market," with Brent having surged past $120/bbl in March before partially recovering. Any genuine diplomatic breakthrough would be an immediate market catalyst — particularly for airline, industrials, and consumer discretionary stocks pressured by high energy costs.

**Sunday Night Futures:** S&P 500 futures +0.1%, Nasdaq 100 futures flat to +0.1%, Dow futures +86 points (+0.2%). Markets were muted as investors balanced strong earnings tailwinds against unresolved Middle East uncertainty.

**Fed Leadership:** Powell confirmed he will step down as Chair on May 15 but will remain on the Federal Reserve Board of Governors. Kevin Warsh is expected to succeed him as Chair. Markets are watching closely whether a new Chair accelerates or delays rate cuts given the 8–4 dissent on holding rates.

## Earnings

The week of April 28 – May 1 was the heaviest earnings week of Q1 2026 season, with four Magnificent Seven members reporting on the same evening (April 29).

### Alphabet (GOOGL) — Beat; Stock +10%
- **Revenue:** $109.9B (+22% YoY), beating estimates. Highest growth rate since 2022.
- **Google Cloud:** $20.03B (+63% YoY), with operating income rising to $6.6B from $2.2B prior year. Cloud backlog nearly doubled QoQ to over $460B.
- **Net Income:** $62.58B, or $5.11/share, up 81% YoY.
- **Capex:** Full-year capex updated to up to $190B.
- **Reaction:** Stock surged ~10%, hitting all-time highs. Cloud becoming primary growth driver for the first time. CEO Sundar Pichai: "Enterprise AI solutions have become our primary growth driver for cloud for the first time in Q1." Relevance to thesis: GOOG is a Tier 2 AI/Robotics/Quantum thesis name; this quarter validates the infrastructure thesis.

### Amazon (AMZN) — Beat on Every Line; Stock +5%+
- **Revenue:** $181.5B (+17% YoY), beat estimates.
- **EPS:** $2.78 vs. $1.64 estimate — a massive beat.
- **AWS:** $37.6B (+28% YoY), fastest growth rate in 15 quarters.
- **Capex:** $44.2B in the quarter, primarily AI infrastructure and data centers.
- **Reaction:** Stock broke out to all-time highs. AWS reacceleration narrative firmly in place. AMZN is an AI/Quantum Tier 2 name; the AWS custom chip (Trainium/Inferentia) buildout is progressing.

### Meta Platforms (META) — Beat Revenue; Stock -7% After-Hours
- **Revenue:** $56.31B (+33% YoY), beat estimates of $55.52B.
- **Capex guidance:** Raised to $125–$145B for full-year 2026 (from $115–$135B prior), citing higher component pricing and AI data center costs.
- **Q2 Revenue Guidance:** $58–$61B.
- **Reaction:** Stock fell ~7% in after-hours on the capex increase, then partially recovered Friday. The market's reaction reflects the tension between AI spending and near-term ROI. Relevance: Meta's MTIA custom silicon deepens AVGO's XPU backlog.

### Apple (AAPL) — Beat; Stock +3.4%
- **Revenue:** $111.18B (+17% YoY) vs. $109.66B estimated.
- **EPS:** $2.01 vs. $1.95 estimate. Gross margin 49.3% vs. 48.4% expected.
- **iPhone:** $56.99B — largest segment, but missed estimates for 2nd time in 3 quarters.
- **Services:** $30.98B — all-time record for the category.
- **Capital return:** $100B buyback approved; dividend raised 4% to $0.27/share.
- **Reaction:** Stock jumped 3.4% Friday, carrying NDX and SPX to fresh records to close the week. Strong China performance was highlighted by management.

### Microsoft (MSFT) — Strong Azure; Stock modestly positive
- **Azure:** +31% YoY, with ~12 points from AI services. Still leads in headline cloud growth though AWS gap narrowed.
- Reaction: Modestly positive; investors focused on the Alphabet/Amazon fireworks.

### Key Q1 2026 Earnings Season Stats
- **Blended earnings growth:** 27.1% — highest since Q4 2021
- **Beat rate:** 84% of reporters beat EPS estimates — highest since Q2 2021
- **Consecutive quarters of double-digit growth:** 6th

**VIX at 17 tells a clear story:** The panic from the Iran war's early shock phase (which sent VIX into the 30s in March) has fully unwound. At 17, implied volatility is priced for calm — which also means the market has little cushion if Iran talks break down or earnings disappoint. The 8-4 FOMC dissent is a subtle flag: unusual internal Fed division could inject rate-path uncertainty into a market that has priced in a smooth transition to new leadership under Warsh.

**Breadth caution:** The S&P 500's advance-decline line has not confirmed the new price highs. The April rally was described by multiple sources as "top-heavy," with large-cap tech accounting for a disproportionate share of index gains. This is not unusual in a concentrated AI-driven cycle, but it does mean the rally's durability depends heavily on continued megacap execution.

**Looking ahead:** Jobs week. April nonfarm payrolls report and a full slate of labor data will dominate the week of May 4–8. Any softness in labor could revive rate-cut hopes; a hot print would reinforce the hold narrative under an already divided FOMC.
