# Crypto & Alternative Assets — Week Ending May 1, 2026

## Bitcoin

**Current price (as of Sunday May 3, ~8:48 AM EDT): ~$78,804** (range across sources: $78,200–$78,900)

Bitcoin posted its best monthly gain in a year during April, surging **+11.87%** to close the month at approximately $76,580 — the second consecutive green monthly candle after five straight down months. The April high reached roughly $79,500 before some profit-taking into month-end.

The weekly tape for April 28–May 1 was choppy. BTC dipped to around $75,500 mid-week amid ETF outflow pressure, then recovered sharply on May 1 as big-tech earnings optimism bled into risk assets and spot ETF inflows returned ($630M on May 1 alone). By the weekend, BTC has climbed back into the $78,200–$78,900 band.

**Key levels:**
- Resistance: $79,331 (near-term), $80,000 (major psychological/liquidity wall), $84,000–$85,500 (next target on a convincing close above $80K)
- Support: $75,109 (first floor), $73,000 (deeper flush zone), $72,352 (100-day MA)

**Bitcoin dominance: ~58.4–60.7%** — elevated and approaching the ~60% ceiling that historically, when rejected, has preceded large altcoin rallies. Dominance has climbed from ~56% in late March to this range as capital has rotated back to BTC.

**Market narrative:** CNBC flagged that despite the April rally, buyer demand remains structurally weak — CryptoQuant noted the move was futures-driven rather than spot accumulation-led, raising the risk of another multi-month pullback if $80K fails to break. BTC is also ~38% below its all-time high of $125,100 set in October 2025. Total crypto market cap is approximately **$2.7 trillion** as of May 3, with 24-hour trading volume around $55.5B.

Ark Invest published its "Big Ideas 2026" report on May 1, forecasting Bitcoin's market cap could reach **$16 trillion by 2030** (~$761K/BTC), driven by ETF adoption, corporate treasury accumulation, and gold-substitution demand. U.S. ETFs and public companies now hold ~12% of total Bitcoin supply, up from ~9% a year prior.

## Ethereum

**Current price (as of Sunday May 3): ~$2,315–$2,326**

Ethereum is treading water. ETH held the $2,300 key support zone through the week, closing May 1 at approximately $2,307. The **ETH/BTC ratio** ticked marginally lower (-0.59% on May 1), reflecting Bitcoin's continued dominance leadership.

**Weekly performance:** ETH lagged BTC in April. Spot ETH ETFs saw four straight days of outflows (April 27–30, totaling ~$183.7M) before snapping back with $101.2M in net inflows on May 1. Price remains pinned below $2,500, with analysts citing $2,300 as the critical floor — a break below would open the door to $2,100.

**Gas fees / network activity:** Ethereum mainnet fees have fallen dramatically — average gas prices dropped ~95% year-over-year to approximately 0.052 Gwei (~$0.01–$0.16 per transaction), driven by L2 adoption. Layer 2 networks now handle ~95% of Ethereum's total transaction throughput (~2 million daily transactions), with mainnet itself processing a record 2.89M transactions in a single day in February 2026. Search interest in Ethereum has been climbing even as price stagnates, suggesting latent demand.

**Protocol:** No major protocol updates or hard fork news this week. The Pectra upgrade (earlier 2026) continues to underpin L2 fee compression. The dominant narrative is that Ethereum's "fee burn" mechanism is dampening ETH issuance, but L2 fee compression means less ETH is being burned than in prior cycles — a headwind for the deflationary supply thesis.

**Technical target:** Analysts cite $2,800–$3,000 as an end-of-May target if ETH holds $2,300 support, though price action remains unconvincing. ETH is substantially underperforming its 2021-cycle ATH relative to BTC.

## Regulation & Institutional

### CLARITY Act — Approaching Senate Markup
The Digital Asset Market Clarity Act is nearing a key legislative milestone. Senator Cynthia Lummis confirmed: **"We are going to markup the Clarity Act in May. We are going to get it to the finish line."** The Senate Banking Committee is targeting markup the **week of May 11**.

Key development this week (May 1–2): Senators Tillis and Alsobrooks released a stablecoin yield compromise within the CLARITY Act — banning yield equivalent to bank deposits but permitting "bona fide activities." Crypto trade groups (Coinbase, Circle) immediately endorsed the deal. The SEC also scheduled a CLARITY Act roundtable for May.

Outstanding obstacles: Senator John Kennedy has withheld support; Senator Tillis raised a new DeFi-specific law enforcement concern. The Memorial Day recess (May 21) creates an extremely tight calendar window.

**The SEC schedules CLARITY Act roundtable for May, with Senate Banking markup targeted for May 11 week** — this is the most significant near-term regulatory catalyst for the sector.

### SEC-CFTC Framework (March 17, 2026 — Background)
The SEC and CFTC issued a landmark joint interpretation on March 17 classifying **most crypto assets as commodities** rather than securities — the first formal federal token taxonomy covering digital commodities, collectibles, tools, stablecoins, and digital securities. SEC Chair Atkins described the framework as ending regulatory "turf wars." A new MOU between the agencies serves as a coordination roadmap.
