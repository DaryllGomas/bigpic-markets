## Regulation & Institutional

**CLARITY Act passes Senate Banking Committee (May 14, 2026):** The headline regulatory event of the week. Senate Banking Committee approved the **Digital Asset Market Clarity Act by 15-9 vote**. The 309-page bill:
- Formally **divides oversight between SEC and CFTC** with clearer jurisdictional boundaries
- Establishes when a digital asset is a security vs. commodity
- Creates disclosure and compliance obligations for digital-asset firms
- Now heads to **full Senate floor**, where it needs **60-vote cloture**
- If passed, House-Senate conference must reconcile differences on stablecoins, DeFi, ethics rules

Opposition: banking trade groups, law enforcement (AML/illicit finance concerns), and labor unions warning of financial-stability risk.

**SEC clarification:** SEC issued a press release (#2026-30) clarifying application of federal securities laws to crypto assets — consistent with prior pro-crypto interpretive stance under the current administration.

**Institutional adoption:**
- **Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy):** Holds **~780,000 BTC**; reportedly bought an estimated **25,000 BTC this week** via the STRC vehicle even amid the price drawdown
- Corporate treasuries added **~62,000 BTC in Q1 2026** (most in Jan and early March)
- **Public companies now control >5% of total BTC supply**
- Institutional demand running at **~2.8x new mining supply** (ETFs + treasuries) per Bitcoin Magazine
