# Equities & Sectors — Week Ending 2026-05-15

## Market Overview

US equities closed a volatile week marginally higher in the S&P 500 and Nasdaq, but the tone turned sharply defensive into Friday after a hot CPI print, a 1-year high in 10-year Treasury yields, and an underwhelming Trump-Xi summit deflated mid-week records. The **S&P 500 closed Friday at 7,408.50 (-1.24% on the day, ~+0.3% on the week)**, the **Nasdaq Composite at 26,225.14 (-1.54%, ~+0.3% on the week)**, the **Dow at 49,526.17 (-537.29 pts, -1.07%; -0.05% on the week)**, and the **Russell 2000 at 2,793.30 (-2.44% Friday)**. Earlier in the week, the S&P 500 had topped 7,500 for the first time and the Dow retook 50,000, but Friday's reversal erased most of the rally as traders priced out remaining 2026 rate cuts.

The dominant narrative was the collision of three macro forces: (1) April CPI surprised hot at **+3.8% YoY** (highest since May 2023) with PPI accelerating to **6.0% YoY** from 4.0%; (2) WTI crude settled at **$105.42** (+4% Friday) and Brent at **$109.26** as the Iran/Strait of Hormuz blockade kept ~10M bpd of Gulf exports offline; and (3) the Trump-Xi summit in Beijing (May 14-15) delivered only modest deliverables — a Boeing 200-jet order (vs. 500 floated), US beef access to China, and a Trade/Investment Council framework — disappointing markets that had positioned for a bigger breakthrough. The **10-year Treasury yield spiked 9 bps to 4.55%**, its highest in a year, with markets now pricing virtually zero rate cuts through 2027.

Mega-cap tech absorbed the brunt of the sell-off Friday — NVDA fell 4.42% to $225.32 (off Thursday's all-time-high close of $235.74, but still +11.46% on the week) and AMZN lagged — while Cisco surged 13.4% on a blockbuster AI-networking print. Breadth deteriorated under the surface even as headline indices touched records: NYSE McClellan A-D Oscillator has been below zero since May 11, decline-week breadth rose to 61.4%, and equal-weight S&P 500 closed lower four straight days. Leadership remains narrow and AI-concentrated.

## Weekend Developments

**Geopolitics dominates the weekend tape and threatens Monday's open.**

1. **Iran reasserts Strait of Hormuz blockade (Sat May 16).** After a brief reopening — at least 8 oil/gas tankers transited Saturday morning — the IRGC declared control over Hormuz has "returned to its previous state." Iranian gunboats reportedly fired on a merchant vessel; India summoned Iran's ambassador after a shooting incident involving two Indian-flagged vessels. Iran said Hormuz status will remain "tightly controlled" until the US ends its blockade of Iranian ports. **This is the operative weekend catalyst — expect oil to gap higher Sunday night and an extension of the inflation/yields bear-flattening setup that crushed Friday's tape.**

2. **Trump-Xi summit aftermath underwhelming.** Weekend post-mortems (Euronews, Atlantic Council, CFR, CNBC) characterize the outcome as "meagre" — no grand breakthrough. Boeing fell 4% Friday on the smaller-than-expected aircraft order. China did commit to buying US crude (Trump said Chinese ships will load in Texas, Louisiana, Alaska), which is a partial offset for energy, and tariff arrangements were extended. ⚠️ Risk that disappointed CCP-side messaging in Asian press over the weekend feeds renewed trade-war fear into Monday's open.

3. **Corporate weekend headlines:**
   - **Starbucks** announced 300 corporate layoffs (third round of cuts) plus ~$400M in restructuring charges.
   - **Long Lake to acquire American Express Global Business Travel for $6.3B** — sizeable strategic M&A in the travel-tech space.
   - **SpaceX IPO prospectus expected as soon as this coming week** (Cramer flagged speculative-excess concerns Friday).

4. **April CPI ripple effects.** Weekend analyst pieces (IndexBox, Intellectia) emphasize that the 3.8% headline / 2.8% core CPI has materially repriced the Fed path; futures now imply no cuts through 2027. Watch for hawkish Fed-speak Monday-Tuesday.
