# Commodities & Forex — Week Ending 2026-05-15

## Energy

The energy complex was dominated by the ongoing **2026 Iran war fuel crisis** and the continued effective closure of the **Strait of Hormuz**, characterized by the IEA as the "largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market."

- **WTI crude (CLM26):** Closed near **$105.66/bbl** on Friday May 15, with the contract surging more than +4.2% on the session (Friday-only gain of +$4.25). The contract booked a **weekly gain of approximately +11%**, as the Strait of Hormuz remained effectively closed and stalled US-Iran talks weighed on supply expectations.
- **Brent crude:** Traded around **$109-111/bbl** intraday on Friday, finishing **up ~6-7% on the week**. North Sea Dated cited around $110/bbl by mid-week. The peak during the broader Hormuz crisis has reached as high as $126/bbl (earlier in March).
- **Natural gas (Henry Hub front-month):** Rose to **$2.96/MMBtu** on Friday (+2.28% on the day). The contract spent most of the week below $3.00; ⚠️ a specific weekly change percentage was not directly confirmed in available sources but appears modestly positive given the prior week's sub-$3.00 prints.

**OPEC / supply news:**
- OPEC production fell **1.7 million bpd in April** following a **7.9 million bpd plunge in March**; cumulative wartime decline now exceeds **9.7 million bpd (>30% of OPEC output)**.
- Iraq, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, UAE, Qatar, and Bahrain shut in **~10.5 million bpd** of crude in April. Saudi output fell to its lowest since 1990.
- **UAE formally exited OPEC effective May 1, 2026** — the May 13 Monthly Oil Market Report was the last to include UAE data.
- OPEC+ announced a **+188,000 bpd** June output increase at its first post-UAE-exit meeting (May 3) — symbolic given the magnitude of disrupted Gulf production.
- **OPEC cut 2026 global oil demand growth forecast to ~1.17 mbpd** (from ~1.38 mbpd prior); 2027 outlook *raised* to 1.54 mbpd.
- **EIA inventory:** ⚠️ Week ending May 8 report released May 13; specific weekly change number not confirmed. Reports cite US crude inventories at **~461.6M bbl, roughly in line with the 5-year average**.
- **Forward-looking:** UBS, JPMorgan, and Saudi Aramco CEO Amin Nasser have all warned that OECD commercial inventories could approach **operational stress / record lows by end-May into June** if the Strait remains closed. Aramco estimates the oil market won't normalize until **2027** if disruption persists past mid-June.

## Metals

A **violent risk-off / dollar-strength selloff** crushed precious metals into the close, with US PPI/CPI inflation prints driving rate-cut-removal repricing.

- **Gold:** Closed near **$4,530-4,540/oz**, down ~2% on Friday and **down approximately 3-4% on the week** — the lowest level since early May. Drivers: surging April US wholesale inflation (fastest pace since 2022), April CPI hottest since 2023, rising US yields, DXY strength, and improving US-China trade sentiment after a Trump-Xi meeting earlier in the week. The Senate confirmed **Kevin Warsh as new Fed Chair (54-45 vote)** to replace Powell whose term expired May 15 — read as marginally hawkish.
- **Silver:** Suffered a brutal Friday session — opened at **$77.41/oz**, down **9.3% from Thursday's $85.32 close**, ending the day around **$77.52 (-10.61%)** in a synchronized risk-off selloff. Weekly performance was sharply negative (⚠️ exact full-week percentage not directly confirmed, but the Friday move alone implies a double-digit weekly drawdown).
- **Copper:** Fell to **~$6.25/lb** on Friday (-4.81% on the day), a second straight session of losses as elevated price levels (and Hormuz-driven global growth fears) curbed Chinese buying activity. Weekly direction was lower. The demand signal is mixed: Hormuz-driven uncertainty plus China consumer caution is offsetting the structural 2026 deficit story (ICSG 150K MT / J.P. Morgan 330K MT deficit) cited in the critical-minerals thesis.

## Forex

The dollar dominated as inflation prints reignited Fed-on-hold/Fed-hike risk and Hormuz-driven oil/inflation feedback strengthened the safe-haven bid.

- **DXY:** Closed around **99.27** on Friday, on track to post a **weekly gain of more than +1%**, supported by mounting US inflationary pressures.
- **EUR/USD:** Slid into the **1.1620-1.1615** range on Friday — **fifth consecutive daily decline**, new five-week lows, **more than -1.9% off the April high**. Weekly performance materially negative.
- **USD/JPY:** Rose to **158.59** on Friday; the yen ended the week **down >1%** against the dollar. ⚠️ The yen's weakness is notable given the high oil price (Japan a major energy importer) — adds intervention/BOJ risk.
- **GBP/USD:** Fell to **1.3320** on Friday (-0.58% on the day), the lowest since early April. **Weekly loss near -2%** (high of 1.36495 May 11 → low of 1.33185 May 15). Compounded by UK political risk after MP Josh Simons stepped aside, raising the profile of Andy Burnham as a potential bond-market-unfriendly challenger to PM Keir Starmer.
- **USD/CNY:** Rose to **6.8120** on Friday (+0.40%); PBOC fixed the midpoint near **6.7976-6.8415** during the week — orderly drift weaker for the yuan despite the Trump-Xi week-earlier meeting. ⚠️ No sign of aggressive PBOC depreciation or US intervention.
- **EM:** ⚠️ No standout EM dislocations located in available sources beyond general dollar strength.

## Weekend Developments

The Strait of Hormuz crisis remained the dominant weekend story; the situation is **deteriorating, not improving**:

- **May 16:** Fortune and CNBC reported escalating warnings that **oil markets face a "moment of truth in June"**, with risk of a **"non-linear" price spike and panic buying**. UBS and JPMorgan both flag OECD commercial inventories approaching **all-time lows / operational stress levels** by end-May into early June.
- **Trump-Xi meeting**: Failed to produce a breakthrough to reopen the Strait. The US naval blockade of Iranian oil continues; Iran continues attacks on shipping in the Persian Gulf.
- **UK military deployment:** The UK announced deployment of drones, fighter aircraft, and a Royal Navy warship to an international defensive mission securing commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz.
- **Saudi Aramco** (May 11 statements still live in market discussion): >600 tankers stuck inside the Persian Gulf, 240 more waiting outside; oil market normalization slipping to 2027 if disruption extends past mid-June.
- **No weekend OPEC announcement, no currency intervention, no major mining/supply disruption** identified beyond the ongoing Hormuz crisis through Sunday May 17.

**Trading-day Monday implication:** Inventory-stress narrative is gaining momentum into a market already +11% on the week in WTI. Risk skew is asymmetric — any actual operational stress trigger (refinery slowdowns, shipping panic buying) could produce the "non-linear" spike analysts are warning of.
