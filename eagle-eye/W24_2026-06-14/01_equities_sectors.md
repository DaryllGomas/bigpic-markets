## Market Overview

US equities ground out a modestly higher, value-led week — and then the weekend handed the bulls a much bigger gift. The dominant tape story of the week was **rotation, not direction**: the **Russell 2000 surged +3.9%** while the mega-cap indices added only ~0.65%, as money rotated out of an unwinding AI/semiconductor trade and into small caps, financials, and "real-economy" sectors. The two defining events were **SpaceX's record-shattering IPO on Friday** (the largest in history) and a **Sunday-night US–Iran peace-deal announcement** that sent oil sharply lower and index futures sharply higher heading into Monday.

## Weekend Developments

**This is the most market-moving item in the report.** Treat everything here as *post-Friday-close, as of Sunday 2026-06-14*.

- **US–Iran peace deal declared "complete."** Late Sunday (2026-06-14), President Trump said on social media that the deal with Iran is **"now complete,"** and **authorized the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz**. Pakistan PM Shehbaz Sharif said an official **signing ceremony will take place Friday in Switzerland**. This caps a week of escalating optimism (Trump's mid-week cancellation of planned strikes drove a ~900-point Dow day on Thursday 6/11). Caveats remain: earlier in the weekend Iranian officials were cautious on timing, and reports cited Israeli strikes in Lebanon as a risk to the agreement. *(Source: CNBC live blog 2026-06-14; CNN 2026-06-14; Time 2026-06-12.)*

- **Oil plunged on the Hormuz reopening.** US crude **fell nearly 5% on Sunday 2026-06-14** as the geopolitical risk premium drained out. This is a tailwind for consumers/transports and a headwind for the energy complex that led the tape this week. *(Source: CNBC 2026-06-14; Bloomberg Markets Wrap 2026-06-14.)*

- **Sunday-night index futures jumped.** As of Sunday evening 2026-06-14: **Dow futures +342 pts (~+0.7%), S&P 500 futures +0.9%, Nasdaq-100 futures +1.4%** — risk-on, with growth/tech leading the futures bid even though small caps/value led the cash week. *(Source: CNBC 2026-06-14.)*

- **SpaceX (SPCX) is now public** (see Market Overview / Thesis Watchlist) — the weekend's other structural story, repricing the entire space complex.

**Net read for Monday:** a hot-inflation/hawkish-Fed setup that would normally pressure equities is, for now, being overwhelmed by the Iran de-escalation, lower oil, and the SpaceX-driven risk appetite. Watch whether the bid holds into Tuesday's FOMC start.

## Earnings

A heavy AI/software docket — and the reactions were a clinic in "good news isn't good enough" at premium valuations.

- **Oracle (ORCL) — record beat, stock fell ~10% AH.** Reported Q4 FY2026 on 2026-06-10: **total revenue $19.2B (+21%), total cloud $9.9B (+47%), OCI revenue +93% to $5.8B**, FY26 revenue $67.4B (+17%). **RPO/backlog exploded to $638B** (from $553B). Yet shares **fell ~10% after hours** on the *funding* of that growth: **FY2027 capex guided to ~$70B** and a planned **$20B share sale**. A direct, high-conviction read on the AI thesis's core tension — backlog is real, but the market is now scrutinizing the cash burn and dilution behind the buildout. *(Sources: Oracle IR / SEC 8-K 2026-06-10; indmoney; ERP Today.)*

- **Adobe (ADBE) — beat-and-raise, stock fell ~6%.** Reported 2026-06-11: **record revenue $6.62B (+13%)**, crossed **$500M+ in AI-first ARR (tripled YoY)**, and raised full-year guidance. Stock still **fell ~6%+ to a 52-week low (~$218)** — driven less by the print than by **CFO Dan Durn's abrupt departure to Marvell** (effective 6/15) on top of an ongoing CEO search. A second example of AI monetization proving out (Firefly ARR now material) but being overshadowed. *(Sources: TechTimes / TIKR 2026-06-11/12; Adobe 8-K.)*

- **Cyber (context, early June):** CrowdStrike and Palo Alto both beat with upbeat guidance the prior week but **sold off ~8% and ~3%** respectively post-print, as investors demanded faster AI payoff. PANW CEO Nikesh Arora said demand is "off the charts" (1,200+ companies inbound on AI strategy) but warned against expecting an immediate "windfall." Both names are up ~60% in 2026. *(Source: CNBC 2026-06-05; Bloomberg 2026-06-02.)*

**Theme:** Across Oracle, Adobe, and cyber, the same pattern — **strong fundamentals, premium multiples, and a market now punishing anything short of perfection** (especially capex/dilution and "show-me-the-AI-revenue" skepticism). This is the AI thesis's "proof phase, not promise phase" playing out in tape.
