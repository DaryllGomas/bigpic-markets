## Yield Curve

The curve is **positively sloped and modestly steeper than its prior inversion**, a "late-cycle" shape with a high, sticky front end (2Y anchored near 4.1% by hawkish Fed repricing) and a long end held down by the oil-relief move.

- **2s10s: +39 to +40 bp** as of 2026-06-12/14 (centralbank.watch; StreetStats). Roughly flat-to-marginally steeper on the week (2Y fell slightly more than 10Y).
- **3m10y: +70 to +77 bp** (3-month bill ~3.71%, 10Y ~4.48%). Comfortably positive after a brief negative excursion earlier in the cycle.
- centralbank.watch's 12-month recession-probability model reads **15.9%** — elevated vs. calm conditions but below recessionary thresholds.

The shape is consistent with a "higher-for-longer / possible-hike" Fed: the market is NOT pricing easing, so the front end stays elevated, while the belly/long end reflect oil-driven inflation relief rather than growth optimism.

## Fed Watch

**This was the pre-FOMC blackout week — no Fed speakers.** The 2026 June blackout runs ~June 6 through June 18 (the day after the meeting). The last public Fed communication was 2026-06-07; the next will be Chair Warsh's press conference Wednesday 2026-06-17 (Babypips; TradingView/ForexLive; St. Louis Fed blackout calendar). No FOMC minutes were released this week (the next minutes, from the June meeting, publish in ~3 weeks).

**June 17 FOMC — what's priced:**
- **Rate decision: HOLD is near-certain.** CME FedWatch implied **~97-99.5%** probability of no change, leaving the target range at **3.50-3.75%** (CME FedWatch via growbeansprout/Kalshi, as of 2026-06-13).
- **This is Kevin Warsh's debut meeting and first dot plot as Chair.** Warsh (Fed governor 2006-2011, long-time critic of balance-sheet expansion) is regarded as materially more hawkish than Powell. Markets have repriced sharply since his nomination/confirmation.
- **Dot plot / SEP expected to turn hawkish:** consensus looks for the median 2026 dot to shift to **no cuts** (from one 25 bp cut in the March SEP), with some officials potentially projecting a hike, and near-term inflation forecasts revised up on energy. Commentators flag Warsh may even reform/withhold projections (multiple June previews: Chase, Continuum Economics, TradingKey, VT Markets — all dated June 2026).
- **Rest of 2026:** CME FedWatch shows roughly **70%** odds of at least one HIKE by year-end (CME Group, post-NFP 2026-06-05), with ~43% specifically pricing a +25 bp move by December and >20% pricing a +50 bp move. This is a near-complete inversion of the "3 cuts / 75 bps" base case from February's calendar note. The policy debate has shifted from "when do they cut" to "do they hike, and how much."

**Backdrop driving the hawkish repricing:** May CPI +4.2% YoY / +0.5% MoM (core +2.9% YoY); May PPI +6.5% YoY / +1.1% MoM (core +0.4%). Energy did the damage — CPI energy +3.9% MoM (+23.5% YoY), PPI energy +10.7% MoM, wholesale gasoline +23.4% — almost entirely the Middle East oil shock rather than broad domestic inflation. A resilient labor market (strong May NFP) reinforces the hawkish lean.

## Credit Markets

Credit remained **historically tight but began showing early signs of widening** as the Iran war and inflation shock crept into risk premia.

- **IG (ICE BofA US Corporate) OAS: ~80 bp** — still near multi-cycle tights (FRED BAMLC0A0CM / investmentgrade.com, June 2026). BBB ~100 bp, AA ~50 bp.
- **HY (ICE BofA US High Yield) OAS: ~275 bp** (2.75%) per FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2 / Trading Economics (June 2026), up modestly from ~281 bp at end-2025 reference levels and showing the first signs of widening.
- **Direction:** Morningstar (June 2026) reported that "amid Iran war, credit spreads show early signs of widening." The IG-HY gap (~195 bp) is wide-ish for the cycle but not stressed. MUFG's 2026 base case looked for HY drift toward ~450 bp; spot remains well inside that.
- **Supply watch:** heavy long-duration IG issuance — much of it AI/data-center-linked capex financing — is a structural widening risk flagged by PineBridge/MUFG, though it hadn't bitten meaningfully as of this week.

Net: credit is sending a cautious-but-not-alarmed signal. Spreads are tight enough to say "no recession priced," but the upward creep amid geopolitics and a hawkish Fed bears watching, especially into the June 17 SEP.

## Liquidity

- **TGA (Treasury General Account): ~$830.4B** as of 2026-06-09 (Daily Treasury Statement via IndexBox); ~$866.1B on 2026-06-02. Treasury had guided ~$900B end-Q2 target, so cash is running a touch below plan. Debt-ceiling pressure is off the table — the ceiling was lifted to $41.1T under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act.
- **ON RRP (overnight reverse repo): effectively drained / de minimis.** The facility ran down from its >$2.5T 2022 peak to near-empty (sub-$200B by late 2025) as money funds moved into T-bills; it is no longer a meaningful liquidity buffer. *(No clean dated 2026-06 daily figure confirmed; treat as "near-zero/structurally drained" rather than a precise number — earlier searches surfaced a stale 2021 $991B figure that is NOT current and was excluded.)*
- **Bank lending / reserves:** with RRP drained, reserves are the marginal liquidity cushion, and the TGCR-to-ON-RRP-rate spread has grown sensitive to TGA swings (NY Fed / Fed notes). No acute funding stress flagged this week; the inflation/Fed story is dominating over plumbing.

## Weekend Developments

**Iran-US peace MOU — the market-moving weekend event.** This is the swing factor for rates and oil into the FOMC:
- Friday 2026-06-12: Iran's Mehr News reported a draft **14-point US-Iran memorandum of understanding** — reopening the Strait of Hormuz, lifting oil sanctions, ending hostilities. Trump publicly disputed Iran's account of the terms and decried a new drone attack ("dishonorable people"), injecting uncertainty (CNBC, 2026-06-12).
- Weekend (Sun 2026-06-14): Pakistani PM Shehbaz Sharif said the US and Iran reached a peace deal including "immediate and permanent termination of military operations on all fronts, including in Lebanon," with **signing slated for 2026-06-19 in Switzerland**. Iran's Deputy FM Gharibabadi said the MOU text is finalized; Trump said he was ending the US naval blockade of the Strait (CBS News live updates; Al Jazeera; CNBC; Polymarket).
- **Rates/oil implication:** the de-escalation pulled oil and inflation-risk premium lower into the weekend, the proximate cause of Friday's yield dip. If the 2026-06-19 signing holds, it removes a key upside-inflation catalyst — modestly dovish for the rate path at the margin and supportive of a still-tight credit tape. A breakdown (Trump's pushback, Israeli strikes in Lebanon reportedly threatening the deal) would re-ignite the oil/inflation/hawkish-Fed loop. This tension sits right on top of Warsh's 2026-06-17 debut.

**No post-Friday Fed commentary** — blackout remains in force through 2026-06-18. No weekend Treasury announcements of note.

**Convergence reminder:** Per the market-structure calendar, this is a quadruple-catalyst window — FOMC (Jun 17) + Triple Witch / S&P rebalance (Jun 19) + Russell reconstitution (Jun 26) — so rate vol around the decision could be amplified by index/derivatives flows.
