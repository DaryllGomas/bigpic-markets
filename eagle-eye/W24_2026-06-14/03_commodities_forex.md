## TL;DR — The One Story That Matters

The entire commodities complex is trading off a single binary: **the US–Iran war (ongoing since Feb 28, 2026) is on the brink of ending.** This week, mediators announced that Washington and Tehran reached the text of a peace deal — a 60-day ceasefire, reopening of the Strait of Hormuz (blockaded ~3.5 months), and dismantling of Iran's nuclear program — with a signing ceremony scheduled for **Friday, June 19, 2026, in Switzerland**. That deal-optimism crushed oil for its biggest weekly drop of the year (WTI -8% to ~$85, Brent below $86.50) and pulled gold and silver lower as the war-risk premium bled out. The key weekend wrinkle: an **Israeli strike on a Hezbollah site in Beirut on Sunday, June 14**, now threatens the agreement — Trump publicly said it "should not have happened," and Iran warned it could imperil the deal. Energy markets reopen Sunday night facing genuine headline risk in both directions.

## Energy

### Crude oil — biggest weekly drop of 2026 on Iran-deal hopes
- **WTI (front-month):** settled **~$84.88/bbl Friday June 12, 2026**, down ~3.2% on the day and ~8% on the week, an eight-week low. As of the weekend (June 14), WTI is quoted **~$81.31/bbl** on continued deal optimism. [tradingeconomics, Reuters via tradingeconomics]
- **Brent:** fell more than 4% Friday to **below $86.50/bbl**, the lowest since early March 2026, down sharply on the week. [tradingeconomics]
- **Driver:** Traders priced out a worst-case Hormuz/Iran escalation as a US–Iran framework came together. Iran's Mehr News reported a 14-point draft including lifted oil sanctions and a commitment to reopen the Strait of Hormuz within 30 days. The 1H-2026 war had driven Brent toward ~$96+ in early June (during overnight Israel–Iran missile exchanges around the June 5 jobs report); the de-escalation this week unwound much of that premium. [CNBC, tradingkey, bullionvault]

### Strait of Hormuz — still the supply story (CONTEXT + LIVE)
- The conflict began **Feb 28, 2026**, when Israel and the US launched strikes on Iran (Supreme Leader Khamenei was killed); Iran responded by closing the Strait of Hormuz — through which ~20% of global oil and ~20% of LNG transit. [House of Commons Library; Britannica; Al Jazeera]
- Shipping has been near-frozen: WTO/industry data cited a ~95% reduction in crude tankers and ~99% reduction in LNG carriers through the strait. Middle East producers cut output **>11 million bbl/day in May vs. pre-conflict levels.** [EIA STEO June 2026; tradingeconomics]
- **This week:** Fresh US strikes (June 10–11) prompted Iran to re-announce a "complete" closure of the strait on **June 11**, even as the US said some ships were still transiting and peace talks advanced in parallel. EIA expects the strait effectively closed near-term with oil shipments resuming in Q3 2026. [Al Jazeera June 11; ms.now June 10; EIA]

### EIA inventories
- **Week ended June 6, 2026:** US commercial crude **-3.6 million bbl** to ~432.4M bbl. (A subsequent draw of ~1.3M bbl to ~424.4M bbl, ~4% below the five-year average, also appears in EIA highlights.) Gasoline built +4.8M bbl; distillates +1.7M bbl. [EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report; Seeking Alpha]

### Natural gas
- **Henry Hub:** **~$3.12/MMBtu on June 12, 2026** (+1.1% on the day), but pressured toward $3.00 during the week — a two-week low — after a larger-than-expected **+108 Bcf** storage build (vs. ~101 Bcf expected). [tradingeconomics; EIA]
- **Global divergence:** Henry Hub stays insulated, but the Hormuz LNG disruption keeps international benchmarks elevated (TTF and JKM well above US levels). EIA has lifted its natural gas price view, citing Hormuz disruptions stretching into 2027. [EIA Today in Energy; Natural Gas Intelligence]

### Freight / supply-chain dislocation (notable)
- **Tanker rates at record extremes:** The Baltic MEG–China VLCC index (TD3C) hit a record **~$423,736/day** (up ~94% in a session) during the crisis; VLCC Worldscale spiked toward 700 as insurers pulled war-risk cover. Charter rates surged ~77% on Hormuz tightness. [Lloyd's List; TT News; maritimenews]
- **Baltic Dry Index** (dry bulk, separate market) was softer, ~2,700s and sliding — a clean illustration of how the war fractured freight: tankers at all-time highs, dry bulk cooling. [tradingeconomics; breakbulk.news]

## Metals

### Precious metals — war premium bleeding out
- **Gold:** spot **~$4,216/oz on Friday June 12, 2026** (roughly flat on the day), down for a **second straight weekly decline.** The repricing started the prior Friday (June 5) when a hot May jobs report (NFP +172k vs. ~85k expected) sent gold to a two-month low (~$4,314) and spiked December Fed rate-hike odds; this week, fading war-risk on Iran-deal hopes kept bullion heavy. [Fortune; CNBC; USAGOLD; bullionvault]
- **Silver:** **~$67–67.8/oz June 12, 2026**, heading for a **fifth consecutive weekly decline** despite a sharp ~5.5% intraday pop Friday on Trump's "war is over" claim. Gold/silver ratio ~62. [Yahoo Finance; Fortune; tradingeconomics]
- **Read-through:** Both metals retain large YTD war/inflation premiums; the risk into next week is two-sided — a clean June 19 signing could pull them lower, while a collapse of the deal (see Weekend) would re-bid safe havens hard.

### Copper — global demand barometer
- **Copper** traded **below ~$6.20/lb (~$13,600/tonne) on June 12, 2026** — elevated all year on the structural deficit thesis but choppy on macro/rate fears. 2026 LME has ranged ~$12,500–13,000/tonne on average. Goldman expects copper to ease from record highs but stay historically high in 2026. [metalcharts; Goldman Sachs; tradingeconomics]
- **Thesis tie-in (critical minerals):** The 2026 structural copper deficit (ICSG ~150kt, JPM ~330kt) underpins **FCX** (Grasberg phased restart, Q2 2026) and **SCCO**. War-driven risk-off is the near-term headwind; the supply gap is the multi-year tailwind.

### Uranium / nuclear fuel
- **U3O8 spot:** **~$86.1/lb on June 12, 2026** (flat), holding above the Q1 trough (~$83.90 end-Q1) but below the Jan 29 YTD high of $101.41. Muted utility spot buying; term-contract premiums still building. [carboncredits; metalcharts; INN]
- **Context:** SPUT has been an aggressive structural buyer (>5M lbs YTD; >60M lbs held). Thesis names — **CCJ, UEC, DNN, UUUU, URA/SRUUF** — track the tight fundamentals (~30–40M lb annual deficit) more than spot, which has gone quiet during the geopolitical risk-off.

Ukrainian corn FOB ~$237/t, Australian wheat ~$282/t in early June; July soybeans slipped to ~$448/t. No fresh ag shock in the June 8–12 window. [USDA Feb 2026 outlook; Fastmarkets]

The unwinding of the Hormuz risk premium is disinflationary at the margin for the back half of 2026 **if** the deal holds — but the war has already pushed US CPI/PPI to multi-year highs (see Forex), the reason the Fed is now in play for a *hike*, not a cut.

### Dollar — softer on peace optimism, but rate-hike repricing is a floor
- **DXY:** **~99.81 on Friday June 12, 2026** (-0.05% on the day), held down by reduced safe-haven demand as Trump flagged a weekend Iran signing. [tradingeconomics; longforecast]

Following the war's oil-driven inflation surge plus the strong May jobs print, **fed funds futures now price a December 2026 HIKE (~51% odds), with cumulative tightening odds rising into early 2027** — a sharp reversal from the three *cuts* the market priced earlier in 2026. ## Weekend Developments

**This is the live, market-moving section for the Sunday-night open.**

1. **US–Iran peace deal text agreed (June 12).** Pakistan's PM Shehbaz Sharif (a mediator, alongside Qatar) announced the US and Iran reached a deal for "the immediate and permanent termination of military operations on all fronts." Trump: "The Deal with the Islamic Republic of Iran is now complete." Terms: 60-day ceasefire, reopening of the Strait of Hormuz / lifting of the US blockade, Iran accepting a ~15–20 year enrichment lockout and dismantling of nuclear sites, plus eased sanctions. **Formal signing set for Friday, June 19, 2026, in Switzerland** (officials also discussed a virtual signing). [CBS News live; NewsNation; CNN June 12–13]

2. **Israeli strike on Beirut threatens the deal (Sunday, June 14).** On Sunday morning, Israel struck a Hezbollah command/infrastructure site in Beirut's Dahieh suburb. **Trump said publicly: "This morning's attack on Beirut should not have happened,"** and Iran warned the strike could imperil negotiations. Iranian hardliners are also protesting FM Araghchi over the concessions. This is the key downside risk into the open. [CBS News live, June 14]

3. **Hormuz still effectively shut right now.** Despite the framework, shipping through the strait remains minimal; fresh US strikes (June 10–11) preceded the breakthrough, and Iran re-announced closure June 11. Physical reopening is a *future* event (deal commits to ~30 days / Q3 2026), not a done fact. [Al Jazeera June 11; EIA]

**Trading implication:** Oil gapped/drifted lower into the weekend (WTI ~$81) pricing a successful June 19 signing. The Beirut strike injects two-sided risk: a clean path to signing argues for further unwind of the war premium (bearish oil/gold), while any collapse of the ceasefire — with Hormuz still physically blocked — would violently re-bid crude and safe havens. Watch the headlines Sunday night.
