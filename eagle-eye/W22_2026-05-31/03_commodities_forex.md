# Commodities & Forex — Eagle Eye Weekly

*Agent 3 snapshot. Point-in-time as of Sunday, 2026-05-31 (Pacific). Covers the trading week ending Friday 2026-05-29 plus breaking weekend news through Sun 5/31.*

**Dominant macro overlay this week:** The ~3-month-old US–Israel–Iran war and the closure of the **Strait of Hormuz** remain the single biggest driver across the entire commodity/FX complex. The Strait — which normally carries ~one-fifth of global oil and LNG — has been **closed to most shipping for roughly three months**. The defining story of the week was the market pricing in a *prospective* de-escalation: the US and Iran reportedly "mostly agreed" on a 60-day MoU to extend the existing ceasefire (in place since April 8) and reopen Hormuz, but as of the weekend **Trump had not signed**, having sent the text back with tougher demands. This drove oil sharply lower and reset inflation expectations.

---

## Energy

**Crude oil — biggest monthly crash since 2020.** Oil fell again into Friday's close as traders priced in an eventual Hormuz reopening and Iranian barrels returning to market.

- **WTI:** closed **~$87.36/bbl Friday 5/29, -1.73% on the day** (CNBC/TradingEconomics, 2026-05-29). WTI ended May down roughly **-16%**.
- **Brent:** Friday quotes ranged by source and timing — TradingEconomics put the **futures close near $91–92.5/bbl** (down ~1.2% on the day to ~$92.56 per CNBC); Fortune's 9:02am ET spot quote was **$94.44/bbl** (Fortune, 2026-05-29). All sources agree Brent fell **~17–19% in May — its worst month since the Covid crash of 2020** — and is now down ~20% from its 2026 peak.
- **Driver:** Reports of a tentative US–Iran ceasefire-extension deal (60-day MoU) that would reopen Hormuz, lift the US naval blockade, clear Iranian mines, and issue sanctions waivers so Iran can sell oil freely. Analysts cautioned any supply recovery would be **gradual** — mines must be cleared, damaged infrastructure repaired, shut-in production restarted, and tanker delays unwound (CNBC/Axios, 2026-05-23 to 05-28).

**EIA inventories.** US crude stocks have drawn down sharply through May amid the supply disruption:
- Week ended **5/22**: crude **-3.327M bbl** to 441.7M (vs. ~4M draw expected); ~2% below the 5-yr average.
- Week ended 5/15: **-7.9M bbl** (plus a 9.9M bbl SPR draw).
- Week ended 5/8: -4.306M bbl. (EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report / Investing.com, May 2026.)

**OPEC+ — structural shock.** The **UAE formally exited OPEC and OPEC+ effective May 1, 2026** — a founding-era member (since 1967) leaving over a quota dispute (ADNOC's ~4.85M bpd capacity vs. a ~3.5M bpd quota). The UAE is now **unfettered by quotas** and can ramp output rapidly once Hormuz reopens, raising the risk of a future price war. OPEC+ approved only a **limited production increase** at its early-May meeting after the departure (Al Jazeera / WorldOil / Gulf News, Apr–May 2026).

**Natural gas.** Henry Hub firmed to **~$3.21/MMBtu (5/28), the highest since March 2026** (TradingEconomics). EIA storage injection of **+92 Bcf** for the week ended 5/22 (below the ~95–96 Bcf consensus and last year's +104), lifting inventories to **2.483 Tcf** — ~0.9% above year-ago and ~6.2% above the 5-yr average. EIA still sees Henry Hub averaging ~$3.50/MMBtu in 2026.

## Metals

**Gold — sharp 5/29 bounce, but down for the month.** Gold rebounded hard on Friday to **~$4,541–4,543/oz (+~0.9–1.0% on the day)** — its sharpest single-session recovery since early May — after April PCE landed in line with expectations, cooling fears of accelerated Fed tightening (Fortune / TradingEconomics, 2026-05-29). Despite the bounce, gold was on track for a **~-0.8% month**. Context: gold hit an all-time high of **~$5,602 in late January 2026**, spiked above **$5,300** when US/Israeli strikes on Iran began (~Feb 28, 2026), then fell **>10% from the war's onset** as oil-driven inflation and higher-for-longer rate expectations weighed on it — a notable case of safe-haven demand *fading* despite an active war (Euronews / Discovery Alert, Mar–May 2026).

**Silver.** Closed **~$76.43/oz (+0.51%) Friday 5/29**, on track for a **>3% monthly gain** — outperforming gold (Fortune / TradingEconomics, 2026-05-29).

**Copper — second straight monthly advance.** Copper held **~$6.40/lb Friday**, up on the month, underpinned by **AI/data-center demand optimism** and supply constraints (top producer **Chile** scaling back refiner capacity/output). The tentative Iran peace deal also eased supply-disruption fears (TradingEconomics, late May 2026). Relevant to the critical-minerals thesis (FCX, SCCO): the structural 2026 deficit narrative remains intact, with copper near record highs and AI buildout (1.1M→2.5M MT data-center demand by 2040) as the secular driver.

**Uranium.** Spot **U3O8 ~$85–86.5/lb** in late May, near a two-month high but off the early-year speculative peak (~$100/lb in Jan). Market focus has shifted back to muted utility spot buying (utilities largely on long-term contracts). Geopolitical energy volatility and AI/hyperscaler nuclear demand remain supportive — consistent with the nuclear thesis (CCJ, UEC, UUUU, SRUUF/URA) (TradingEconomics / uranium.info, May 2026).

## Commodities

**Critical minerals / lithium.** **Lithium carbonate (China) surged to a ~3-year high** — past **CNY 200,500/t on 5/13**, easing to **~CNY 191,000/t by 5/20** as the price spike prompted producers to restart mining. Drivers: strong Chinese NEV output/sales (+5.5%/+9.7% YoY) and Beijing's pledge to double national EV charging capacity to 180 GW by 2027. Supply-side tightening: **Zimbabwe imposed lithium-concentrate export quotas plus a full export ban starting next year** (processed lithium still exportable), aimed at forcing domestic processing (TradingEconomics / SMM, May 2026). This is a clear positive for the energy-storage and critical-minerals theses (ALB, SQM, PLS, LAC) — lithium has decisively exited the 2024–25 "lithium winter."

*Rare earths:* No fresh in-window China export-control headline surfaced this week. The thesis-level catalyst remains the **November 2026 expiry of the US–China trade agreement** (controls currently suspended). Treat as a forward catalyst, not a this-week event.

**Agriculture.** Grains stayed weak/below breakeven (fourth straight year of losses flagged): **July SRW wheat ~$6.27/bu** (fifth straight decline to a multi-week low before a small Friday bounce), **July corn ~$4.57/bu** (near lowest since mid-April), **July soybeans ~$11.95/bu** (lowest since 5/15). **Coffee fell to ~265.6 ¢/lb on 5/29 (-3.15% on the day)**, near its lowest since Nov 2024 on expectations of a bumper Brazilian harvest (Farm Futures / TradingEconomics, late May 2026).

**Supply chain / freight.** The **Baltic Dry Index gained ~20% over May** (around ~2,960–3,005 late in the month). Container markets remain distorted by Hormuz: transpacific rates ~**+$1,000/FEU vs. pre-war**, while Asia–Europe rates have mostly slipped back to pre-war levels. Businesses are **front-loading inventory** to insulate against the ~3-month energy crisis — an inflation/stockpiling signal worth watching (TradingEconomics / Freightos, May 2026).

Softer-than-expected April PCE eased fears that the energy shock would meaningfully worsen inflation, pressuring the dollar late in the week; news of the tentative US–Iran peace deal reinforced the move (TradingEconomics, 2026-05-29).

**Majors (levels as of 5/29):**
- **EUR/USD:** ~**1.1660** (prior close ~1.1650; day range 1.1624–1.1687).
- **USD/JPY:** ~**159.27** — yen remains weak near multi-decade-soft levels.
- **GBP/USD:** ~**1.3461**.
(Investing.com / FXStreet / TradingEconomics, 2026-05-29.)

The FX theme: a softer dollar into the weekend on cooling inflation fears and Iran-deal optimism, even as the dollar held a modest monthly gain from the earlier war-driven safe-haven bid.

## Weekend Developments (Sat 5/30 – Sun 5/31)

**The Iran/Hormuz deal remained unsigned and tense through the weekend — the key catalyst for Monday's open.**

- **Fri 5/29:** Trump ended a White House Situation Room meeting **without a "final determination,"** then **sent the deal text back with changes**, hardening demands: Iran "must agree" to never have a nuclear weapon, and Hormuz must be **"immediately open" to unrestricted shipping with no tolls** (CNBC / Fox News, 2026-05-29/30).
- **Sat 5/30:** An adviser to Iran's Supreme Leader said Trump, "by continuing the naval blockade and making excessive demands… has once again proven he is not inclined toward negotiation," signaling Tehran pushback (CNN live, 2026-05-29/30).
- **Defense posture:** Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth warned the **US military is ready to resume combat in the Gulf** if needed and is "more strongly placed" than at the war's start (CNN, 2026-05-29).
- **Sun 5/31:** Reporting indicates **Trump again returned the deal text with edits** (on enriched uranium and Hormuz), pushing talks into another week (CNN Politics, 2026-05-31). Three international economic organizations warned Friday that if oil shipping doesn't normalize, there are "increasing risks for fuel security, market conditions, and broader economic resilience" as summer demand builds.

**Net read for Monday:** Oil is positioned for two-sided risk. A signed deal → further downside (Iranian barrels + unconstrained UAE supply). A collapse/renewed strikes → a sharp Hormuz risk-premium snap-back. Brent has already round-tripped most of the war premium (down ~20% from 2026 peaks).

**Weekend proxy prices (electronic/24-7 markets, as of Sun 2026-05-31):**
- **Bitcoin: ~$73,400–73,800** (off the ~$75,800 high of 5/27; BTC/ETH ETFs shed ~$2B combined 5/20–5/29) — a soft risk-sentiment tell into month-end (Fortune / lines.com / finbold, 2026-05-30/31).
- Gold/silver/oil futures: Friday levels above are the last regular-session marks; no confirmed weekend OTC re-rating beyond the Iran headlines.
