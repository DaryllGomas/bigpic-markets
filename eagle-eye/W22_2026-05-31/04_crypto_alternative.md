# Crypto & Alternative — Eagle Eye W22

*Point-in-time snapshot as of Sunday, 2026-05-31 (Pacific). Window: trading week ending Fri 2026-05-29 + breaking news through now. Crypto trades 24/7 — current weekend prices labeled below.*

**One-line takeaway:** Risk-off week. US airstrikes on Iran near the Strait of Hormuz triggered ~$1B in liquidations and pushed BTC below $73K; spot Bitcoin ETFs bled ~$1.4B over a 10-session outflow streak (longest since 2024). BTC sits ~$73.8K this weekend on Iran-truce hopes, ETH languishes near the $2,000 line, and BTC dominance has climbed toward ~60% as alts underperform.

**Current price (as of Sun 2026-05-31):** ~**$73,800–$73,900** (BTC traded $73,856, +~0.5% on the day per market data on 5/31; Coinbase quoted $73,876 on 5/30–5/31). This is a modest stabilization after a sharp mid-week drop.

**Friday 2026-05-29 close/level:** ~**$73,100–$73,640**. Fortune logged $73,105.71 at 9:15am ET Friday; Phemex showed BTC/USDT at $73,642.90 by Friday with a 24h range of $72,561–$73,922. Both BTC and ETH "opened at their lowest levels all week" on Friday.

**Weekly action:** Net **down** on the week and locked in a tightening compression range below all major moving averages. BTC dropped below **$73,000** on 2026-05-28 after US airstrikes on Iran near the Strait of Hormuz (plus fresh sanctions) sparked a broad risk-asset sell-off and ~$1B in crypto liquidations. It briefly bounced back near **$74,000** on 5/29 on reports of a 60-day Iran truce extension on Trump's desk, then drifted to ~$72,500 intra-week before stabilizing. Monthly: down ~4.4% from ~$76,500 a month ago. BTC is ~30% below its 2025-10-06 all-time high of $126,198.

**Key levels:**
- Resistance: ~$75,500 (7-day MA), then the $77,000–$79,500 psychological zone that has repeatedly capped rallies.
- Support: $72,500 (recent intra-week low); deeper support cited at $62,872 (52-week close low).
- Momentum is oversold (MFI ~28, lowest in ~4 months).

**BTC dominance:** Elevated and rising — most trackers put **BTC.D in the high-50s to ~60%** (TradingView Hub cited 57.3% mid-month; other data showed dominance edging to ~60.2% by week-end). Rising dominance reflects capital rotating out of altcoins into BTC. BTC market cap ~$1.33T.

## Regulation & Institutional

- **GENIUS Act implementation (stablecoins):** Treasury launched its first GENIUS Act rulemaking (87-page proposal); additional issuer rules (licensing, capital, custody, AML) are due **2026-07-18**. The framework (100% liquid reserves, monthly disclosures) is credited with re-pricing Bitcoin's "monetary premium" (CoinDesk, 5/27/2026).
- **CLARITY Act** advancing — would classify Bitcoin as a commodity, removing the regulatory limbo that currently complicates pension/endowment allocation and index inclusion.
- **Bitcoin perpetual futures approved in the US for the first time** (per weekly recap, week of 5/30) — a notable market-structure milestone.
- **SEC approved Paxos as the first blockchain-based clearing agency** in the US — a structural win for tokenized settlement.
- **DTCC** announced plans to integrate Stellar (XLM) with its tokenized-securities platform (drove the XLM rally).
- **Institutional positioning (Q1 2026 13Fs):** **Goldman Sachs fully liquidated its XRP and Solana ETF positions** while keeping its Bitcoin ETF allocation largely intact (~$700M) — a "flight to BTC quality" within crypto.
- **Sanctions/enforcement:** The US Treasury seized nearly **$1B in Iran-linked crypto assets** amid the conflict.
- Friction: Ripple vs. Sen. Elizabeth Warren over OCC Trust Charters; crypto 2026 election spending topped $500M.

## Weekend Developments (if any)

- **US–Iran conflict drove the tape (5/28–5/31):** US airstrikes near the Strait of Hormuz + new sanctions on 2026-05-28 sent BTC below $73K and triggered ~$1B in liquidations. By 5/29, reports of a **60-day truce extension** awaiting Trump's signature (and talk of the Strait reopening) sparked a partial relief bounce toward $74K. Heading into the weekend the market stayed cautious — risk of re-escalation vs. truce hopes — with BTC stabilizing ~$73.8K. (Strait carries ~20% of global petroleum; a reopening would ease the oil/inflation premium on risk assets.)
- **Macro backdrop:** US equities ironically hit ATHs on de-escalation hopes; but the **30-yr Treasury yield hit ~5.19% (highest since 2007)** and the 10-yr ~4.6%, raising the opportunity cost of non-yielding BTC. Markets await Core CPI/PCE for Fed rate-cut signals.
- **Stablecoin hack — StablR (weekend of 5/24–5/26):** European issuer StablR's 1-of-3 minting multisig was compromised; attacker minted ~8.35M USDR + 4.5M EURR (~$13.5M unbacked) and netted ~$2.8M. Root cause was key-management failure, not smart-contract code. A reminder that "digital dollars" can still break — though contained and not a major-stablecoin de-peg.
- **Network reliability:** Sui suffered multiple outages within 24 hours; Base shipped its Azul mainnet upgrade; Sei announced a "Giga" roadmap (200k TPS target); Bittensor activated Dynamic TAO (5/25).
- **No major BTC/ETH or large-cap stablecoin de-peg over the weekend** beyond the contained StablR incident as of this snapshot.
