## Weekend Developments

Mostly quiet on price (futures flat), but several market-moving storylines carried over the weekend:

- **US-Iran 60-day ceasefire MOU (the week's biggest macro catalyst).** US officials told CNBC (Thu 5/28) that negotiators reached a 60-day memorandum of understanding to extend the Iran ceasefire and open talks on Iran's nuclear program; President Trump still had to sign off. This drove **Brent crude down ~19% in May** (worst month since the Covid crash) to **~$93.71** Friday, and **WTI to ~$87.18** (-16.5% MTD), as the market priced in reopened Strait of Hormuz shipping. Falling oil was a key tailwind for the broad equity rally but a headwind for energy producers' forward earnings. Watch for confirmation/signing over the window.
- **AI mega-cap IPO pipeline heating up (CNBC, Sun 5/31).** SpaceX is reportedly in "pole position" to debut **within ~2 weeks** — the only one of the three to have publicly released an S-1. OpenAI is reported to be working on a confidential filing; Anthropic could come public as soon as Q4. This is directly relevant to the Space and AI theses below.
- **Anthropic ~$1T valuation (CNBC, 5/28).** Anthropic announced a **$65B Series H at a ~$965B valuation** (led by Altimeter, Dragoneer, Greenoaks, Sequoia), surpassing OpenAI as the most valuable AI startup, with a reported **$47B revenue run-rate** (up from ~$30B earlier in 2026). Reinforces the AI software/model-layer thesis and private-market premium.
- **Week ahead:** Friday's nonfarm payrolls report is the marquee event for Fed-policy read-through.

No new geopolitical shock, M&A, or guidance bombshell emerged Saturday 5/30–Sunday 5/31 beyond the above.
