## This Week's Take

**The biggest story isn't last week's record highs — it's the Iran deal that didn't get signed.** US stocks closed May at all-time records on the back of a powerful, AI-led rally and a collapse in oil, both driven by the market pricing in an end to the ~3-month US–Israel–Iran war and a reopening of the Strait of Hormuz. A 60-day ceasefire memorandum of understanding was "mostly agreed," crude fell ~17–19% on the month (Brent's worst month since the 2020 Covid crash), and the inflation scare that had gripped markets in mid-May began to drain out. But **the deal sits unsigned heading into Monday.** Over the weekend President Trump twice returned the text with hardened demands — Iran must "never" have a nuclear weapon and Hormuz must reopen "immediately" with no tolls — Iran's side pushed back publicly, and Defense Secretary Hegseth warned the US military is "ready to resume combat" in the Gulf. After a real US airstrike on an Iranian site near Hormuz on 5/28 (which briefly knocked Bitcoin below $73K and triggered ~$959M in liquidations), Monday opens on a knife-edge: **a signed deal sends oil lower and extends the risk-on tape; a collapse snaps the Hormuz risk premium back into crude and reignites the inflation fight.**

**Underneath the geopolitics, the equity setup is constructive but stretched.** The S&P 500 closed at a record **7,580.06**, its **ninth straight weekly gain** (longest since 2023); the Dow cleared **51,000** for the first time; the Nasdaq capped an **+8% May**. The engine was AI infrastructure: **Dell** posted its best day ever (+34%) on **$16.1B of AI-server revenue** and a **$51.3B AI backlog**, validating the buildout, while the private-market AI IPO pipeline (SpaceX, OpenAI, Anthropic — the latter raising near a **$1T valuation**) heated up. But breadth is only moderate (~59% of the S&P above its 200-day), the index RSI is overbought (~73.6), and VIX at **15.3** leaves little cushion. This is a tape priced for good news into a payrolls week.

**Rates are the quiet regime change.** Treasuries rallied (10Y **4.45%**, down ~11 bps on the week) as oil fell and April core PCE cooled on a monthly basis — but the *annual* PCE still printed **3.8% headline / 3.3% core**, the hottest in years. New Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh** (sworn in May 22) inherits that backdrop into his first FOMC on June 16–17, with markets having **priced out essentially all 2026 cuts** and a minority now flagging a *hike* tail — a view reinforced by strikingly hawkish April FOMC minutes (four dissents, the most since 1992). Credit shrugged everything off (IG OAS ~73 bps, HY ~272 bps — historically rich). The week ahead is all about **labor**: JOLTS, ADP, and Friday's **jobs report** are the only major catalysts, with no fresh inflation print to reset the 3.8% PCE before the Fed meets.
