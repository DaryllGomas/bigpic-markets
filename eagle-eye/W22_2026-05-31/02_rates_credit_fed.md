# Rates, Credit & Fed — Eagle Eye W22

*Point-in-time snapshot as of Sunday, 2026-05-31 (Pacific). Report window: trading week ending Friday 2026-05-29, plus weekend breaking news through Sun 2026-05-31.*

**One-line take:** A textbook bull rally in Treasuries — yields fell 9–15 bps across the curve as the mid-May twin shocks (hot April CPI + a US–Iran conflict/oil spike) reversed on Iran de-escalation reports and a softer month-over-month April PCE print. Credit shrugged off the volatility and re-tightened to historically rich levels. The overhang: new Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh** (sworn in May 13) takes the gavel into a 3.8% headline-inflation backdrop, with markets pricing essentially no cuts in 2026 and a non-trivial hike tail.

---

## Treasury Yields

The week ending **Friday 2026-05-29** was a broad rally (yields down). Markets were closed Monday 5/25 for Memorial Day, so the trading week ran Tue 5/26 – Fri 5/29. The move unwound part of the mid-May selloff that had pushed the 10Y to a ~16-month high near 4.7% on May 20 after a hot April CPI (3.8% headline YoY) and a US–Iran flare-up spiked oil.

| Tenor | Fri 5/29 close | Prior Fri 5/22 | Weekly change |
|-------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| 2Y | ~3.98% | 4.13% | **−15 bps** |
| 5Y | ~4.15% | 4.27% | **−12 bps** |
| 10Y | ~4.45% | 4.56% | **−11 bps** |
| 30Y | ~4.98% | 5.07% | **−9 bps** |

*Levels are FRED H.15 constant-maturity series. As of this writing FRED had posted the 2Y at 3.98% (5/29) and 5Y/10Y/30Y through 5/28 (4.15 / 4.45 / 4.98); intraday Friday prints had the 10Y ticking as low as ~4.44%, its lowest in more than two weeks. Treating 5/28–5/29 as the Friday close given the holiday-shortened week.*

**Drivers (in-window):**
- **Iran de-escalation** was the dominant catalyst. Returning from the Memorial Day break, Treasuries caught up to a sharp drop in oil (Brent −5.3% to ~$94, WTI −5.6% to ~$89 mid-week) as President Trump signaled peace negotiations were "proceeding nicely." The 10Y fell more than 8 bps on Tuesday 5/26 alone.
- **April PCE (released Thursday 5/28, in-window)** reinforced the rally on the margin: headline PCE +0.4% m/m (below the +0.5% consensus) and core PCE +0.2% m/m (below +0.3%), even though the *annual* readings stayed hot — headline **3.8% YoY** (highest since May 2023) and core **3.3% YoY** (highest since Oct 2023). The softer monthly pace gave bulls cover.

The short end led the rally (2Y −15 vs 30Y −9), consistent with the move being driven by an easing of the energy-inflation scare rather than a growth-fear bid into duration.

## Fed Watch

**The structural story is leadership.** **Kevin Warsh was confirmed as Fed Chair by the Senate 54–45 on May 13, 2026 and sworn in the same week.** His **first FOMC meeting is June 16–17, 2026** (a SEP/dot-plot meeting). Warsh is broadly read as hawkish: he has argued inflation "victory" requires a shift in public *perception*, not just hitting 2%, implying rates may stay higher for longer; he has said he wants "messier" meetings with "a good family fight." A 24/7 Wall St. piece dated **5/31/2026** ("18 words from Warsh that could keep rates higher for years") underscores the in-window market focus on his framework.

**Rate pricing (as of late May 2026):**
- **June 16–17 FOMC:** CME FedWatch ~**70% hold** / ~28% for a 25 bp cut / ~2% hike. Prediction markets lean even more firmly toward a hold. The Fed funds target is **3.50–3.75%** (effective 3.62%).
- **Through year-end 2026:** Markets have priced **out** the easing that was assumed earlier in the year. The CALENDAR baseline of "3 cuts (75 bps) by year-end" (Feb 2026 view) is now stale — after hot April CPI/PPI and 3.8% PCE, futures reflect roughly *no cuts* for 2026, with some sources flagging a rising (still minority) probability of an inflation-fighting **rate hike**. Terminal-rate expectations have drifted up; the market is no longer confident the next move is a cut.

**FOMC minutes (April 28–29 meeting):** Released **May 20, 2026** — *just outside* this report window (and therefore not a fresh catalyst for the 5/23–5/31 period), but highly relevant context. The minutes were strikingly hawkish: the April meeting carried **four dissents — the most since 1992** — and a *majority* of officials saw **rate increases as likely necessary if the Iran war continued to aggravate inflation**. Dallas Fed's Lorie Logan dissented against including any easing bias. The Fed's own April projections nudged 2026 inflation up to ~2.7%.

**Fed speakers in-window:** No major market-moving on-the-record Fed speeches were confirmed inside the 5/26–5/29 trading window (Powell, Kashkari, Hammack, Barr, Bessent, Logan, Barkin, Daly appearances were largely on the calendar for the week *ahead*). The dominant Fed narrative this window was Warsh's incoming hawkish framework and the hawkish April minutes, not new speeches.

## Credit Markets

Credit was a non-event in the best sense — spreads barely flinched during the rate volatility and **re-tightened back to historically rich levels** by week's end.

| Index | Fri ~5/28 | Prior Fri 5/22 | Weekly change |
|-------|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| IG OAS (ICE BofA US Corp) | **~73 bps** | 74 bps | **−1 bp (tighter)** |
| BBB OAS | **~93 bps** | 93 bps | flat |
| HY OAS (ICE BofA US HY) | **~272 bps** | 274 bps | **−2 bps (tighter)** |

*Source: FRED BAMLC0A0CM, BAMLC0A4CBBB, BAMLH0A0HYM2 (values through 5/28).*

- **IG OAS ~73 bps** is exceptionally tight — near the tightest sustained levels since the pre-financial-crisis era. The broad IG index has compressed from ~95 bps at end-2025 to the low-70s by late May 2026.
- **HY OAS ~272 bps** sits well below its ~490 bp 20-year average. During the mid-May CPI/Iran scare, HY OAS only widened to ~286 bps (5/19) before re-tightening — a remarkably contained reaction that signals strong risk appetite and no funding stress.
- **Issuance/supply:** 2026 has seen heavy, well-absorbed IG supply (Q1 IG issuance ~$620B, the largest quarter since Q2 2020), with the "up-in-quality" bid intact. No notable credit-event/stress headlines surfaced in-window.

**Takeaway:** Credit is pricing benign fundamentals and ample demand. The risk is asymmetric — at sub-75 bp IG / sub-275 bp HY, spreads have very little cushion if the Warsh-era hawkishness or a renewed Iran escalation reprices risk.

## Liquidity

- **ON RRP balance: effectively drained.** The Fed's overnight reverse repo facility printed in a **~$1–13B daily range** through late May (e.g., $11.7B on 5/29, ~$1.2B on 5/28), down from the trillions of 2022–23. This is structurally important: the cash buffer that cushioned QT has been exhausted, so further reserve drains now bite directly into bank reserves — a key plumbing watch-item for funding stress and the eventual end of balance-sheet runoff.
- **TGA (Treasury General Account): ~$830B** as of the week of 5/27 (FRED WTREGEN $830.3B), up from ~$781B the prior week and broadly in the $750B–$1,006B band seen since the April tax season. A rebuilding/elevated TGA drains liquidity from the system, reinforcing the tight-plumbing backdrop alongside the empty RRP.
- **Effective fed funds: 3.62%**, holding mid-range within the 3.50–3.75% target — no signs of money-market dysfunction despite the drained RRP.
- **Bank lending (SLOOS):** No new Senior Loan Officer survey was released in-window (the next quarterly SLOOS is not due this period); credit-spread behavior and heavy primary issuance suggest lending conditions remain accommodative, not stressed.

## Weekend Developments

**Iran deal — the live tail risk (Sat 5/30):** President Trump held a **~two-hour meeting with aides on 5/30/2026 to decide whether to move forward with an Iran deal but announced no decision.** US and Iranian negotiators had approved a draft peace framework on 5/23, and the two sides are reported to have largely agreed on a **60-day memorandum of understanding to extend the ceasefire**, pending Trump's sign-off. This is the single biggest swing factor for rates and oil into the new week:
- **Bullish-for-bonds scenario:** A signed ceasefire MOU caps the oil/inflation premium, validating this week's rally and the soft-PCE narrative.
- **Bearish-for-bonds scenario:** Talks collapse / ceasefire expires without agreement → oil re-spikes → the inflation scare and "Fed on hold or hiking" thesis intensifies, pressuring the front end and re-steepening fears.

**Warsh framework (Sun 5/31):** Continued weekend commentary on the new Chair's hawkish "perception-of-inflation" framework (e.g., 24/7 Wall St., 5/31) kept the higher-for-longer narrative front-of-mind heading into his first June meeting.

**Sovereign ratings:** **No new US sovereign rating action in-window.** For the record, the most recent major action is historical: **Moody's stripped the US of its last AAA in May 2025 (to Aa1, now stable outlook)**; S&P (AA+) and Fitch (AA+) both already below top-tier with stable outlooks. Nothing fresh from any agency in May 2026.

**Cross-asset read-through to financing-sensitive sectors (thesis tie-in):** The pullback in long yields (30Y back under 5%) is a marginal positive for the capital-intensive, financing-sensitive names across the BigPic theses — nuclear/SMR developers (OKLO, SMR, CEG/VST/TLN project finance), grid-scale storage (FLNC, EOSE), and pre-revenue space (RKLB, LUNR). But with the market now pricing ~zero 2026 cuts and a hawkish Warsh Fed, the "higher-for-longer" discount-rate headwind for long-duration growth equities is intact; this week's rally is a relief, not a regime change.
