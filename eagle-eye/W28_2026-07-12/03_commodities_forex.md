# Commodities & Forex — Eagle Eye Weekly

**Report section:** 03 — Commodities & Forex (Agent 3)
**Point-in-time snapshot:** Sunday, 2026-07-12
**Covers:** Trading week ending Friday 2026-07-10, plus breaking weekend developments through Sunday 2026-07-12.

> **Headline:** The dominant driver across every commodity complex this week and this weekend is the re-escalation of the **2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis**. A three-month US–Iran ceasefire collapsed on July 8, 2026, and over the weekend of July 11–12 the conflict flared into open exchanges of strikes, with Iran's IRGC declaring the Strait closed after attacking a container ship and the US retaliating against ~140 Iranian targets. Oil finished the week up ~4–5%, gold and silver rallied on safe-haven/inflation demand, and crude-tanker freight rates hit historic extremes. See **Weekend Developments** below, which is the most important part of this report.

## Energy

### Crude oil (WTI & Brent)

|---|---|---|
| WTI (front-month) | **~$71.41/bbl** | **+~4%** |

- Oil rose for the week on a **geopolitical risk premium** as the US–Iran ceasefire collapsed and Strait of Hormuz tanker traffic was disrupted. Brent **briefly topped $80/bbl intraday on July 8, 2026** when President Trump declared the ceasefire "over" at a NATO summit in Turkey following fresh Iranian attacks on shipping; WTI touched ~$75 that day. ([S&P Global, 2026-07-08](https://www.spglobal.com/energy/en/news-research/latest-news/crude-oil/070826-factbox-oil-futures-pass-80b-as-us-iran-ceasefire-appears-to-collapse); [CNBC, 2026-07-08](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/08/oil-prices-brent-wti-iran-us-hormuz.html))
- **Friday July 10 was a down day** (Brent ~$76.80 intraday early ET, down ~$2.45 on the session) as prices pared the week's spike, but both benchmarks still closed the week solidly higher. ([Fortune, 2026-07-10](https://fortune.com/article/price-of-oil-07-10-2026/); [ts2.tech Oil Week, 2026-07-10/12](https://ts2.tech/en/oil-week-diesel-premium-nears-80-brent-sees-fresh-monday-gap/))
- **OPEC+ (supply offset):** On **July 5, 2026**, seven OPEC+ members agreed to accelerate the unwind of voluntary cuts by a combined **188,000 bbl/day from August 2026** — the fifth consecutive monthly increase since April 2026. This added supply is the main bearish counterweight to the war premium. ([Vantage Markets, week of 2026-07-06 to 07-10](https://www.vantagemarkets.com/market-analysis/brent-crude-oil-price-wti-opec-output-usousd-usousd-july-6-10-2026/))
- **EIA inventories:** For the week ending **July 3, 2026** (released July 8), US commercial crude stocks **rose +2.998 million barrels to 411.3 million barrels** — the **first build after ten consecutive weekly draws**, and against expectations for a ~2.4M-bbl draw. Inventories remain ~6% below the five-year average. ([EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report](https://www.eia.gov/petroleum/supply/weekly/))
- **Historical context (NOT this week):** The broader crisis began **February 28, 2026** (US/Israel "Operation Epic Fury" strikes on Iran; Iran blockaded Hormuz). Brent passed $100 for the first time in four years on **March 8, 2026** and peaked near **$126/bbl in March 2026** — the largest-ever monthly increase and described as the largest disruption in the history of the world oil market. Current ~$71–76 prices are far below that March peak. ([Wikipedia: 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis))

## Weekend Developments (breaking — most market-relevant)

**The Strait of Hormuz crisis re-escalated sharply over the weekend of July 11–12, 2026.** This is the biggest single risk into Monday's open.

- **Fri July 10:** US–Iran ceasefire (declared "over" July 8) leaves the region on a hair-trigger; oil closes the week up ~4–5%.
- **Sat July 11:** Iran's **IRGC struck the Cyprus-flagged container ship *GFS Galaxy*** transiting the Strait — setting it ablaze, with ~23 crew stranded and **one crew member missing**. The IRGC **declared the Strait of Hormuz closed** to maritime traffic. ([Al Jazeera, 2026-07-10](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/10/strait-of-hormuz-shipping-grinds-to-halt-as-us-iran-resume-fighting); [Time, 2026-07-12](https://time.com/article/2026/07/12/what-is-the-status-of-the-strait-of-hormuz-/))
- **Overnight into Sun July 12:** **US CENTCOM launched a major missile assault, striking ~140 Iranian sites** — missile and drone sites, naval/fast-attack-boat capabilities, ammunition storage, communications networks, coastal surveillance and air-defense (including targets on Qeshm Island). ([Fox News live, 2026-07-12](https://www.foxnews.com/live-news/us-iran-war-irgc-centcom-strait-hormuz-july-12))
- **Disputed status (as of Sun July 12):** The US and Iran are **publicly contradicting each other** on whether the Strait is open. CENTCOM and President Trump (Sunday NBC *Meet the Press*) insist it is **open** to lawful transit; Iran's port authority (PGSA) says **"passage is currently not possible."** ([Time, 2026-07-12](https://time.com/article/2026/07/12/what-is-the-status-of-the-strait-of-hormuz-/))
- **Shipping reality:** **No large vessel has crossed the Strait via the US-coordinated route while broadcasting its position since Tuesday, July 7**; traceable transits have "effectively ground to a halt." Al Jazeera frames the disruption as compounding "the biggest supply disruption in history." ([Al Jazeera, 2026-07-10](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/10/strait-of-hormuz-shipping-grinds-to-halt-as-us-iran-resume-fighting))

**Weekend price/positioning read:**
- Oil futures are set for a **volatile Monday open (July 13)** — traders will react first to the strike-and-shipping news. With Brent having closed Friday ~$76 and having already shown it can trade to $80–$126 on Hormuz disruption, a gap-up risk premium is likely if the Strait stays shut; conversely, any renewed talks or resumption of tanker traffic could bleed the premium out quickly. ([ts2.tech Oil Week](https://ts2.tech/en/oil-week-diesel-premium-nears-80-brent-sees-fresh-monday-gap/))
- *Caveat / unconfirmed:* One outlet ran a headline suggesting a US–Iran "deal to reopen" the Strait, but this is **contradicted by the weight of weekend reporting** (Al Jazeera, Fox, Time) showing active strikes and a shut/contested Strait. Treat any "reopening deal" as **unconfirmed** as of Sunday 2026-07-12.
- Physical **gold and silver** (which trade nearly around the clock and reopen Sunday evening ET) are biased higher on the escalation given Friday's strong safe-haven/inflation-driven close; **crude-tanker rates** are likely to extend their record run if Hormuz transits remain halted. No verified Sunday-evening electronic settle was available at the time of writing — figures above are the confirmed Friday 2026-07-10 closes unless labeled otherwise.
