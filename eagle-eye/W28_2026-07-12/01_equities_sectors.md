The Fed has turned hawkish: its June 17 SEP raised the 2026 median inflation forecast to 3.6% (from 2.7%) and nudged the median fed funds projection up to 3.8% (from 3.4%). Markets are now debating whether the **next Fed move is a hike** — CME FedWatch-implied odds of a September rate hike were cited around **61%**. This is a notable regime shift versus the start of the year (when multiple 2026 cuts were priced).

**Breadth was the week's cautionary flag.** With **NVDA, META, and AVGO accounting for nearly all of the S&P 500's weekly gain**, leadership was extremely narrow. Supporting data:
- **% of US stocks above their 50/150/200-day moving averages (combined) ≈53.12%** in early July, **−0.40% on the week** — holding above the 50% line but consolidating, not broadening. (streetstats/MacroMicro-type breadth aggregates.)
- A widely cited nuance: an index tracking the **Magnificent Seven is up only ~3.2% YTD** — i.e., several mega-caps (AAPL/TSLA/GOOGL, etc.) have lagged badly, and 2026's broad strength earlier came from small/mid-caps (Russell 2000's best H1 since 1991). **Last week reversed that**: mega-cap tech led (Mag-7 proxy +4.9%) while small caps and materials lagged. The market is leaning back on a handful of names — a fragility Bloomberg flagged as stocks "priced for sunshine and rainbows" heading into the earnings test.

- **CNN Fear & Greed Index jumped to 49 (neutral) from 32** a week earlier — improving but not euphoric.
- **Put/call ratio: could not confirm a specific 7/10 reading** — flagged as unavailable.

Net: bullish price action, complacent vol, but thin breadth + rising real-rate pressure = a market vulnerable to disappointment on next week's CPI (7/14) and bank earnings.
