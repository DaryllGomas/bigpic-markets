## Yield Curve

The curve is **modestly positively sloped and bear-steepening** — long yields rising as much or more than the front end in a high-inflation, hold-or-hike environment. This is explicitly *not* the bull steepener that would signal a Fed pivot; it reflects the market pricing longer-term inflation persistence.

- **2s10s:** approximately **+35 bps** at Friday's close (10Y 4.56% − 2Y 4.21%). It printed **+38 bps** on Thu Jul 9 (10Y 4.54% − 2Y 4.16%), flattening a touch into Friday as the 2Y backed up. Slope is modestly steeper than a few weeks ago. [primerates, Jul 9; the-weekly-investor, Jul 2026]
- **3m10y:** roughly **+71 to +73 bps** and firmly **positive/disinverted** (10Y ~4.54–4.56% vs 3M ~3.83%). [primerates, Jul 9 2026]
- **Characterization:** Bear steepener. "A steepening curve in a high-inflation, hold-or-hike environment signals the market is pricing longer-term inflation persistence — not a growth recovery." The distinction matters: no recession/pivot signal here, just term-premium and inflation risk being rebuilt into the long end. [fractionalx, Jul 2026]

## Fed Watch

**Chair confirmation:** The Fed is chaired by **Kevin Warsh** (17th Chair). He was confirmed by the Senate **54–45 on 2026-05-13** (most divisive Fed vote on record) and took the oath of office on **2026-05-22** for a four-year term ending 2030-05-21. He was a Fed Governor 2006–2011. [Federal Reserve bio; Fed press release, May 22 2026]

**June FOMC (Jun 16–17, decision Jun 17 2026) — a hawkish hold:**
- FOMC **unanimously held** the target range at **3.50%–3.75%**. [CNBC, Jun 17 2026; Fox Business, Jun 17 2026]
- The statement **dropped its easing-bias language** and was cut to about one-third its usual length, consistent with Warsh's stated preference that the Fed communicate less about future intentions. [Chatham Financial FOMC recap, Jun 2026]
- **Dot plot turned hawkish:** median end-2026 fed funds projection rose to **3.8%** (from 3.4% in March), implying at least one **hike** this year. Dots split roughly 8 no-change / 1 cut / **9 hike**. **Warsh withheld his own dot.** [Yahoo/Bloomberg "9 Fed officials signal 2026 hike"; bondsavvy dot-plot analysis, Jun 2026]

**June FOMC minutes (released Wed 2026-07-08, 2:00 pm ET):**
- Confirmed a committee split roughly **9–9** on whether a hike is appropriate by year-end; "a few" participants saw a case for hiking already in June. Inflation risks described as still tilted to the upside. [CNBC, Jul 8 2026; Bloomberg, Jul 8 2026]
- Because Warsh withheld his projection, the minutes are the committee's only on-record read on a possible September hike — giving them unusual market weight. [goldsilver / techtimes, Jul 7–8 2026]

**Fed speakers in-window (week of Jul 6–10 2026):**
- **Chair Warsh (Sintra/ECB forum, 2026-07-01):** declined to hint at the July decision but said inflation is "too high" — "we've all looked around, and we've seen that prices are too high"; the Fed "will not be comfortable with inflation above 2%." Emphasized price stability, notably omitting the usual maximum-employment reference. [CNBC, Jul 1 2026; Yahoo Finance, Jul 1 2026]
- **Gov. Waller** spoke Monday (policy panel, Rome); **Williams and Logan** were scheduled Thursday Jul 9 — providing a "live" read into the committee's thinking. [ZeroHedge "Key Events This Week," Jul 2026]

**Fed funds futures pricing (as of ~Jul 7–10 2026):**
- **July FOMC (Jul 28–29 2026):** hold heavily favored — CME FedWatch ~**79.5%** hold; prediction markets (Polymarket/Kalshi) >**85%** hold. Cut odds are negligible. [CME FedWatch; Kalshi/Polymarket, Jul 2026]
- **September FOMC:** a **hike** is roughly a coin flip — **~50–55%** odds of a 25 bp increase, down from ~66% before the June jobs miss. [Forbes/Simon Moore, Jul 7 2026]
- For 2026 overall, essentially **zero cuts priced**; the tail is toward tightening, not easing. [cryptobriefing; CME FedWatch, Jul 2026]

**Warsh institutional reform (context):** Warsh has stood up five task forces (communications, balance-sheet policy, data, productivity/jobs, inflation) — a "quiet revolution" in how the Fed sets and explains policy, with longer-term implications for how rates markets read Fed signals. [CNBC, Jun 21 2026; watrust, Jul 10 2026]

**Week ahead (Jul 14–15 2026) — the next catalyst:**
- **June CPI: Tue 2026-07-14, 8:30 am ET.** [Finance Calendar / BLS]
- **June PPI: Wed 2026-07-15, 8:30 am ET.** [BLS PPI schedule]
- **Warsh semiannual testimony:** **House Financial Services on 2026-07-14** and **Senate Banking on 2026-07-15** — his debut Humphrey-Hawkins appearances. The House hearing lands ~90 minutes after CPI, making inflation unavoidable in Q&A. [cryptobriefing, Jul 2026; congress.net, Jul 2026]
- Caveat on CPI: the **June** report covers a month in which front-month WTI fell ~20% (pre-escalation), so the print may show *moderation* even as the *current* July oil spike argues the opposite for forward inflation — a setup for a hawkish market reaction if CPI surprises high. [Finance Calendar, Jul 2026]

## Credit Markets

Spreads remain **historically tight** and showed little stress despite the rates selloff — credit is not corroborating any inflation/geopolitics fear priced in Treasuries.

- **High-yield OAS (ICE BofA US HY):** **~267 bps as of 2026-07-07**, ~270 bps around Jul 8 — near the tight end of the historical range. [Convex, Jul 7 2026; FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2]
- **Investment-grade OAS (ICE BofA US Corporate):** **~80 bps**, with BBB ~100 bps and AA ~50 bps (early-2026 reference levels; no material widening reported through Jul 10). [investmentgrade.com, 2026]
- **HY–IG differential:** roughly ~185–200 bps — compressed vs. history.
- The WA Trust week-in-review carried **no explicit spread-widening commentary**, and market reaction to the oil/geopolitics news was described as "measured" — consistent with no credit stress this week. [watrust, Jul 10 2026]
- **SLOOS:** No new Senior Loan Officer Opinion Survey was released in-window; the next SLOOS typically lands the week after the FOMC (i.e., early August, post the Jul 28–29 meeting). No bank-lending-condition update this week.

## Liquidity

Money-market plumbing is running with **near-zero RRP, elevated TGA, and still-ample but slowly declining reserves** (as of ~2026-07-08):

- **ON RRP (reverse repo):** **~$3B** — effectively drained. The RRP is no longer a meaningful liquidity buffer. [streetstats Fed balance sheet; FRED RRPONTSYD]
- **TGA (Treasury General Account):** **~$774B** — elevated, reflecting post-debt-ceiling Treasury cash rebuilding, which drains reserves from the banking system. [streetstats, Jul 8 2026; FRED WTREGEN]
- **Bank reserves:** **~$3,099B**. [streetstats, Jul 8 2026]
- **Trend:** Over the trailing year, net liquidity fell ~$165B and reserves declined ~$231B, as liability growth (TGA rebuild, currency) outpaced modest asset changes. With RRP near zero, further TGA builds now pull more directly on reserves — a slow tightening of the liquidity backdrop worth watching for funding-market stress. [streetstats, Jul 2026]

## Weekend Developments

The dominant driver into and through the weekend is a **renewed US–Iran military escalation and oil spike**, which is the transmission channel from geopolitics to rates via inflation expectations.

- **Ceasefire breakdown / strikes:** Treasury yields jumped on **2026-07-08** after President Trump indicated (at a NATO summit in Turkey) that the ceasefire with Iran had "effectively ended," with the US and Iran exchanging strikes. **Brent crossed ~$80/bbl** (WTI ~$76 mid-week); the 30-year climbed toward ~5.07% and the 2-year rose >5 bps to ~4.218% as investors priced a higher-for-longer / possibly-tighter Fed. [Yahoo Finance, Jul 2026; Business Recorder, Jul 2026; TradingEconomics, Jul 2026]
- **Market reaction stayed "measured":** the bond move was orderly — investors were reluctant to fully price sustained energy disruption, and credit spreads did not widen. The risk is asymmetric into next week: a hot June CPI (Jul 14) layered on top of the oil spike could force markets to lift September-hike odds further. [watrust, Jul 10 2026]
- No post-Friday FOMC decisions, emergency actions, or scheduled Fed speeches confirmed over the weekend of Jul 11–12; the next Fed events are Warsh's testimony beginning Jul 14. [as of 2026-07-12]
