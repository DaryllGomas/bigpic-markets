**Big Friday-of-prior-week regulatory development:** On April 13, 2026, the SEC issued a no-action statement that it would not object to **"Covered User Interface Providers"** -- technology firms that operate software interfaces enabling users to prepare and submit transactions in crypto asset securities -- without requiring broker-dealer registration. ## Weekend Developments (Apr 18-19)

### THE STORY: Kelp DAO -- $292M Hack -- Largest DeFi Exploit of 2026

**Saturday April 18 (late):** An attacker exploited Kelp DAO's **LayerZero-powered cross-chain bridge** to drain **116,500 rsETH (~$292 million, ~18% of circulating supply).** The attacker manipulated LayerZero's cross-chain messaging verification into believing a valid transfer instruction had arrived from another chain. rsETH (restaked ETH) is deployed across 20+ networks (Base, Arbitrum, Linea, Blast, Mantle, Scroll) via LayerZero's OFT standard.

**The contagion play (Saturday-Sunday):**
1. Attacker deposited stolen rsETH onto **Aave V3 as collateral**.
2. Borrowed **wrapped ether (WETH)** against it.
3. Aave's contracts were not technically compromised -- but the protocol was left holding **~$196M-$236M in bad debt** concentrated in the rsETH-WETH pair.
4. **Aave TVL plunged $6.6B** (from $26.4B Apr 18 → ~$20B Sun AM) on emergency withdrawals.
5. **AAVE token: -16% to -20% on the day.**
6. Aave V3/V4 froze rsETH markets within hours; LTV ratios set to zero. SparkLend and Fluid also froze rsETH markets.
7. Aave's Umbrella reserve **may not fully cover the deficit** -- **stkAAVE holders may absorb residual losses.**

**Community reaction:** CoinDesk's headline -- **"DeFi is dead"** -- captured the mood. The exploit exposes the **systemic risk of "modular" cross-chain security without minimum standards.** Bloomberg: *"Crypto Hack Worth $290 Million Triggers DeFi Contagion Shock."*
