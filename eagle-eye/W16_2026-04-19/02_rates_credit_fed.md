# Rates, Credit & Fed — Week 16 (Apr 13–17, 2026)

*Snapshot as of Sunday, April 19, 2026. Recap covers Mon Apr 13 – Fri Apr 17. Includes weekend developments through midday Sunday.*

The week was defined by a hawkish FOMC narrative crystallizing around the Iran war/oil shock, a Fed entering its pre-FOMC blackout (Apr 28–29 meeting), and a deepening political crisis around Powell's tenure and the Warsh confirmation timeline. Yields finished the week roughly unchanged at the long end and slightly lower at the front, with a modestly steeper curve. Credit spreads tightened on truce optimism even as Waller signaled caution on near-term cuts.

## Yield Curve

- **2s10s**: +50 bps on Apr 10 → +54 bps on Apr 17 (using H.15). Steepened ~4 bps on the week ([Fed H.15](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/), [FRED T10Y2Y](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/T10Y2Y)).
- **3M10Y**: +62 bps on Apr 10 → +62 bps on Apr 17. Essentially unchanged ([Fed H.15](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/)).
- **5s30s**: +97 bps → +102 bps. Steepening ~5 bps.

The 2s10s has been positive since the un-inversion, but it has "swung back and forth from positive to negative territory" since late February as the Iran shock has whipsawed the curve ([dshort Apr 17 snapshot](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/dshort/updates/2026/04/17/treasury-yields-snapshot-april-17-2026)). The dominant narrative remains a **bear steepener** — the long end rising faster than the short end on inflation and term-premium concerns rather than the front end falling on cuts ([FinancialContent Bond Market Review](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-4-14-bond-market-review-treasury-yields-hold-steady-as-yield-curve-steepens-amid-geopolitical-volatility)).

## Fed Watch

### FOMC pricing (CME FedWatch as of Apr 17)

- **April 28–29 meeting**: ~98% probability of a hold at 3.50–3.75% — essentially priced as a done deal ([CME FedWatch via search](https://www.cmegroup.com/markets/interest-rates/cme-fedwatch-tool.html)).
- **May**: hold ~83% priced ([same](https://www.cmegroup.com/markets/interest-rates/cme-fedwatch-tool.html)).
- **June 16–17 (SEP meeting)**: roughly a coin flip — ~48% probability of at least one 25 bp cut by June; remainder split between hold and (low-probability) hike ([Atlanta Fed Market Probability Tracker](https://www.atlantafed.org/cenfis/market-probability-tracker), [iShares Fed Outlook 2026](https://www.ishares.com/us/insights/fed-outlook-2026-interest-rate-forecast)).
- **July**: probability of at least one cut by July moves higher — most cut probability concentrated in Sep/Nov.
- **Year-end 2026**: ~55–65% expect one 25 bp cut to 3.25–3.50%; 30–40% expect no cuts at all; 2–5% probability of any 2026 hike ([iShares Fed Outlook 2026](https://www.ishares.com/us/insights/fed-outlook-2026-interest-rate-forecast)).

### Fed speakers — week of Apr 13

**Gov. Christopher Waller — Apr 17 economic outlook speech** ([Fed speech text](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/speech/waller20260417a.htm), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-17/fed-s-waller-signals-caution-on-rate-cuts-sees-risk-of-longer-conflict), [Reuters via Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/economy-news/feds-waller-says-swift-war-end-could-keep-rate-cut-hopes-alive-4621395)):

- **Hawkish lean near term**: "leaving me cautious about rate cuts now and more inclined toward cuts to support the labor market later this year when the outlook is more steady" — conditional on a quick end to the Strait of Hormuz disruption.
- Conditional dovish path: "a forecast in which underlying inflation would continue to move toward 2 percent" if the war ends quickly.
- Downside scenario: "the longer energy prices remain elevated and the Strait of Hormuz is constrained, the greater the chances that higher inflation gets embedded across a wide variety of goods and services."
- Notable structural call-out: net immigration collapsed from ~2.3M (2024) to near-zero (2025–26), so "very little or no net job creation is necessary" to maintain employment — explains why Waller is willing to discount weak headline payroll prints.
- This is widely viewed as the **last Fed speech before the pre-FOMC blackout** for the Apr 28–29 meeting ([Reuters](https://www.investing.com/news/economy-news/feds-waller-says-swift-war-end-could-keep-rate-cut-hopes-alive-4621395)).

**Powell — Harvard appearance, early-mid April** ([Harvard Gazette](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2026/04/powell-issues-a-warning-on-u-s-debt/)):
- Warned on US debt trajectory: "The country has to get back to ensuring that the economy is growing fast enough to keep pace with spending… It will not end well if we don't do something fairly soon."
- Reaffirmed 2% inflation commitment despite tariffs and the Iran conflict; said the Fed will "wait and see" on Iran-related oil, monitoring inflation expectations "very, very carefully."

### FOMC Minutes — March 17–18 meeting (released Apr 8, in scope for the week of Apr 13 reading)

[Fed press release](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260408a.htm), [minutes](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260318.htm):

- Held funds rate at 3.50–3.75% in March.
- "Some participants remarked that further progress in reducing inflation had been absent in recent months."
- Notably hawkish line: "some participants judged that there was a strong case for a two-sided description of future interest rate decisions" — code for keeping a hike on the table if inflation re-accelerates ([Fortune coverage](https://fortune.com/2026/02/19/warsh-hawkish-fed-powell-cuts-hike-policy-meeting-notes/)).
- "The vast majority of participants judged that upside risks to inflation and downside risks to employment were elevated and that they had increased with developments in the Middle East."

## Credit Markets

### High Yield (ICE BofA HY Master II OAS, BAMLH0A0HYM2)

- **Level**: ~285 bps in April 2026 ([Trading Economics / FRED](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/bofa-merrill-lynch-us-high-yield-option-adjusted-spread-fed-data.html), [FRED series](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2)).
- Global HY broader index: end-March spread ~343 bps, +30 bps wider on the month, +40 bps from YTD lows ([Nomura HY Monthly](https://www.nomura-asset.co.uk/insight/high-yield-monthly-update/)).
- **Mid-April reversal**: Credit investors moved back into riskier paper as the US-Iran ceasefire took hold. JPMorgan flow data shows in the first half of April, investors bought a net **$500M of bonds in the lowest tier of investment grade** while selling **$7.3B of higher-quality tiers** ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-18/traders-ready-to-put-war-behind-them-dial-up-risk-credit-weekly)).

### Investment Grade (ICE BofA US Corporate OAS, BAMLC0A0CM)

- **Level**: ~90 bps, up from a cycle-low ~70 bps earlier in the year ([Morningstar](https://www.morningstar.com/markets/amid-iran-war-credit-spreads-show-early-signs-widening), [FRED series](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLC0A0CM)).
- The widening reflects Iran-war repricing in late Feb / March that has begun to reverse in April.

### Short-term funding stress

- AA-rated 30-day non-financial CP / 1M SOFR spread widened to **+6 bps** vs. **0 bps pre-Iran-war (Feb 28)** — a small but non-zero stress signal ([Reuters via Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/economy-news/us-shortterm-credit-market-shows-early-signs-of-stress-as-iran-war-persists-4601299)).

### Net read

Spreads have widened meaningfully off cycle tights since the Feb 28 outbreak of the Iran war but remain historically tight. The week of Apr 13 saw further tightening on truce optimism — credit is "tuning out" the war risk, with positioning rotating into lower-quality IG and high yield ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-17/tuning-out-iran-war-risk-is-paying-off-for-credit-investors)).

## Weekend Developments

### Trump–Powell escalation continues

The Trump administration's pressure campaign on Fed Chair Powell intensified into the weekend ([Washington Today Apr 18](https://nationaltoday.com/us/dc/washington/news/2026/04/18/trump-escalates-pressure-on-fed-chair-powell-through-criminal-probe/), [PBS News](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/as-trump-threatened-to-fire-powell-federal-prosecutors-showed-up-unannounced-at-the-federal-reserve-building), [CNN Apr 15](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/15/economy/powell-trump-fire-fed-chair)):

- Trump told Fox Business' Maria Bartiromo on Apr 15 that he will fire Powell "next month" if he doesn't step aside. Powell's term as Chair expires in mid-May.
- Federal prosecutors showed up unannounced at the Fed building this past week, were denied access. DOJ subpoenas tied to the renovation cost-overrun probe were quashed by Judge James Boasberg, who ruled the "dominant (if not sole) purpose" of the subpoenas was to "harass and pressure Powell either to yield to the President or to resign."
- Net market consequence: the Trump pressure campaign has paradoxically **prolonged Powell's tenure** by stalling Warsh's confirmation. Bond market is watching this as a 2H 2026 risk to Fed independence pricing.

### Iran ceasefire / oil

- 10-day Lebanon-Israel ceasefire took effect; US-Iran diplomatic talks signaled to resume "as soon as the weekend" ([yourNEWS Apr 17](https://yournews.com/2026/04/17/6804394/oil-prices-slide-as-middle-east-ceasefire-and-iran-talks/), [CNBC Apr 8](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/us-iran-war-ceasefire-middle-east-strait-of-hormuz-oil-markets.html)).
- Brent fell -1.35% to $98.05; WTI -1.74% to $93.40 in Friday trading on truce hopes.
- **April 21 deadline** referenced in coverage as a key inflection — coincides with the Warsh hearing. If talks collapse and Hormuz disruption persists, expect immediate repricing of the back-half-2026 cut path lower (i.e., terminal rate higher).
