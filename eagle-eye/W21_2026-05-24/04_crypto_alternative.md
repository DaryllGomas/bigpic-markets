## Regulation & Institutional

### GENIUS Act — Now Law
The U.S. Stablecoin GENIUS Act passed the Senate (68–30) on June 17, 2025, the House on July 17, 2025, and was signed into law by President Trump on July 18, 2025. The act established the first federal stablecoin regulatory framework in U.S. history, requiring payment stablecoins to be backed 1:1 by liquid reserves, restricting yield-bearing products, and bifurcating oversight between federal (OCC/Fed) and state regulators. The GENIUS Act is now the governing framework for USDT and USDC operations in U.S. markets. Source: Davis Polk, Fenwick, Wikipedia.

### CLARITY Act — Senate Markup Underway
The Digital Asset Market Clarity Act (CLARITY Act) — which governs the broader SEC/CFTC jurisdictional split for crypto assets — passed the House in 2025. The Senate Banking Committee completed a markup of its 309-page draft bill on May 14, 2026. A 60-vote Senate threshold is needed. Polymarket traders assign **75% probability** the CLARITY Act becomes law in 2026. Key provision: tokens deemed sufficiently decentralized move from SEC (securities) to CFTC (commodities) oversight. Source: CoinGabbar, FinanceMagnates, Latham & Watkins Crypto Policy Tracker.

### SEC Blocks Prediction Market ETFs
The SEC delayed the launch of 24 prediction-market ETFs filed by Roundhill Investments, Bitwise, and GraniteShares, citing need for additional study, investor risk clarity, and CFTC jurisdictional overlap. The SEC's pause echoes the multi-year delay before spot Bitcoin ETF approval and is seen as caution rather than outright opposition. Source: CNBC (May 10), BanklessTimes (May 21), Phemex.

### EU Sanctions — Crypto Dimension
The European Union activated its **20th sanctions package** in the week of May 17–23, prohibiting EU-based crypto-asset service providers from transacting with entities in Russia or Belarus. The package explicitly blacklisted the Russian Digital Ruble, the Belarusian Digital Ruble, and Rosbank's ruble stablecoin. Source: BanklessTimes May 24 Recap.

### U.S. Strategic Bitcoin Reserve
Established by executive order in March 2025, the Strategic Bitcoin Reserve (SBR) continues to underpin institutional confidence in BTC as a sovereign-level asset. This backdrop has driven corporate treasury accumulation throughout 2026.

### Corporate Treasury Bitcoin Holdings
Public companies collectively hold approximately **1.19–1.22 million BTC** (~5.5–5.7% of total supply) as of early May 2026. MicroStrategy holds ~257,000 BTC (as of year-end 2025). Strive's Bitcoin treasury reached 15,000.5 BTC as of May 1, 2026. Institutional accumulation continues despite ETF outflows — a divergence that analysts attribute to long-duration balance sheet buyers vs. shorter-term ETF trading vehicles.

### Quantum Threat Note (per THESIS.md)
Bitcoin's long-run quantum vulnerability remains a background risk: approximately $718B in BTC sits in quantum-vulnerable P2PK addresses. BIP-360 (quantum-resistant upgrade) is estimated to take 5–10 years. The CNSA 2.0 PQC compliance deadline (January 2027) is driving PQC migration across the broader financial system; Bitcoin's upgrade timeline lags significantly. This is not an acute 2026 risk but warrants monitoring as quantum hardware milestones (IBM Kookaburra 2026, Riverlane MegaQuOp) approach.
