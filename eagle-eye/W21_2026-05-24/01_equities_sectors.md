## Weekend Developments

Multiple material developments occurred Saturday and Sunday, May 23-24, that have direct implications for Tuesday's open (markets are closed Monday, May 25 for Memorial Day).

### 1. Iran–U.S. Peace Deal: "Largely Negotiated," But Not Signed

The most consequential weekend development. President Trump stated Saturday (May 23) that a deal to end the war with Iran and reopen the Strait of Hormuz is **"largely negotiated"** and would be announced shortly. However, on Sunday (May 24) Trump walked back the urgency, saying the U.S. would not rush into an agreement. Iran's semi-official Tasnim news agency reported that "differences over one or two clauses of the possible memorandum of understanding still persist."

Key sticking points as of Sunday evening:
- Iran insists nuclear issues are NOT part of the current MOU discussion
- Iran has made no commitments on its enriched uranium stockpiles
- A 30-day negotiating period after signing was proposed

**Market implication:** WTI crude closed around $96.35 on Friday, down ~4% for the week on deal optimism, but oil spiked late Friday when Iran's Supreme Leader reaffirmed that enriched uranium must remain inside Iran. A signed deal opening the Strait (through which ~20% of global oil passes) would likely trigger a sharp further decline in crude, benefiting transportation and consumer sectors while pressuring energy stocks. A breakdown would reverse those moves violently. This is the most important variable for Tuesday's open.

**Equity futures will open Sunday evening** for the Tuesday session; any Saturday/Sunday headline on Iran could gap ES and NQ materially.

Sources: CNBC (May 23), Axios (May 6), Wikipedia – 2026 Iran war ceasefire, NPR (May 24), CNN (May 24).

### 2. SpaceX IPO Filing — $1.75 Trillion Nasdaq Debut Set for June 12

SpaceX released its S-1 prospectus on May 20 (Thursday), with a Nasdaq listing under ticker **SPCX** targeting June 11 pricing and June 12 debut. The company is seeking to raise ~$75 billion at a **$1.75 trillion valuation** — which would shatter the previous IPO record (Saudi Aramco's $29 billion in 2019) and rank SpaceX immediately among the ten most valuable public companies globally.

The IPO includes SpaceX's rocket launch business, Starlink (9M+ subscribers, $10.4B revenue), and xAI (Musk's AI company, merged with SpaceX in February 2026). If it prices at the top of range, SpaceX's market cap would exceed Tesla's.

**Market implication for thesis names:** This is expected to act as a major re-pricing catalyst for the entire space sector. RKLB, PL, LUNR, and the UFO ETF may see sustained pre-IPO enthusiasm in the weeks ahead. The IPO will also establish a precedent valuation for Starlink's broadband business, potentially repricing satellite internet plays.

Sources: TradingKey, CoinDesk, CNBC SpaceX IPO live updates (May 20), Analytical Insight.

### 3. Kevin Warsh Sworn In as Fed Chair

Kevin Warsh was sworn in as the 17th Chair of the Federal Reserve on Friday, May 22, in an East Room ceremony at the White House — the first Fed chair sworn in at the White House since Alan Greenspan in 1987. The Senate confirmed him 54-45 on May 13, the most divisive confirmation in Fed history.

**Market reaction Friday:** The Dow traded 364 points higher intraday, though much of the move was attributed to the Iran deal headlines. Warsh's oath itself was not a surprise — he was already priced in.

**The Motley Fool (May 24)** published an analysis Sunday headlined "Here's the One Thing New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh Could Do That Would Actually Crash the Stock Market," reflecting ongoing Sunday investor anxiety about Warsh's credibility on rate cuts. Strategists expect the Fed to hold rates steady through the rest of 2026 as energy-driven inflation and the Iran war fuel costs remain elevated.

Sources: Al Jazeera (May 22), Washington Post (May 22), PBS NewsHour, CNBC (May 22).

**VIX:** ~16.76 at Friday's close — well below the long-term average of ~19, indicating complacency or genuine calm.

**Market breadth — mixed signals:**
- The S&P 500's advance/decline line has been trending higher since the Iran conflict selloff nadir in late March, alongside the index recapturing 7,000 and then 7,400+.
- Goldman Sachs' proprietary breadth measure was the weakest since late 2023 as of mid-May.
- Approximately 20% of consumer discretionary stocks were at 52-week lows even as the index hit new highs — a notable divergence.
- The financial sector (XLF) was reportedly down ~7% YTD as of late April/early May, while technology rallied ~17%, creating a narrow-leadership dynamic.
- The "AI 11" (Information Technology + Communication Services megacaps + AMZN) account for the overwhelming majority of 2026's YTD S&P 500 advance per Goldman Sachs.

**Advance/Decline:** Directionally positive for the week on the strength of breadth improvement in small/mid-caps (Russell 2000 led on Friday). Specific NYSE A/D numbers not confirmed.

**New Highs/Lows:** The DJIA set a new all-time record close (50,579.70) on May 22. S&P 500 at 7,473.47 remains within range of all-time highs (ATH was ~5,700 area in January on the reset, though the index has been in recovery).

**Put/Call Ratio:** Not confirmed for the week. VIX at 16.76 implies options markets are not in fear territory.

**Seasonal note (from CALENDAR.md):** We are in the "Sell in May" window (May 1-Oct 31), historically the weaker 6-month period. Counter to that seasonal headwind, the market posted an 8th straight weekly gain — suggesting the AI/Iran dual catalyst complex is overriding seasonal weakness for now.
