**Five-week losing streak.** The S&P 500 posted its fifth consecutive weekly decline -- the longest streak since 2022 -- falling 3.39% over the five trading days. The Dow Jones entered correction territory on Friday after dropping nearly 800 points. The Nasdaq Composite fell below 21,000 and is now in correction, down more than 10% from its highs. The Russell 2000 continues to underperform, down more than 7% for March alone.

**Primary driver: Iran war / Strait of Hormuz oil shock.** The conflict, now in its fifth week, remains the dominant macro force. Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz beginning March 4 has disrupted ~20% of global oil supply -- the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market per the IEA. Brent crude surged from $72.48 on Feb 28 to $112.57 on March 27 (+55%). WTI briefly touched $100/bbl on Friday before settling at $99.64 (+5.46% on the day). The oil shock is fueling inflation fears, compressing growth forecasts, and forcing a dramatic rotation out of growth/tech and into energy, materials, and defensive sectors.

**Fed held rates at 3.50-3.75%** at the March 18 FOMC meeting but acknowledged "increased uncertainty due to war in the Middle East." The committee continued to project one rate cut this year, but futures markets are now pricing >50% probability of a rate *hike* in late 2026 if oil prices persist at elevated levels.

**Moody's recession model** now assigns 49% probability to a U.S. recession -- the highest level in years.

**Anthropic "Claude Mythos" leak** (March 26-27) was an additional catalyst for tech/cyber selling on Friday. A configuration error at Anthropic exposed ~3,000 unpublished assets including details of a next-generation AI model described as having "unprecedented cybersecurity risks," sparking a sharp selloff in cybersecurity stocks.

## Weekend Developments
*Post-Friday close through Sunday evening, March 29, 2026*

**S&P 500 futures -0.5%, Dow futures -253 pts (-0.6%), Nasdaq futures -0.5%** on Sunday evening as investors entered a holiday-shortened week (markets closed Friday April 3 for Good Friday).

**Iran war escalation continues.** Over the weekend, oil industry executives and CNBC analysts warned that if the Strait of Hormuz is not reopened within 1-3 weeks, oil prices could surge toward $150-200/bbl. Global shortages are emerging: Pakistan has told cricket fans to watch games from home to conserve fuel; Thailand and Australia report gas station shortfalls; Europe faces imminent diesel shortages. Refined fuel prices (diesel, jet fuel) have exceeded $200/bbl at times. Iran's new supreme leader vowed to keep blocking the Strait.

**The world has lost 4.5-5 million barrels/day** of oil supply, and that number is expected to double by mid-April, making this the largest loss of crude supply in history.

**Week ahead:** Holiday-shortened trading week (Good Friday April 3). Key data: JOLTS (Tue), ADP Employment (Wed), March Jobs Report (Fri, released despite market closure). Earnings: Nike (NKE), McCormick (MKC), Conagra (CAG).
