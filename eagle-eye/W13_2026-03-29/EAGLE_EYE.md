## This Week's Take

The Strait of Hormuz crisis is now the defining macro event of 2026. As the blockade enters its one-month mark, the largest oil supply disruption in history — 4.5-5 million barrels/day lost, roughly 5% of global supply — has reshaped every asset class. Brent crude closed Friday at $112.57 (+55% since late February), WTI briefly touched $100, and oil executives are warning that if the Strait isn't reopened by mid-April, disruptions double and $150-200/bbl becomes plausible. This is no longer a "geopolitical premium" story — it's a regime change forcing a complete repricing of the inflation, rates, and growth outlook.

The market consequences are cascading. The S&P 500 fell 3.39% to post its fifth consecutive weekly decline — the worst streak since 2022 — while the Dow entered correction territory. The most dramatic shift: futures markets now price a >50% probability of a Fed rate *hike* by year-end, a complete reversal from pre-war expectations of 2-3 cuts. The 10-year Treasury yield hit 4.44%, the 30-year touched 5.00% intraday, and the yield curve has "solidly uninverted" via bear steepening — not a growth signal, but an inflation alarm. Credit markets show stress concentrating in CCC & Lower (974 bps OAS), while IG and BB remain orderly.

Adding to Friday's selling, Anthropic's accidental leak of details about its next-generation "Claude Mythos" AI model — described as having "unprecedented cybersecurity capabilities" — triggered a 4-7% flash crash across cybersecurity stocks (CRWD -7%, PANW -6%, ZS -4.5%). The sector rotation story is now stark: XLE +38% YTD vs. tech in correction. Moody's recession probability stands at 49%. Sunday night S&P futures are down 0.5%, and Powell speaks Monday — the first post-FOMC appearance since the March 18 hold. Markets face a gauntlet this week: JOLTS, ISM Manufacturing, Retail Sales, and the March jobs report, all while the Hormuz clock ticks toward a mid-April hard deadline.

## Fed & Rates Outlook

**Current rate: 3.50-3.75%** (held March 18, 11-1 vote with one dissent for a cut).

The March FOMC meeting revealed a deeply fractured committee: 7 members project no cuts in 2026, 7 project one cut, and the remaining members are split between 50-100+ bps of cuts. The median dot projects one 25 bps cut (to 3.25-3.50%), but this masks a bimodal distribution. Core PCE forecast was raised to 2.7% for year-end (from 2.5%), and the longer-run neutral rate shifted up to 3.0%.

**The most stunning market shift:** CME FedWatch now prices a ~52% probability of a rate *hike* by year-end — a complete reversal from pre-war expectations of 2-3 cuts. April is 94.8% hold, June is ~85% hold / ~10% cut / ~5% hike. The 2-year yield at 3.93% has flipped from pricing cuts to pricing hikes in three weeks.

**Treasury curve bear-steepening:** The 2s10s spread at +51 bps and 3m10y at +71 bps reflect a curve that has "solidly uninverted" — but via inflation fears and term premium, not growth optimism. The 30-year briefly touched 5.00%. The MOVE index above 130 signals bond market stress.

**Credit:** IG OAS ~120 bps, HY ~320 bps — wider but orderly. The bifurcation story is CCC & Lower at 974 bps, approaching distress territory. If oil persists at these levels and the Fed shifts to hiking, the lowest-quality credit faces a real crunch.

**Liquidity:** QT ended December 2025. The Fed is doing $40B/month in Reserve Management Purchases (T-bills) to maintain $3.02T in reserves. ON RRP near zero — the excess liquidity buffer is gone. TGA at ~$871B absorbing cash. Fed plans to taper RMPs after mid-April, which will need monitoring.

**Powell speaks Monday March 30 at 2:30 PM ET** — the first public appearance since the March 18 presser. Markets will parse every word on whether oil-driven inflation is viewed as transitory or risks becoming entrenched.

## Positioning & Thesis Update

**The macro regime has changed.** The Iran/Hormuz crisis has forced a repricing of everything. The key actionable takeaways:

**Energy remains the trade.** XLE +38% YTD with further upside if Hormuz persists. The mid-April deadline is the next binary catalyst — if the Strait reopens, energy gives back gains fast. If it doesn't, $150 Brent becomes the base case.

**Critical minerals thesis accelerating.** NdPr +105%, cobalt +161% YoY, lithium above $20K/t. The supply disruption narrative from Hormuz (fertilizer, shipping) compounds existing structural deficits. ALB hitting new 52-week highs. FCX Grasberg restart in Q2 is the key catalyst for copper.

**Nuclear thesis intact but prices consolidating.** Uranium at $83.90/lb in a structural 30-40M lb deficit. Energy security urgency from Hormuz reinforces the nuclear renaissance narrative. CEG, VST, and CCJ are thesis core. Part 53 final rule (March 27) is a potential SMR catalyst.

**Cybersecurity thesis faces a new narrative risk.** The Mythos leak introduced AI-disruption fear into the sector for the first time. Monitor whether this is a one-day event or the start of structural derating. Defense IT names (LDOS, CACI) with government backlogs may be better positioned than commercial platforms.

**Crypto at a crossroads.** BTC at $66,600 with Fear & Greed at 12/100 for 46 straight days — historically a contrarian buy signal (~78% of the time). But miner capitulation (hash rate -22%), persistent ETF outflows, and macro headwinds argue against catching the knife. ETH at $2,000 is the line in the sand. The SEC/CFTC taxonomy framework is a structural positive that will matter more when risk appetite returns.

**Rates positioning is the most dangerous market.** The 10Y at 4.44% with a bear-steepening curve, ON RRP near zero, and a Fed that's fractured 7-7 on cuts vs. hold — this is a setup for extreme volatility. Powell Monday and NFP Friday are the week's binary events. If both signal stagflation (persistent inflation + weakening labor), the 10Y goes to 4.50%+ and the 30Y breaks above 5.00%.

**Key risk: the Hormuz deadline.** Trump's ~April 6 deadline for Iran is now the single most important date for global markets. Everything else — earnings, data, Fed — is secondary until the oil supply question is resolved.
