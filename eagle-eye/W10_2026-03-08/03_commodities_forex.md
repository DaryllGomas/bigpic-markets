# Commodities & Forex Report -- Week 10, 2026-03-08

*Eagle Eye Weekly Macro Report -- Point-in-time snapshot as of Saturday, March 8, 2026*

---

**CRITICAL CONTEXT: The trading week ending March 6, 2026 was dominated by the US-Israeli military strikes on Iran beginning February 28, which killed Supreme Leader Khamenei and triggered a full-scale Middle East war. The Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed, Qatar halted LNG production after drone attacks on Ras Laffan, and Iraq is shutting in production. Oil posted its largest weekly gain in futures history. ## Energy

### Crude Oil

| Benchmark | Friday Close (Mar 6) | Weekly Change | Prior Week Close |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------|------------------|
| WTI (CL, Apr '26) | $90.90/bbl | **+35.63%** (largest weekly gain in futures history, dating to 1983) | ~$67.00 |
| Brent (May '26) | $92.69/bbl | **+35%+** (surged 8.52% on Friday alone) | ~$68.60 |

**What happened:** US-Israeli strikes on Iran on February 28 killed Supreme Leader Khamenei and triggered a full-scale military escalation. Iran retaliated with strikes on Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Qatar, and the IRGC declared the Strait of Hormuz closed to all shipping. Brent touched $94 intraday Friday -- the highest since September 2023. Insurance companies withdrew war risk coverage, effectively halting tanker traffic through the strait, which handles ~20% of global oil supply.

**OPEC+ decision (March 1):** Despite the crisis, OPEC+ agreed to boost production by 206,000 bpd for April, above the initially expected 137,000 bpd increase but well short of the maximum 548,000 bpd option discussed. The modest increase was widely viewed as insufficient to offset Hormuz disruption.

**Supply disruptions in progress:**
- Iraq shutting down 1.5 million bpd of production due to inability to export via Hormuz
- Kuwait cutting production as storage capacity exhausts
- Kurdish Kormor field halted as precautionary measure
- Goldman Sachs target: $110/bbl; JP Morgan sees $120-130 if disruptions persist

**EIA inventories (week ending Feb 27, released Mar 4):** US crude inventories at 424.4 million barrels, approximately 4% below the five-year average for this time of year.

### Natural Gas

| Benchmark | Level (Mar 6) | Weekly Change | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|-------|
| Henry Hub | ~$3.00+/MMBtu | Rallied sharply | Reclaimed $3, supported by oil correlation and geopolitical risk |
| Dutch TTF (Europe) | EUR 55-58/MWh | **+66% weekly** | Highest since 2023; surged 49% on Mar 2 alone |

**Qatar LNG shutdown:** Iran launched drone strikes on Qatar's Ras Laffan Industrial City and Mesaieed Industrial City, forcing QatarEnergy -- the world's largest LNG producer -- to halt all production and export operations. Qatar supplies ~20% of global LNG. Goldman Sachs estimates the pause reduces near-term global LNG supply by approximately 19%.

**European exposure is acute:** Gas storage across Europe is below 30% capacity (Germany at 20.5%, France at 21%), compounding the impact of the Qatar outage. The TTF front-month surged from the mid-EUR 30s to above EUR 55/MWh in a single week.

**Thesis links:** The natural gas spike reinforces the AI/data center thesis -- natural gas is "the bridge" fuel for data center power (Williams Companies $5.1B commitment, Kinder Morgan 40% of US gas transport). Higher gas prices increase the economic case for nuclear baseload power (CEG, VST thesis).

**The gold paradox:** Despite being the ultimate safe-haven asset during a major Middle East war, gold posted its worst weekly performance in months. The driver was a surging US dollar (DXY gained 1%+ on the week), which made dollar-priced bullion more expensive for foreign holders. Additionally, rising oil-driven inflation fears reduced expectations for Fed rate cuts, pushing real yields higher. There was also evidence of liquidation/margin-call-driven selling as equity markets cratered.

**Weekend recovery:** Gold is recovering in weekend OTC/Asian trading, touching $5,186 on March 8, as safe-haven demand reasserts. If the conflict deepens, gold likely resumes its uptrend. The 52-week high of $5,595 remains the key resistance level.

### Container & Tanker Shipping

**Tanker rates hit all-time highs** as insurers withdrew war risk protection for the Middle East. Supertanker rates surged to record levels.

**Container shipping surcharges:**
- Hapag-Lloyd: War Risk Surcharge of $1,500/TEU and $3,500 for reefer/special equipment (effective Mar 2)
- Maersk: Emergency freight rate increase for all Middle East-bound cargo
- Ships rerouting around the Cape of Good Hope, adding significant transit time and cost

**Commodity freight impacts (Fastmarkets):** Freight rates for aluminium, bauxite, zinc, lead, and lithium shipments all elevated by Strait of Hormuz tensions. Limited direct fallout in China thus far, as Chinese refiners hold strategic reserves.

**Thesis links:** Supply chain disruption reinforces the critical minerals reshoring thesis. Western processing independence (MP Materials 10-X facility, USA Rare Earth CHIPS Act facility, Ucore RapidSX) becomes more strategically urgent when Middle East chokepoints are closed.

## Weekend Developments

**THIS SECTION COVERS POST-FRIDAY NEWS THROUGH MARCH 8, 2026 (SATURDAY). THESE ARE THE MOST IMPORTANT ITEMS FOR MONDAY'S OPEN.**

### Iran Conflict Deepening -- No Ceasefire in Sight

- **New Supreme Leader named (Mar 8):** Iran's Assembly of Experts selected Mojtaba Khamenei (son of the killed leader) as the new Supreme Leader. IRGC and top leaders pledged allegiance. This signals continuity of the hardline position.
- **Iran rejects ceasefire (Mar 7-8):** Foreign Minister Araghchi told NBC's "Meet the Press" that Iran will not accept a ceasefire, stating "We need to continue fighting for the sake of our people." He said the US and Israel had already broken the previous ceasefire.
- **Trump escalates rhetoric (Mar 7):** Called the new supreme leader "lightweight" and said Iran "will be hit very hard." Signaled plans to escalate attacks. No diplomatic off-ramp is visible.
- **Israel strikes Tehran oil depots (Mar 8):** Israel bombed Tehran oil infrastructure, further escalating the conflict.
- **Multiple nations urging citizen evacuations:** US, UK, Germany, France, Italy, China, and Poland urged citizens to leave Iran and Gulf states.

### Market Implications for Monday

1. **Oil will likely gap higher at Monday's open.** Weekend developments show the conflict deepening with no ceasefire prospects. Iran's rejection of diplomatic efforts and continued Israeli strikes on Tehran oil infrastructure suggest Brent could test $95-100 next week. Goldman's $110 target and JPM's $120-130 scenario become increasingly probable.

2. **European gas (TTF) at acute risk.** Qatar LNG remains offline. European storage below 30% entering the spring refill season is an emergency-level situation. TTF could spike further above EUR 60/MWh.

3. **Gold likely to recover.** The weekend spot price ($5,174-5,186) shows buying interest returning. If equities sell off further on Monday, gold should benefit from safe-haven rotation despite dollar strength.

4. **US jobs report (Mar 6) adds complexity.** NFP came in weak, increasing Fed rate cut expectations. This cross-current -- war-driven inflation vs. weakening labor market -- creates a challenging backdrop for Fed policy. The March 17-18 FOMC meeting (SEP meeting with dot plot, per CALENDAR.md) is now the most important macro event in the next two weeks.

5. **Equity risk-off likely to continue.** Oil's surge is a de facto tax increase on consumers and businesses. The Motley Fool noted oil prices are "flashing a warning last seen in 2022" -- the last time oil spiked this rapidly, it preceded significant equity weakness.

6. **Nuclear and defense equities may outperform.** The energy security thesis is dramatically reinforced. CEG, VST, CCJ (nuclear), LMT, NOC (defense/space), and critical minerals names (MP, FCX, UUUU) should see relative strength.

### Other Weekend Developments

- **No OPEC emergency meeting announced** as of Saturday. The 206,000 bpd April increase is insufficient to offset Hormuz disruption.
- **No significant weather events** affecting agricultural markets.
- **China NPC in session:** Expected to provide growth assurance for the Chinese economy in 2026; watch for any fiscal stimulus announcements that could support copper and industrial metals.
