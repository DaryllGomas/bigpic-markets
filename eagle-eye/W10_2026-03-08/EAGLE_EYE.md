## This Week's Take

The Iran war is the story. Everything else — the worst jobs report since the pandemic, Broadcom's blowout AI quarter, CrowdStrike becoming the first $5B ARR cybersecurity company — is secondary to the fact that oil just posted its largest weekly gain in the history of futures trading (WTI +35.6% to $90.90), the Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed, and Sunday night futures show crude rocketing past $108/bbl. S&P 500 futures are down ~1.5% and Dow futures down ~1.9% heading into Monday's open. This is the most hostile macro backdrop since the 2022 Russia-Ukraine shock: a textbook stagflation setup with the economy shedding 92,000 jobs in February while oil-driven inflation surges, wages run hot at +3.8% YoY, and the Fed is pinned at 3.50-3.75% with no good options. The March 18 FOMC meeting — now just 10 days away — will deliver its first 2026 dot plot into a world where the inflation outlook has been shattered by a Middle East war that shows no signs of de-escalation.

Beneath the geopolitical chaos, the fundamental picture is diverging sharply by thesis. AI infrastructure spending remains structurally intact — Broadcom's AI revenue doubled (+106% YoY to $8.4B) and Q2 guidance of $22B crushed estimates, confirming the $650B hyperscaler capex cycle is real. Cybersecurity is validated by CrowdStrike's record quarter. Nuclear energy and defense benefit structurally from the energy security crisis. But these sector tailwinds are overwhelmed in the near term by the macro headwinds: the VIX hit 29.49 on Sunday night (up ~50% from the prior week), the Russell 2000 dropped -3.7% (signaling collapsing risk appetite), and the Materials sector posted its worst week since April at -7%. The market is pricing stagflation, and until the Hormuz crisis resolves or the Fed signals its response, risk assets face persistent downward pressure.

The cruel irony is that Monday also marks T-9 for March 20 Triple Witch, where the pre-expiration positioning rally has historically averaged +0.82%. That statistical tendency faces its sternest test against a war that is actively reshaping global energy markets.

## Positioning & Thesis Update

### The Stagflation Trade

The macro regime has shifted decisively toward stagflation: weak employment (-92K NFP), hot inflation (core PCE 2.9%, oil $100+, wages +3.8%), and a paralyzed Fed. This is the worst possible environment for long-duration growth equities and the best for:

1. **Energy equities** — XLE was the only positive sector. Direct beneficiaries of $100+ oil.
2. **Nuclear thesis** — Iran war elevates energy security to national priority. CEG, VST, CCJ benefit from the strategic case for domestic baseload power independent of fossil fuel supply chains. The oil/gas price spike increases the economic case for nuclear.
3. **Defense/Space** — LMT, NOC, LDOS, CACI. War spending accelerates. USSF budget already $40B+.
4. **Cybersecurity** — CRWD's blowout quarter + nation-state threat escalation = structural spending tailwind.
5. **Critical minerals reshoring** — Hormuz closure reinforces the case for Western processing independence. MP, FCX, UUUU benefit from supply chain urgency.

- **VIX at 29.49 and rising** — hedging costs are elevated but protection is warranted

- **ON RRP at zero** — liquidity buffer is gone. Quarter-end (Mar 31) could stress funding markets
- **Triple Witch positioning** — statistical tailwind but may be overwhelmed by macro headwinds
