## Bitcoin

**Current Price (Mar 8):** ~$67,255 | **Weekly Change (Mon open to Sat):** -3.5% (opened ~$69,700 on Monday after a short-covering spike)

Bitcoin had a volatile week defined by geopolitical shock and institutional outflows. The week began with a short-covering-driven spike above $69,000 on Monday as traders squared positions ahead of the Iran conflict escalation. BTC briefly touched $72,700 mid-week as crypto outperformed equities during the initial risk-off session -- decentralized exchanges like Hyperliquid saw volume spike to ~$200M/day as crypto's 24/7 markets became the primary venue for real-time geopolitical price discovery when traditional markets were closed. However, a toxic convergence on Friday drove a ~$110B market cap wipeout: (1) a New York federal judge certified a class-action lawsuit against Tether/Bitfinex, (2) Azerbaijani security forces neutralized an Iranian plot to sabotage the BTC oil pipeline, spiking Brent crude to $85/bbl, and (3) Bitcoin ETFs posted $227.83M in net outflows on Friday alone.

**Key Support/Resistance Levels:**
- **Resistance:** $68,683 (immediate), $70,000 (psychological), $72,000-$72,500 (61.8% Fibonacci retracement from $78,988 high to $60,500 low)
- **Support:** $65,000-$66,000 (principal support zone), $60,000 (critical -- breach accelerates downside)
- Price is trading below the 200-day SMA

**Dominance:** 58.88% (+2.3 percentage points over the past 30 days), continuing the trend of institutional allocators preferentially accumulating BTC over altcoins

**Hash Rate:** 1.007-1.053 ZH/s (all-time highs). Mining difficulty at 145.04T, with the next adjustment (~Mar 20) expected to decrease to ~142.20T. Rising oil prices from the Iran conflict are compressing miner margins, with some public miners forced to liquidate BTC treasury reserves to maintain solvency.

**On-Chain Metrics:**
- Exchange whale ratio surged to 0.64 (highest in 10 months) -- 64% of all exchange inflows came from top-10 deposits, indicating large holders are driving selling activity
- Average exchange inflow climbed to 1.58 BTC (highest since June 2022)
- Exchange reserves continue trending down structurally due to ETF and corporate treasury demand, but whale ratio spike is an early warning
- Bitcoin futures OI reached 680K BTC, highest in nearly two weeks
- Fear & Greed Index: 12/100 ("Extreme Fear") for 22 consecutive days -- only the third such streak in crypto history after COVID crash (2020) and FTX collapse (2022)

**Supply Context:** The next halving is estimated for ~April 2028 (not March 2026 as some sources incorrectly reported). Current block reward: 3.125 BTC. Approximately 19.83M of 21M total BTC have been mined.

## Ethereum

**Current Price (Mar 8):** ~$1,965 | **Weekly Change:** -2.8% (from ~$2,020 Monday open)

Ethereum continues to significantly underperform Bitcoin, trading 60% below its August 2025 ATH of $4,953. The ETH/BTC ratio remains in a prolonged consolidation between 0.028 and 0.030 -- near multi-year lows. A breakout above 0.06 would signal altcoin season; a drop below 0.028 would reinforce Bitcoin dominance further.

**Gas Fees:** Layer-2 adoption has driven mainnet gas fees to historically low levels. The shift to PoS and EIP-1559 fee-burn mechanism make supply dynamics structurally scarcer during periods of heavy activity, but current low activity is resulting in minimal ETH burn.

**Staking Yield:** 3.3-4.2% APY depending on network activity. Total ETH staked: ~35.86M ETH (28.91% of total supply), approaching the 30% milestone. Staking participation growth signals long-term holder conviction despite price weakness.

**L2 Activity:** Ethereum Layer-2 activity is consolidating into a winner-take-most structure:
- **Base:** 11.57M daily transactions, 663K active addresses -- dominates with 46.58% of L2 DeFi TVL and 60%+ of all L2 transactions
- **Arbitrum:** 4.17M daily transactions, 132K active addresses, 30.86% of L2 DeFi TVL (ArbOS Dia upgrade in January 2026 enhanced gas fee predictability)
- **Optimism** rounds out the top 3; together these three process ~90% of all L2 transactions
- Most other rollups have seen usage collapse after incentive cycles -- 21Shares warns many L2s may not survive 2026

**Exchange Supply:** ETH reserves on exchanges dropped to 16M -- a record low -- while daily active addresses surged 112% YoY. This divergence (falling exchange supply + rising activity) is historically a bullish setup, though the weekly price trend remains bearish.

**Funding Rate:** ETH perpetual funding rate at -0.0088%, confirming bearish positioning extends across the market cap spectrum.

## Regulation & Institutional

### SEC Framework Submission

The SEC submitted interpretive guidance to the White House on March 3, 2026, establishing a "token taxonomy" framework for categorizing crypto assets to determine which fall under SEC jurisdiction as securities. This is a landmark development -- the guidance, titled "Commission Interpretation on Application of the Federal Securities Laws to Certain Types of Crypto Assets," aims to resolve years of ambiguity about crypto asset classification. The SEC and CFTC are progressing toward harmonized regulation, reflecting a meaningful shift toward structured oversight.

### Legislative Landscape

- **GENIUS Act** (signed July 17, 2025): First comprehensive federal stablecoin framework. However, New York prosecutors have raised alarms that the law fails to protect fraud victims and gives legal cover to companies profiting from fraud. Tether launched USAT, a US-regulated dollar-backed stablecoin, to comply.
- The SEC has halted 12 enforcement cases, signaling the regulatory posture shift under the current administration
- Indiana enacted a law formally defining cryptocurrency in state statute
- **MiCA** is now fully operational in the EU

### Institutional Adoption

- **Corporate Treasury BTC Holdings:** 172+ publicly traded companies held Bitcoin in Q3 2025, up 40% QoQ, collectively holding ~1,075,000 BTC (4.8% of circulating supply)
- **Strategy (MicroStrategy):** Holds 660,624 BTC valued at ~$44.5B at current prices (down from $60.8B at higher price levels)
- **Institutional Maturation:** Bitcoin's $1.35T market cap stability is being characterized as "institutional maturation" rather than a bear market -- suggesting the asset class has structurally different holder behavior than prior cycles
- Spot Bitcoin ETFs have seen cumulative net inflows exceeding $57B since launch, with total AUM approaching $130B

### Tether Legal Risk

A New York federal judge approved class-action certification in a market manipulation lawsuit against Tether and Bitfinex on March 6. This is a material legal threat to the $187B USDT ecosystem and was cited as a catalyst for Friday's selloff.
