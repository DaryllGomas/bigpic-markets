The S&P 500 went negative for 2026 and the week ended in broad risk-off positioning across equities, with only the Energy sector posting gains.

## Weekend Developments

**THIS IS THE MOST CRITICAL SECTION -- THESE EVENTS WILL MOVE MONDAY'S OPEN.**

### Oil Futures Rocket Past $100 (Sunday Night, March 8)

As of Sunday evening futures open, WTI crude has surged past $100/bbl -- up ~18% from Friday's close of ~$90.90. Oil has gone from $66/bbl on February 20 to above $108 in the Sunday night session. This is the dominant market-moving development heading into Monday.

**Key developments over the weekend:**
- Qatar's energy minister indicated Gulf producers may need to call force majeure, potentially shutting down production -- which could send oil to $150/bbl
- Roughly 9 million barrels/day are off the market due to facilities being hit or producers taking precautionary measures
- Ships carrying ~20 million barrels/day remain stranded in the Persian Gulf, unable to transit the Strait of Hormuz
- US Energy Secretary Chris Wright said disruption would last "weeks, certainly not months" -- attempting to provide reassurance that the US will destroy Iran's ability to attack tankers
- National average gas price: $3.45/gallon (up 16% in one week per AAA)

### S&P 500 Futures Collapse

- S&P 500 futures: **down ~1.5%** on Sunday night
- Dow futures: **down ~1.9%**
- VIX spiked ~50% over the past week to **$29.49**, and will likely open significantly higher Monday

### Tesla NHTSA Deadline (Monday, March 9)

Tesla faces a critical NHTSA deadline on March 9 to submit FSD safety data (crash data, video, EDR, and CAN bus files). NHTSA opened Preliminary Evaluation PE25012 after connecting 58+ incidents (later 80+) to FSD-equipped vehicles. Tesla's Austin robotaxi service has been involved in 14 crashes since launching in June 2025. An adverse ruling could halt the Austin rollout and materially impact TSLA's AI/robotaxi premium. This was already flagged as a key risk heading into the weekend.

### Artemis II Launch Delayed Again

NASA's Artemis II crewed lunar mission, originally set to launch March 6, was pushed back again due to a helium flow issue. Negative for LUNR sentiment in the near term, though the company's IM-2 lunar lander successfully touched down on March 6 (landing ~400 meters from target at Mons Mouton).

### FOMC Context

The March 17-18 FOMC meeting is now 10 days away -- the first SEP meeting of 2026 with updated dot plots. The stagflation backdrop (weak jobs + rising oil/inflation) creates a policy dilemma. CME pricing was 60% hold / 38% cut before the jobs report; the weak NFP + hot wages combo makes the path forward unclear. This will dominate macro narrative next week.

### Calendar Note: Triple Witch Positioning Starts Monday

Per our structural calendar, T-9 for the March 20 Triple Witch begins Monday, March 9. Historically, positioning from T-9 to T-1 averages +0.82%. The pre-expiration rally pattern may provide a floor IF geopolitical panic subsides, but this cycle's macro backdrop is uniquely hostile.
