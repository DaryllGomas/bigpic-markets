This Week's Take

The Market Demands Proof — And It’s Not Satisfied

The week ending February 27 was a decisive inflection toward risk-off. Hot January PPI data (+0.5% MoM headline vs. +0.3% expected) collided with growing “AI fatigue” — epitomized by NVIDIA’s paradoxical selloff (-5.5%) despite blowout earnings ($68.1B revenue, +73% YoY) — and unresolved US-Iran nuclear talks to produce broad equity weakness. The S&P 500 closed at 6,878.88 (-0.4%), the Dow shed 521 points (-1.05%), and the Russell 2000 fell -1.9% as small caps bore the brunt of tightening fears. The defining feature was a pronounced defensive rotation: utilities, staples, healthcare, and energy outperformed while technology and financials lagged.

The bond market told a growth-scare story. The 10-year yield broke decisively below 4% to 3.97% (-11 bps weekly), while the 2-year plunged to 3.38% (-36 bps) — its lowest since August 2022. The 2s10s spread exploded to +59 bps, the widest since January 2022, in a textbook bull steepening that signals the market pricing in economic weakness faster than the Fed is willing to acknowledge. Yet the Fed remains hawkish: CME FedWatch pricing a 94% chance of a March hold. The tension between the bond market’s growth-scare pricing and the Fed’s inflation-fighting posture is the dominant macro dynamic heading into the March 17-18 FOMC.

Commodities confirmed the risk-off/inflation-hedging dynamic. Gold surged to ~$5,236/oz (+2.7% weekly, +20% YTD, +83% YoY) on central bank buying and safe-haven demand. WTI crude rose to $67.02 (+3.2%) on the Iran diplomatic breakdown. Critical minerals flashed supply-side catalysts: Zimbabwe’s surprise lithium export ban spiked prices +5.4%, and NdPr rare earths hit $123/kg (+14% weekly) — crossing the $110/kg threshold that triggers US government price support for MP Materials.

KEY SIGNAL: NVDA -5.5% despite $68.1B blowout — the market is rotating from “promise” to “proof”

Risk Radar

Bond Market vs. Fed Collision

2s10s at +59 bps and three cuts priced by year-end directly contradicts the Fed’s hawkish-hold posture. The March FOMC dot plot will either validate the bond market’s growth-scare pricing or force a painful repricing of rate expectations.

AI Fatigue Deepening

NVIDIA’s -5.5% selloff despite blowout earnings signals a sentiment regime change from “promise” to “proof.” If the rotation continues, mega-cap AI semis could see further multiple compression.

Nonfarm Payrolls Binary Event

Waller flagged February jobs as the “decision point” for March. Payroll revisions suggesting 2025 employment actually declined make the March 6 report the most-watched of the quarter.

Iran Nuclear Breakdown

Unresolved US-Iran nuclear talks keep $8-10 geopolitical premium in Brent. 20M bbl/day flows through Strait of Hormuz. Oil spike could re-accelerate inflation at worst possible time.

Critical Minerals Supply Shocks

Zimbabwe lithium ban and NdPr crossing $110/kg DOD trigger in same week reflects global scramble for supply chain security. These supply-side shocks are not priced into consensus.

International Outperformance

European indices at record highs; MSCI World ex-US outperforming for the ninth consecutive month. Persistent US-to-international rotation could accelerate if March FOMC disappoints.
