## Weekend Developments

### BREAKING: Trump Orders Naval Blockade of Strait of Hormuz (April 12)

**This is the single most important development for markets heading into Monday.**

On Saturday, April 12, Vice President JD Vance announced that U.S.-Iran peace talks hosted by Pakistan had collapsed. Iran's demands -- control of the Strait of Hormuz, war reparations, and a regional ceasefire including Lebanon -- were unacceptable to the U.S. delegation, which insisted on Iran surrendering nuclear weapons ambitions.

President Trump immediately declared a U.S. naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz via Truth Social: *"Effective immediately, the United States Navy, the Finest in the World, will begin the process of BLOCKADING any and all Ships trying to enter, or leave, the Strait of Hormuz."*

**Market impact (Sunday evening / early Monday):**
- **Brent crude** surged ~7.8% toward $103/barrel; WTI jumped ~7%
- **European natural gas futures** jumped as much as 17%
- **S&P 500 futures** fell ~1% (to ~6,766), erasing roughly a third of the week's gains
- **Goldman Sachs warns** Brent could average $100+ throughout 2026 if the Strait remains closed another month, and $120/bbl in Q3 if longer

**Strategic significance:** The Strait of Hormuz facilitates ~20 million barrels/day of oil transit -- roughly 20% of global seaborne oil trade. This is described as the largest disruption to world energy supply since the 1970s oil crisis. The two-week ceasefire that powered the week's rally lasted only four days before breaking down; Iran's parliamentary speaker cited three of ten ceasefire clauses as already violated (Israeli strikes in Lebanon, drone over Fars province, enrichment rights denied).

**Expected Monday impact:** Risk-off positioning likely. Energy stocks (XOM, CVX, OXY, DVN, FANG) should gap higher. Defense primes (LMT, NOC, RTX) may benefit. Technology, consumer discretionary, and airlines face headwinds from oil-driven inflation fears. VIX likely spikes above 25.
