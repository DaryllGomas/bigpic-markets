## Regulation & Institutional

### SEC/CFTC Landmark Joint Interpretation (March 2026)

The most significant regulatory development of the year: On March 17, 2026, the SEC and CFTC jointly published a **68-page interpretive release classifying 16 major cryptocurrencies as digital commodities** (not securities), to be regulated by the CFTC. The release establishes a five-part token taxonomy:
1. Digital commodities
2. Digital collectibles
3. Digital tools
4. Stablecoins
5. Digital securities

This followed the SEC-CFTC MOU signed March 11, 2026, committing both agencies to "clarify, coordinate, and harmonize" crypto regulation. The Senate Banking Committee is expected to hold a markup for market-structure legislation in April 2026.

**SEC Safe Harbor proposal:** Chairman Atkins previewed a startup exemption (up to $5M over 4 years) and a fundraising exemption (up to $75M annually) for qualifying crypto offerings.

**SEC approved additional Bitcoin ETF options trading** on April 4, enabling covered call strategies on IBIT and GBTC -- potentially boosting yields 8-12% annualized for income-focused portfolios.
