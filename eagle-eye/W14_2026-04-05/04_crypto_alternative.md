**Key Levels:**

- **Resistance:** $2,100 (local), $2,243 (0.382 Fibonacci — requires clean break for bullish confirmation), $2,400

**Network Developments — Glamsterdam Upgrade (June 2026 target):**
The Glamsterdam upgrade is Ethereum's most significant hard fork since the Merge, targeting:
- Gas limit increase from 60M to 200M per block
- Throughput scaling to ~10,000 TPS (from current ~30 TPS)
- Enshrined Proposer-Builder Separation (ePBS) for MEV reform and decentralization
- Block-Level Access Lists (BALs) for parallel transaction execution
- 78% reduction in gas fees

Devnet testing is underway with public testnets and dual audit phases planned for spring. This is an "L1-first" upgrade designed to strengthen the base layer, contrasting with recent L2-focused upgrades. If successfully deployed in June, it could provide a meaningful re-rating catalyst for ETH.

**Market Cap:** ~$252 billion

## Regulation & Institutional

### CLARITY Act — Four-Way Deadlock

The Digital Asset Market Clarity Act (H.R. 3633), which passed the House in July 2025 with bipartisan support (294-134), is stalled in the Senate amid a four-way fight over stablecoin yield provisions, jurisdictional boundaries between SEC and CFTC, existing regulatory survival, and who oversees the framework. A Senate Banking Committee markup is targeted for the second half of April 2026, with an SEC roundtable scheduled for April 16. This remains the single most important piece of crypto legislation in the pipeline.

### SEC/CFTC Joint Classification

On March 17, 2026, the SEC and CFTC issued a joint final rule classifying digital assets into five categories:
1. **Digital commodities** (BTC, ETH, SOL, ADA, XRP, and 11 others)
2. **Digital collectibles** (NFTs)
3. **Digital tools** (utility tokens)
4. **Stablecoins**
5. **Digital securities**

This was a landmark clarification that enabled the XRP ETF approvals and provides the regulatory framework the market has long demanded — though the CLARITY Act must pass to make it permanent under federal statute.

### Charles Schwab — Spot Crypto Trading Imminent

On April 3, 2026, Charles Schwab confirmed it remains on track to launch "Schwab Crypto" in H1 2026, starting with BTC and ETH. The firm will offer spot trading through Charles Schwab Premier Bank, with a phased rollout beginning in Q2 2026. With $11.9 trillion in client assets, Schwab's entry represents a major distribution channel expansion for crypto. Available to US residents except New York and Louisiana.

### Corporate Treasury / Institutional

- Public companies now collectively hold **over 1.7 million BTC** (~8% of total supply). In several quarters of 2025, corporate purchases exceeded ETF inflows.
- **PwC** declared institutional crypto adoption has passed the "point of reversibility" — stablecoins, tokenized cash, and on-chain settlement are now embedded in banking and asset management workflows.
- **Ripple** launched the first treasury management system with native digital asset capabilities, integrating RLUSD and XRP alongside traditional cash.
- **FASB ASU 2023-08** (fair-value accounting for crypto) is now fully effective, removing the historical cost-minus-impairment barrier that previously deterred corporate treasury adoption.
