## Market Overview

U.S. equities snapped a five-week losing streak, staging the strongest weekly rebound in months despite the Iran conflict continuing to dominate the macro backdrop. Markets were closed Friday April 3 for Good Friday; the final trading session was Thursday April 2.

| Index | Close (Apr 2) | Weekly Change | YTD |
|-------|--------------|---------------|-----|
| S&P 500 | 6,582.69 | +3.4% | -4.6% |
| Nasdaq Composite | 21,879.18 | +4.4% | ~-5% |
| DJIA | 46,504.67 | +3.0% | ~-5% |
| Russell 2000 | 2,528.26 | +0.6% | ~Flat |

The bulk of the weekly gains were concentrated in a single session on Monday March 30 / Tuesday March 31, when unconfirmed reports suggested Iranian President Pezeshkian was open to ending the war with guarantees. The S&P 500 gained 2.91% and the Nasdaq advanced 3.83% on that day alone -- the best single-day performance since May 2025 for each index. The Magnificent 7 outperformed with a 5.2% weekly advance.

However, gains faded into mid-week after President Trump delivered a national address on Wednesday vowing the U.S. will continue to "strike Iran extremely hard" without offering a clear timetable for ending hostilities. Oil surged on the remarks, with WTI settling at $111.54/bbl (+11.4%) and Brent at $109.03 (+7.8%) -- the highest since 2022.

The March nonfarm payrolls report, released on the Good Friday holiday, showed +178,000 jobs (vs. 60,000 expected), rebounding from February's -133,000 decline. The unemployment rate edged down to 4.3%, and average hourly earnings rose 3.5% YoY -- the lowest annual wage growth since May 2021.

**Key narrative:** Markets remain in a tug-of-war between ceasefire optimism (which produces violent short-covering rallies) and the reality of an ongoing military conflict with the world's 4th-largest oil producer amid Strait of Hormuz disruptions. The S&P 500 sits below both its 50-day MA (6,789) and 200-day MA (6,642), signaling a deteriorating technical picture despite the weekly bounce.

## Weekend Developments

**Iran conflict -- No ceasefire in sight.** Iran rejected a US-proposed 48-hour ceasefire on April 3, along with broader 15-point peace terms requiring nuclear curbs, Strait of Hormuz reopening, and proxy force cessation. Polymarket ceasefire probability fell to 1.1% for resolution by April 7, down from 2% the prior day. The market consensus now heavily favors a protracted conflict with 75% probability assigning resolution no earlier than December 31. Former JPMorgan chief strategist Marko Kolanovic warned investors against going long into a holiday weekend during which "hostilities could escalate and the U.S. could put boots on the ground."

**SpaceX confidentially files for IPO.** On April 1, SpaceX filed a confidential S-1 with the SEC, targeting a potential $1.75 trillion valuation. Public S-1 expected late April or May, with a June Nasdaq listing planned. This would be the largest IPO in history by a wide margin, following SpaceX's recent merger with xAI ($1.25T combined). Space-related stocks (RKLB, PL, LUNR) rallied on the news.

**OpenAI closes record $122B funding round.** Valued at $852 billion post-money, with Amazon ($50B), NVIDIA ($30B), and SoftBank ($30B) as anchor investors. OpenAI now generates $2B/month in revenue with 900M+ weekly active users. IPO anticipated later in 2026.

**Jobs report surprise.** The March BLS report (released April 3) showed +178K jobs vs. 60K expected. Healthcare added 76K. While the headline beat was strong, wage growth deceleration (3.5% YoY, lowest since May 2021) and a sharp labor force reduction suggest the recovery may be narrower than headline numbers imply.
