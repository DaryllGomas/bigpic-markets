# Eagle Eye W23 — Rates, Credit & Fed
**Snapshot as of Sunday, June 7, 2026.** Covers the trading week ending Friday June 5, 2026, plus any breaking news through the weekend.

*Educational research — not investment advice. Cash/Treasury markets are closed over the weekend; the "live" narrative is Friday's close plus the unresolved Iran situation.*

**The one-line story:** A blowout May jobs report (172k vs ~80k expected) released Friday June 5 detonated what was left of 2026 rate-cut pricing. Combined with an Iran-war energy/inflation shock, fed funds futures now lean toward a *hike* by year-end rather than a cut. Yields jumped, the curve bear-flattened, and risk assets sold off hard (Nasdaq -4%, ~$1T wiped from semis). New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh chairs his first FOMC on June 16-17 into this mess.

**What happened intraweek:**
- The week opened tense: on **June 1, 2026**, Iran's Tasnim agency reported negotiators were halting U.S. talks and threatened to "completely close the Strait of Hormuz." **Net:** A classic bear-flattener — the front end led the selloff as the market priced *out* cuts (and partly *in* hikes), while the long end moved less. Yields are at/near multi-month highs and have broken out of their prior range (Schwab/Fidelity).

## Fed Watch

**Leadership:** **Kevin Warsh is the new Fed Chair** — confirmed by the Senate **54-45 on May 13, 2026** (closest modern-era vote), sworn in **May 22, 2026**. **June 16-17, 2026 is his first FOMC as chair.** Jerome Powell's chairmanship ended May 15, 2026; he remains a governor and has warned publicly that White House pressure for lower rates "risks damaging" Fed independence (NBC News).

**June 16-17 meeting pricing (post-jobs):** Overwhelmingly a hold at 3.50-3.75%. CME FedWatch readings cited in the ~97% "no change" range for June after the jobs print (one earlier/generic snapshot showed ~70% hold / 28% cut / 2% hike — that looks pre-jobs-report; treat ~97% hold as the post-print figure). June is a **SEP meeting** (new dot plot + projections) — the key event, especially for Warsh's first set of dots.

**The big repricing — cuts → hikes:** Entering 2026 the market expected ~2-3 cuts. After Friday's jobs report, **odds of a Fed *hike* by year-end 2026 rose to ~70%** (CNBC, "Hot jobs report puts Fed cuts further out of reach," Jun 5, 2026). Summer meetings (Jul 29 / Sep 16) are described as coin-flips leaning to no action. This is a dramatic hawkish shift driven by (1) a resilient labor market and (2) the Iran-war energy/inflation shock.

**Inflation backdrop:** Headline **PCE hit 3.8% y/y in April 2026** (highest in ~3 years), largely energy-driven; core/underlying measures excluding tariffs and one-offs are reported near ~2-2.8%. The **2026 Iran war / Strait of Hormuz disruption** lifted Q1 2026 headline inflation ~1.7pp annualized, pushed gasoline above $4/gal, and the Beige Book called Mideast energy costs the "primary driver of inflationary pressures."

**Fed speakers (verified this window):**
- **Gov. Christopher Waller (May 22, 2026 lecture):** Hawkish lean. "Inflation is not headed in the right direction." Notes April PCE 3.8% is the highest in three years; markets were "underpricing the risk of prolonged high energy prices." Position: **hold steady near-term**, and **remove the "easing bias"** because "a rate cut is no more likely in the future than a rate increase."
- **VC for Supervision Michelle Bowman (May 29, 2026 speech):** Dovish lean / "look through." Comfortable looking through temporary energy-driven inflation "provided… one-off tariff effects wane," wary that "waiting to lower the policy rate could lead to persistent labor market damage." Wants to keep forward guidance. Reportedly **aligned with Chair Warsh**, who favors lower rates but dislikes forward guidance.
- The committee is openly split: Bowman/Warsh (dovish-lean) vs. Waller and the April hawks.

**FOMC minutes (April 28-29, 2026 meeting; released ~May 21, 2026):** Held rates; **8-4 vote, the most dissents since October 1992.** Dovish dissent: **Stephen Miran** (wanted a 25 bp cut). Hawkish dissenters: **Beth Hammack, Neel Kashkari, Lorie Logan** (opposed the easing-bias statement, wanted symmetry / left the door open to a hike). Minutes showed a clear shift away from an easing bias, with many participants preferring to drop the easing language and a majority seeing a hike "likely warranted if inflation persists." **No new minutes were released in the June 1-5 window** (next minutes follow the June 16-17 meeting, ~July 8).

## Credit Markets

**Levels (approximate, early-June 2026):**
- **IG corporate OAS:** ~**80 bp** (BBB ~100 bp, AA ~50 bp) — historically tight, near multi-decade tights.
- **HY OAS:** ~**285 bp** (one index quoted ~257 bp on the Bloomberg HY 2% Issuer Capped) — also tight by historical standards.

**Direction:** Despite Friday's sharp equity selloff (Nasdaq -4%, ~$1T off semis), credit held up notably well into/through the week. HY was described as having a "constructive" week, opening firm with a stable tone; month-end rebalancing dominated flows, supply was manageable, and default expectations remain contained (Nuveen weekly). So far this looks like an equity/rates event more than a credit event — spreads firm, not blowing out.

**Structural theme to watch:** A record wave of **AI / data-center-linked issuance** (Meta's $27B datacenter-backed deal in late 2025; Meta+Oracle+Google+Amazon >$100B in Q4 2025 alone; 2026 IG supply forecasts up to ~$2.25T, +35% y/y). Consensus expects this supply to drive *moderate* spread widening and dispersion later in 2026 rather than a violent decompression, given strong all-in yields and demand. This directly intersects the AI-infrastructure and nuclear/power theses (hyperscaler capex funded increasingly in credit markets).

## Liquidity

- **TGA (Treasury General Account):** ~**$874.1B**, within Treasury's $700-900B target band (Daily Treasury Statement / FRED WTREGEN).
- **ON RRP:** Effectively drained — quoted **~$3.3B** (and "under $1B" in some readings) vs. the >$2.3T peak in late 2022. Money-market cash has fully migrated into bills/short paper. No spare liquidity buffer left here.
- **Fed balance sheet:** ~**$6.71T**; **QT continues at ~$25B/month** of runoff (H.4.1, week of June 4, 2026).
- **Bank lending (SLOOS, April 2026 survey):** Modest net tightening of C&I standards for firms of all sizes; higher risk premiums, tighter covenants/collateral. Consumer standards roughly unchanged (auto/cards) with modest tightening in "other consumer." Net tighter standards across all NDFI categories over the past year. Not a credit crunch, but the lending impulse is mildly restrictive.
- **QT-end watch:** With RRP empty and reserves the next buffer, the question is whether Warsh's June 16-17 FOMC signals winding down QT. That is the next genuine liquidity inflection.

## Weekend Developments (Jun 6-7, 2026)

No new market prints over the weekend (cash markets closed). The two live threads carried into next week:

1. **Iran / Strait of Hormuz ceasefire — unresolved and volatile.** Late May saw oil tumble ~20% from 2026 peaks on optimism over a U.S.-Iran 60-day ceasefire MOU; that MOU still needs President Trump's sign-off. But June 1 brought renewed escalation (talks halted, Hormuz-closure threat, strikes including Iranian missiles at Kuwait). UBS noted "little evidence" of improved vessel traffic. This is the single biggest swing factor for the inflation/rates path — any confirmed Hormuz disruption re-ignites the energy-inflation spiral and the hike narrative; a firm ceasefire would let yields ease and revive the "look-through" (dovish) camp.
2. **Jobs-report aftershock + June FOMC setup.** Friday's hot print leaves a market leaning toward a possible hike, going into Warsh's first (SEP) meeting June 16-17 — convergence week with Triple Witch (Jun 19) and the run-up to Russell reconstitution (Jun 26). Watch the dot plot for whether the median now shows zero cuts or a hike for 2026.
