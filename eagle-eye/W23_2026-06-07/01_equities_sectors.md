## Market Overview

The week was a story of two halves: record highs early, then a violent reversal that ended a multi-week winning streak. The S&P 500 set a record close to open June (Sun/Mon June 1) and finished above 7,600 for the first time on Tuesday June 2 as the AI/chip rally extended. By Friday June 5, a hot jobs report and a semiconductor-led selloff erased the gains and turned the week red.

**Friday was the worst day for US stocks since October 2025.** The S&P 500 lost 2.6%, the Nasdaq dropped 4.2% (its worst session since April 2025), and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) fell ~10.3% in a single day. Reporting cited roughly $1 trillion of market value wiped from the semiconductor complex on Friday.

**Two catalysts converged:**

1. **Hot May jobs report (released Fri 6/5).** Nonfarm payrolls rose +172,000 (vs. ~80,000 Dow Jones consensus — roughly double expectations), unemployment steady at 4.3%, average hourly earnings +0.3% m/m / +3.4% y/y. The strong print pushed out Fed rate-cut hopes, lifted Treasury yields, and triggered a "good news is bad news" reaction. (BLS Employment Situation, 2026-06-05; CNBC jobs report 2026-06-05.)
2. **Broadcom guidance disappointment (reported after close 6/3).** AVGO beat on Q2 FY26 results but guided Q3 AI semiconductor revenue to ~$16B vs. ~$17.2B expected and declined to raise its $100B+ AI target — a "sell-the-news" reaction in a richly valued stock. This seeded a two-day chip rout (6/4 and 6/5).

**Rates & sentiment:** The 10-year Treasury yield rose to ~4.54% on Friday. Prediction markets shifted to ~93%+ odds the Fed holds at the June 16-17 FOMC meeting, with Polymarket pricing ~57% odds of zero cuts for all of 2026 — a sharp repricing from earlier in the year. CNN's Fear & Greed Index flipped into "fear" after sitting in "greed" since mid-April.

**YTD context (approximate; pre-Friday YTD as of 5/29 was Nasdaq +16.3%, S&P +11.3%, Dow +6.9% per slickcharts-cited data).** After the down week, rough YTD stands near: Nasdaq ~+11%, S&P 500 ~+8.5%, Dow ~+6.4%. *(YTD figures are approximate — slickcharts direct fetch was blocked; treat as estimates.)*

## Earnings

A heavy week of marquee reports, with a recurring pattern: **beats that still sold off** because expectations in high-flying names were too high.

- **Broadcom (AVGO)** — reported after close **6/3**. Q2 FY26 adj. EPS $2.44 (vs. $2.39 est.), revenue $22.19B (vs. $22.13B est.); AI chip sales $10.8B, +143% y/y. BUT Q3 AI-semi guide ~$16B missed the ~$17.2B est., infrastructure software $7.18B missed ~$7.32B est., and CEO Hock Tan kept (didn't raise) the longer-term AI target. **Stock −~14% on 6/4, then −7.9% on 6/5.** Primary trigger of the chip rout. (CNBC AVGO earnings 6/3; Yahoo/TIKR 6/4.)
- **CrowdStrike (CRWD)** — reported after close **6/3**. Q1 FY27 revenue $1.39B (+26% y/y), non-GAAP EPS $1.10 (vs. $1.07 est.), ARR $5.51B (+24%), record Q1 net-new ARR $256M (+32%); raised FY net-new-ARR growth guide. Announced a **4-for-1 stock split for July**. BUT billings grew only ~18% (below expectations) and the stock had run +~65% YTD (trading ~$747 pre-print vs. ~$564 avg target). **Shares fell ~11% post-earnings.** (CrowdStrike IR 6/3; CNBC; Yahoo Finance.) Relevant to the cybersecurity thesis (Tier 1 name).
- **Lululemon (LULU)** — reported **6/4**. Net revenue $2.5B (+4%; +2% constant-currency). North America revenue −3%, comps −6%; China Mainland +30% (+23% cc). Gross margin −410 bps (−280 bps tariff impact, −140 bps deleverage). Management cited negative brand commentary hurting traffic and weak product launches. A clear consumer/retail soft spot.
- **Also reported this week:** Hewlett Packard Enterprise, Costco, Veeva Systems, Five Below, Macy's, Inditex, Getlink. (Detailed numbers not pulled — flag for follow-up if needed.)

**Cross-read:** In a Fed-repricing tape, even strong AI/security results couldn't hold bids — the market punished any guidance shortfall or decelerating forward metric (AVGO AI guide, CRWD billings). This is the "proof phase / priced-for-perfection" dynamic the AI thesis warns about.
