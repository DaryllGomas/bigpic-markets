## Resolved: the "Hormuz closed but oil only $92" apparent contradiction

Agent 3 framed the Strait as **"closed since March 4, largest oil disruption in history"**; Agents 1–2 framed it as a **fragile-ceasefire/brinkmanship** with oil down ~20% from its 2026 peak. These looked contradictory but are **both correct facets of one situation**, confirmed by independent search:

- The Strait was declared closed Mar 4 2026 and ~20% of world oil flow was disrupted (tanker traffic fell ~70% initially, 150+ ships anchored).
- A US–Iran ceasefire Apr 7-8 then a **brinkmanship phase** followed. Crude **retreated double-digit percentages on optimism the Strait would reopen** plus demand-destruction/Fed-hike fears — which is why oil sits at ~$92 (Brent still +48% YoY) rather than at panic highs.
- Jun 1 2026 Iran halted talks and vowed to fully re-block Hormuz, reviving the premium (oil +3–4% on the week into Jun 5).

→ EAGLE_EYE.md synthesizes both: an active ~100-day war, Strait disrupted/largely shut since March, oil elevated-but-off-peak because the market keeps pricing a reopening that keeps failing to arrive.
