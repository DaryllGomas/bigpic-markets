# Eagle Eye — Agent 4: Crypto & Alternative
**Point-in-time snapshot: Sunday, June 7, 2026** (prices noted as Friday close vs. current weekend)
**Week recapped: trading week ending Friday, June 5, 2026**

> **Headline:** Crypto just closed one of its worst weeks in years. Bitcoin fell ~16% on the week to a Friday June 5 close near **$61,500**, breaking below $60K intraday to its **lowest level since October 2024** and now sitting **~51% below its October 6, 2025 all-time high of $126,198**. The selloff was driven by a **record 13-day spot-BTC-ETF outflow streak (~$4.4B)**, the unwind of the late-February Middle East "war premium," Strategy's first BTC sale since 2022, sticky-inflation / higher-for-longer Fed repricing, and heavy leveraged liquidations (~$3B over two days mid-week). As of the weekend, BTC has bounced modestly to ~$62.8K and ETH to ~$1,640.

The single worst week (7 sessions from ~Jun 2) saw ~**$3.4B** out — the largest weekly outflow on record. ETH-ETF AUM ~$9.78B. ([CoinDesk, Jun 5, 2026](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/06/05/bitcoin-and-ether-etfs-end-record-multi-billion-outflow-streak))

## Regulation & Institutional

**Regulation — CLARITY Act advances (this week's key catalyst):**
- The **Digital Asset Market Clarity Act (H.R. 3633) was reported out of the Senate Banking Committee on June 1, 2026** — a real procedural milestone (the House passed it 294-134 in July 2025; it had been stalled over stablecoin-yield disputes). The bill would give the **CFTC exclusive jurisdiction over digital-commodity spot markets** while the SEC keeps investment-contract assets. Prediction markets price ~72% odds of a 2026 signing, but the practical window narrows as midterm dynamics take over. ([Congress.gov H.R.3633](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/3633/text); [Fortune, May 13, 2026](https://fortune.com/2026/05/13/crypto-clarity-act-senate-markup/); [DL News](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/regulation/key-dates-for-us-crypto-regulation-in-2026/))
- Friction point: a Tillis–Alsobrooks compromise on **stablecoin yield**; Coinbase had pulled support over a proposed ban on stablecoin rewards, and bank lobbies call the compromise too industry-friendly.

**Institutional / corporate-treasury — the "premium era is over":**
- **Strategy (MSTR) sold BTC for the first time since Dec 2022** — a *symbolic* 32 BTC for ~$2.5M (avg ~$77,135), filed ~Jun 1, used to fund STRC preferred dividends. Tiny in size but a sentiment shock: it cracked the "never sell" narrative that defined the treasury-company trade. Strategy still held ~843,706 BTC (avg cost ~$75,699 — now **underwater** at current spot). ([CoinDesk, Jun 1, 2026](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/06/01/strategy-sold-32-btc-for-usd2-5-million-in-late-may-filing-shows); [CNBC, Jun 1, 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/01/strategy-shares-fall-after-selling-2point5-million-in-bitcoin-its-first-sale-since-2022.html))
- **Treasury-company shakeout:** investors are fleeing weaker crypto-treasury firms ("premium era is over"); analysts expect only high-quality BTC/ETH treasuries (Strategy, MARA, Metaplanet, Twenty One Capital) to survive 2026. Grayscale has flagged "MicroStrategy cracks." ([DL News](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/markets/investors-scramble-to-pick-winners-amoung-smouldering-crypto-treasuries/))
- Longer-term institutional adoption framing remains intact (MiCA, MAS stablecoin regime, tokenized Treasuries; ~$147B BTC in ETFs, ~$100B in treasuries) — but near-term flows are decidedly risk-off. ([B2Broker](https://b2broker.com/news/institutional-adoption-of-crypto/))

**Quantum × crypto crossover (ties to /research/quantum/THESIS.md):**
- Not a price driver this week, but thematically live: **CoinDesk (May 24, 2026) — "AI is speeding up the quantum threat to crypto."** Researchers note three papers in ~a year cut the projected qubits needed to attack ECC by ~an order of magnitude. An Apr 24, 2026 demo broke a 15-bit ECC key on public quantum hardware (Bitcoin uses 256-bit; still 10,000–100,000x from a real threat). **BIP-360 (Pay-to-Merkle-Root)** remains the leading Bitcoin quantum-hardening proposal; ETH/Coinbase building phased PQC roadmaps. This matches the thesis-file note on ~$718B BTC in quantum-vulnerable P2PK addresses and a 5–10yr upgrade path. ([CoinDesk, May 24, 2026](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/05/24/ai-is-speeding-up-the-quantum-threat-to-crypto-security-experts-warn); [Project Eleven 2026 report](https://www.projecteleven.com/blog/the-quantum-threat-to-blockchains---2026-report))
