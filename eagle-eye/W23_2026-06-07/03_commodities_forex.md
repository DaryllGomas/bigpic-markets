### OPEC+ supply

OPEC+ is fractured. **The UAE shock-exited the group**, and the remaining seven (Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman) met **May 3, 2026** and agreed a symbolic **+188,000 bbl/d hike for June** (down from +206k the prior month) ([CNBC May 3](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/03/opec-announces-188000-barrels-per-day-output-increase-.html); [Al Jazeera May 3](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/5/3/opec-announces-symbolic-oil-output-rise-during-strait-of-hormuz-closure)). The increase was "symbolic" because Hormuz closure meant Gulf barrels couldn't physically reach market anyway. **The full 41st OPEC+ Ministerial Meeting is set for June 7, 2026** — see Weekend Developments.

### Freight / supply-chain (Hormuz risk premium)

The war embedded an enormous **tanker freight + war-risk-insurance** premium that is still unwinding:
- **VLCC TD3C (Mideast Gulf→China) hit ~$474,000/day on April 17, 2026** — ~4x the pre-war ~$117,360/day ([SAFETY4SEA](https://safety4sea.com/vlcc-insurance-jumps-as-freight-rates-escalate-due-to-tensions/)). Day rates spiked as high as **~$770,000–$800,000/day** in the war's first week.
- **War-risk premiums jumped from 0.25% to 1% of hull value** — adding **$200k–$360k per VLCC voyage**. All 12 International Group P&I Clubs issued 72-hour war-cover cancellation notices effective March 5 ([CNBC Mar 3](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/03/middle-east-crisis-iran-us-shipping-oil-tankers-strait-of-hormuz.html)).
- As of late May, **600+ tankers stranded inside the Persian Gulf, ~240 waiting outside**; just **191 vessels crossed the strait in all of April** vs. ~3,000/month pre-war ([CNN Apr 29](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/29/world/iran-war-gulf-hormuz-shipping-maps-intl-vis)).

Any normalization of freight/insurance as Hormuz reopens is a **disinflationary tailwind** for goods and energy — a key thing to watch into late June.

## Weekend Developments

Two genuinely market-moving items sit right on the snapshot date — both must be watched at the cash open Monday June 8.

### 1. OPEC+ 41st Ministerial Meeting — **Sunday, June 7, 2026** ⚠️ LIVE TODAY

The **41st OPEC and Non-OPEC Ministerial Meeting is scheduled for June 7, 2026** — the day this report is dated ([EBC Financial — 2026 OPEC schedule](https://www.ebc.com/forex/when-is-the-next-opec-meeting-2026-schedule-and-impact); [Ultima Markets](https://www.ultimamarkets.com/academy/when-is-the-next-opec-meeting/)). This is a **semi-annual policy-setting meeting** where July+ quotas and baselines are decided — far more consequential than the monthly +188k tweak. Context going in: OPEC+ has been running symbolic hikes during the Hormuz closure, the UAE has exited the group, and the cartel signaled it would **gradually raise output to refill global markets once free Hormuz navigation is restored** ([The National May 3](https://www.thenationalnews.com/news/gulf/2026/05/03/opec-producers-agree-on-june-output-adjustment-and-reaffirm-market-stability-commitment/)). **The decision/communiqué was NOT yet available via search at writing** — flagged as a same-day event to verify. A larger-than-symbolic July hike (now that barrels can physically move) would be **bearish oil**; a cautious hold citing fragile Gulf security would be **supportive**.

### 2. Strait of Hormuz — limited reopening underway

Tanker traffic is **resuming on a limited basis** under Iranian-imposed routes/controls, the most important real-world de-risking signal:
- **Two LNG tankers crossed out of the Gulf** (bound for Pakistan and China) and **a supertanker carrying Iraqi crude exited the strait** after being stranded since February — reported mid/late May ([Gulf News](https://gulfnews.com/business/energy/is-strait-of-hormuz-now-open-more-oil-lng-tankers-cross-gulf-chokepoint-1.500552390); [Bloomberg May 17](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-17/hormuz-tracker-gridlock-persists-while-blocked-tanker-sails-on)).
- Volumes remain a **fraction of the pre-war ~15 Mbbl/d**; insurers stay cautious and the market continues pricing a prolonged Gulf risk premium.
- Most recent dated incident in the crisis timeline: the bulk carrier **_Lian Star_ intercepted May 30, 2026** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis)).

### 3. U.S.–Iran ceasefire deal — still unsigned

Negotiators reached a **60-day MOU to extend the ceasefire and open nuclear talks**, but **Trump had not formally approved it** as of the latest reporting, and on **May 25 he conditioned any deal on the Abraham Accords** ([Wikipedia — 2026 Iran war ceasefire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Iran_war_ceasefire); [CNBC May 29](https://www.cnbc.com/amp/2026/05/29/oil-prices-iran-ceasefire-us-trump-strait-hormuz-energy-costs.html)). **This is the single biggest two-way commodity catalyst.** Signature → oil/freight lower, dollar softer, gold steady-to-lower. Collapse → snap-back oil spike toward the prior $100–$126 zone.
