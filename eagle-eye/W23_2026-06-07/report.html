⛽ OPEC+ 41st Ministerial — Held Today, Sun June 7 (Decision Pending) • THE supply question
This is the cartel’s semi-annual policy-setting meeting (July+ quotas, 2027 baselines) — far more consequential than the monthly tweaks. Context going in: the UAE formally exited OPEC+ (effective ~May 1), and the remaining members agreed only a symbolic +188k bbl/d June hike because the Hormuz closure meant Gulf barrels couldn’t physically reach market. The June 7 communiqué was not yet available at compile — a larger-than-symbolic July hike (now that barrels can move) reads bearish oil; a cautious hold citing fragile Gulf security reads supportive.
🚢 Strait of Hormuz — Limited Reopening Underway
Tanker traffic is resuming on a restricted basis under Iranian-imposed controls: LNG carriers bound for Pakistan and China crossed out, and a long-stranded Iraqi-crude supertanker exited (mid/late-May reporting). Volumes remain a fraction of the pre-war ~15 Mbbl/d, and insurers stay cautious — the market is still pricing a prolonged Gulf risk premium. How fast Gulf tankers actually move is the third leg of the oil bet.
🛢️ U.S.–Iran 60-Day MOU — Still Unsigned • The single biggest two-way oil catalyst
Negotiators reached a memorandum to extend the ceasefire, reopen Hormuz (unrestricted shipping, mines cleared within 30 days), lift the naval blockade proportionally, and add nuclear commitments and sanctions waivers — but Trump had not given final approval as of the latest confirmed reporting (~May 28), and a real U.S. airstrike near Hormuz on May 28 showed the truce is not clean. Signature → crude/freight lower; collapse → a Hormuz-premium snap-back toward the prior $100–$126 zone.

Ⅱ The Macro Arc Is Well-Sourced: De-escalation Drove a Melt-Up — but It’s Stretched

U.S. equities closed May at record highs — the S&P 500 at 7,580.06 (May 29), its ninth straight weekly gain (longest since 2023), the Dow above 51,000, the Nasdaq +8% on the month — powered by AI infrastructure and by oil’s collapse (Brent −18–20% in May, its worst month since the 2020 Covid crash) as markets priced an end to the ~3-month 2026 Iran war. But the rally is stretched (index RSI ~73.6, breadth only ~59% above the 200-DMA, VIX 15.3), and the inflation backdrop is hot underneath it: April PCE ran 3.8% headline / 3.3% core, the firmest in years. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh (confirmed May 13) inherits a hawkish committee that has priced out essentially all 2026 cuts and now carries a live hike tail — the opposite of January’s “three cuts” script.

🌀 A tape priced for good news, overbought and thin on cushion — respect the trend, but expect air pockets, and confirm the June 1–5 close before extending it.

Ⅲ The Pivot for the Week Ahead Is Inflation, Not the Fed — Yet

Wednesday’s CPI (June 10) is the marquee release and, crucially, the last major inflation read before Warsh’s first FOMC on June 16–17, with Triple Witching + the S&P 500 quarterly rebalance (June 19) and the Russell reconstitution (June 26) stacked right behind it. A hot CPI on top of 3.8% PCE hardens the hike case into that dot plot; a cool print buys the soft-landing trade some room. One cross-asset tell worth watching: crypto is diverging risk-off even as equities sit at records — Bitcoin ~$73,500 (as of June 7), ~42% below its October 2025 all-time high, dragged by record May ETF outflows (≈−$2.3B) and 5%-handle long-end yields.

📅 The structure is unusual: no FOMC, no jobs report, no PCE this week — that makes CPI (Wed June 10) the single first-tier catalyst, carrying outsized weight into the June 16–26 structural cluster.
