## Market Overview

The week of June 22–26, 2026 was defined by a sharp **rotation out of mega-cap technology / AI and into small-caps and defensives**. The Nasdaq Composite posted its **fifth consecutive losing session on Friday** and fell ~4.6–4.7% on the week — its worst week in months — while the Russell 2000 small-cap index hit an **all-time high** mid-week and the Dow actually *rose*. This is the clearest "great rotation" signal of 2026 so far: large-cap growth is up only ~2% YTD while small-caps are up ~21–23% and large-cap value ~15%.

Two AI-specific catalysts drove the tech sell-off, both dated inside the window:
1. **OpenAI reportedly leaning toward delaying its IPO to 2027** (report surfaced Fri 6/26; OpenAI had filed confidentially on June 8). Advisors reportedly turned cautious after **SpaceX's June 12 IPO** rallied to ~$225 (briefly the 4th-most-valuable US company) then collapsed back toward/below its debut price, wiping out ~$600B and most open-market buyers' gains. SoftBank fell >12% in Tokyo on the OpenAI news.
2. **"AI fatigue"** — semiconductors fell ~7.9% on the week (Philadelphia Semiconductor Index's worst week since March 2025), with Nvidia and Alphabet leading mega-cap losers. Even a blowout Micron report (6/24) couldn't lift the group ("sell the news").

## Weekend Developments (6/27–6/28/2026)

**BIGGEST WEEKEND STORY: Major escalation in the ongoing 2026 US–Iran war around the Strait of Hormuz.** This is genuinely market-moving and is the dominant catalyst into Monday's open.

- **Saturday 6/27:** Iran hit the tanker **Kiku** (reported variously as Panamanian-flagged, carrying ~2M barrels of crude) with a one-way drone near the Strait of Hormuz. In response, **US Central Command struck ~10 Iranian military targets** (Sirik, Bandar-e Lengeh, Qeshm Island; surveillance/comms/air-defense/drone-storage/minelayer assets) — the **second straight day** of US strikes. (Sources: CNN, NPR, Fox, Washington Post, Al Jazeera, all dated 6/27–6/28/2026.)
- **Sunday 6/28:** Iran's IRGC launched **missiles/drones at US facilities in Kuwait and Bahrain**; Bahrain's foreign ministry confirmed it was targeted. President Trump accused Iran of violating the framework agreement; **Iran's IRGC threatened to halt negotiations entirely** if US strikes continued. Coverage frames this as "Iran war day 121," i.e., a conflict running since ~late February 2026, with the interim US–Iran ceasefire/framework now **in jeopardy.**
- **Conflicting signal:** One aggregator (ts2.tech) reported "the U.S. and Iran have agreed to halt strikes." This is **not corroborated** by NPR/CNN/WaPo, which describe continued strikes and a ceasefire at risk. Treat the "halt" claim as **unconfirmed**; the weight of reporting points to escalation, not de-escalation, as of Sunday evening.

**Sunday-night index futures (6/28):** modestly **higher** despite the headlines — Dow +0.2–0.3%, S&P 500 +0.4–0.5%, Nasdaq-100 +0.5–0.6%. Markets appear to be reading the strikes as contained so far.

**Oil (Sunday 6/28, current spot — NOT a Friday close):** Brent ~**$72.57** (+0.8%), WTI ~**$70** (+1.1%). Notably, oil remains well off its 2026 peak (down ~20% from peak per late-May reporting) — the market is pricing a *risk premium*, not a Hormuz closure.

**Other weekend/late-week corporate item:** IREN Ltd (IREN) fell 21.3% to $47.21, a fifth straight down day, despite a new >$50M/yr Golden State Warriors jersey sponsorship — a data-center/crypto-AI name caught in the tech downdraft.
