**Drivers (week of 6/22–6/26/2026):** The oil narrative flipped decisively from "supply shock" to "supply recovery." Following the US–Iran ceasefire/MoU (signed 6/15–6/17/2026), Strait of Hormuz transits accelerated and Persian Gulf exports recovered to ~75% of prewar levels. Trump stated (6/20/2026) there would be "no tolls" for Hormuz passage during/after the 60-day ceasefire. Net result: Brent/WTI both posted >10% weekly declines, the largest in a month.

## Weekend Developments (CRITICAL — post-Friday-close, 6/27–6/28/2026)

**The single biggest development: the US–Iran ceasefire fractured into open military exchange over the weekend, reversing the week's de-escalation trade.** This occurred AFTER Friday's commodity closes, so Friday prices do not reflect it — expect a risk-on geopolitical premium (oil up, gold up) at the Sunday-night/Monday open.

Timeline (verified, dated inside the window):
- **June 25, 2026:** Container ship *Ever Lovely* (Singapore-flagged) attacked SE of Dahit, Oman; Trump said Iran launched four drones.
- **June 26, 2026:** CENTCOM reported retaliatory strikes on Iranian missile/drone infrastructure and coastal radar, including Qeshm Island sites.
- **June 27, 2026:** Oil tanker *Kiku* (Panama-flagged) struck by an Iranian attack drone in the Strait; US responded with **airstrikes on five Iranian coastal/communications/air-defense/minelayer sites** near the Strait.
- **June 28, 2026 (Sunday):** **Iran's IRGC launched ballistic missiles and drones at the US Ali Al Salem Air Base (Kuwait) and the US Fifth Fleet HQ (Bahrain).** Kuwait intercepted two ballistic missiles (no injuries); Bahrain reported munitions hit a residential building near its airport (no fatalities). **Trump accused Iran of violating the framework MoU; Tehran threatened to halt negotiations entirely.** Iranian state TV stated Hormuz passage "still requires coordination with Iran."

**Strait of Hormuz status (as of 6/28/2026): restricted / contested.** A widened route near Oman was announced 6/27 to allow more naval traffic, but Iran reasserted that transit requires its coordination. Roughly 20% of global oil flows through Hormuz, so renewed disruption risk is the dominant commodity catalyst going into the new week.

**Market-impact read (analytical, not a confirmed weekend print):** Friday's oil collapse (~$68.86 WTI / ~$72 Brent) was built entirely on the de-escalation/supply-recovery thesis that the weekend strikes now jeopardize. A re-escalation/Hormuz-disruption premium should reassert at the Sunday/Monday open. **Spot gold/oil weekend OTC prints for 6/27–6/28 are not confirmed** (markets closed; one feed quoted gold ~$4,089.50 "as of 6/28" but weekend quotes are unreliable — labeled **as of weekend / unconfirmed**, NOT a Friday close).
