## Fed Watch

**REGIME CHANGE — new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh.** This is the single most important rates/credit story.

- **Leadership:** Jerome Powell's term as Chair expired (term end May 15, 2026). **Kevin Warsh** was confirmed by the Senate **54–45 on May 13, 2026** (the most divisive Fed confirmation vote in history) and **sworn in as the 17th Fed Chair on May 22, 2026**. He was nominated by President Trump in January 2026. The FOMC unanimously elected him its chairman (Fed press release, May 22, 2026). So in 2026, **the current Fed Chair is Warsh, not Powell.**

- **June 17, 2026 FOMC (Warsh's debut):**
  - Held the target range at **3.50–3.75%** (held for four straight meetings since Dec 2025), unanimous vote.
  - **Hawkish dot-plot flip:** median 2026 year-end fed funds projection rose to **3.8%** (from 3.4% in March) — i.e., the median now implies a **HIKE**, reversing March's implied cut. **9 of 18 participants** project at least one hike in 2026; six of those project two 25-bp hikes.
  - Statement **dropped its easing bias** and added a hawkish inflation commitment; Warsh said the Fed will be "unambiguous and unanimous" on stabilizing prices.
  - Warsh **declined to submit his own dot**, and is a known critic of the dot plot and forward guidance ("silence may be the point"). He announced **five task forces** to overhaul Fed communications, data sources, and inflation frameworks.
  - Market reaction: equities fell ~1%+; short-bill yields had their sharpest one-day jump in three months.

- **"Fed Chairman Warsh Speaks" — July 1, 2026 (the calendar event explained):** This refers to Warsh's **Policy Panel appearance at the ECB Forum on Central Banking 2026 in Sintra, Portugal** (forum runs June 29 – July 1, 2026; Warsh's panel is **July 1 at ~2:00 p.m.**), alongside ECB's Lagarde and BoC's Macklem. It is his first major podium since the FOMC and a key chance to refine the rate path before the July 28–29 meeting. NOT a separate US rate event.

- **Fed funds futures pricing (as of ~6/26–6/27/2026):**
  - **July 29 FOMC: ~69% hold** (CME FedWatch, as of 6/27/2026); a hike is the only live alternative — cuts are off the table.
  - **September 16 FOMC: ~63% probability of a 25-bp HIKE.**
  - **December 9 FOMC: ~80% probability of a hike** ("after last week's hawkish pause").
  - Terminal-rate expectations have shifted up: **BofA now forecasts three 25-bp hikes in 2026, taking the range to 4.25–4.50%.**

- **Fed speakers / tone, week of June 22–26, 2026:** Decisively hawkish. A Deutsche Bank tally cited 11 recent Fed-official speeches as hawkish, 5 neutral, 1 dovish, with most growing *more* hawkish since May. Most hawkish voters flagged: **Hammack (Cleveland), Logan (Dallas), Kashkari (Minneapolis)**. Warsh gave a tight-lipped interview (Axios, 6/26/2026) reiterating that market prices — not Fed guidance — should guide policy.

- **FOMC minutes:** The June 17 meeting minutes are **not yet released** (standard ~3-week lag puts them around July 8, 2026). No minutes were released during the 6/22–26 window. *Not found / not applicable this week.*

Contracted-cashflow nuclear fleet operators (CEG, VST, TLN) with 20-yr hyperscaler PPAs are far better insulated. Higher-for-longer also pressures long-duration project finance broadly.

## Weekend Developments (Sat 6/27 – Sun 6/28, 2026)

1. **Supreme Court Fed-independence ruling imminent (BIGGEST item).** Per US News (dated **2026-06-27**), the Supreme Court could rule **"as soon as Monday" (June 29, 2026)** on whether President Trump can remove Fed Governor **Lisa Cook**, whom Trump announced firing in August 2025. At January oral arguments all nine justices voiced doubt about a presidential power to fire Fed governors at will; the Court appears likely to let **Cook stay**. A ruling in Cook's favor **removes a major tail risk for Chair Warsh** — namely a disruptive string of Trump firings (with Warsh himself theoretically at risk). This is a direct Fed-leadership/independence catalyst landing right after the snapshot window. **Watch Monday 6/29.**

2. **US–Iran ceasefire / peace framework (the macro driver behind this week's yield drift).** The mid-June de-escalation — a 14-point MoU (the "Islamabad Memorandum," signed remotely by Trump and Iran's Pezeshkian around June 15–17, 2026) to end ~100+ days of war, reopen the Strait of Hormuz, and lift the US naval blockade — sent **Brent below $80** (down ~5% to ~$78.96; WTI ~$76) for the first time since March. Some weekend coverage (Pakistani PM Sharif "announcing the peace deal," Trump confirming) recirculated this; the *core* deal is mid-June, not a brand-new 6/27–28 event, but its disinflationary oil impulse is exactly why yields and hike-odds eased into the 6/26 close. **Caveat:** one aggregator quoted a 10Y at 4.461% / 2Y at 4.052% alongside the "weekend peace deal" framing — those 10Y/2Y figures look like an intraweek (Monday 6/22) print, not the Friday 6/26 close; I am NOT treating them as the Friday close.

3. **Warsh at Sintra (July 1)** is the next scheduled Fed catalyst — see Fed Watch. Forth/cryptobriefing coverage (late June 2026) frames the SCOTUS ruling + Sintra appearance as the two events that will "define Warsh's early tenure."
