## This Week's Take

**The lede is a war that re-ignited over the weekend.** The mid-June US–Iran ceasefire — the 60-day MoU that had crushed oil and calmed markets all week — **fractured into open military exchange on Saturday and Sunday**, and that, not last week's tech rotation, is what dominates Monday's open. After Iran droned the Panamanian-flagged crude tanker **Kiku** near the Strait of Hormuz on Saturday 6/27, **US CENTCOM struck ~10 Iranian military targets** (surveillance, comms, air-defense, drone storage, minelayer assets), and on Sunday 6/28 **Iran's IRGC fired ballistic missiles and drones at US bases in Kuwait (Ali Al Salem) and Bahrain (Fifth Fleet HQ)**. Kuwait intercepted two missiles; Bahrain reported munitions near its airport. President Trump accused Iran of violating the framework; Tehran threatened to halt talks and reasserted that Hormuz transit "requires coordination with Iran." Roughly 20% of seaborne oil moves through Hormuz, so the entire week's oil collapse — built on the de-escalation thesis — is now at risk of reversing. Sunday-evening equity-index futures were nonetheless modestly **higher** (ES +0.4–0.5%, NQ +0.5–0.6%), reading the strikes as contained *so far*; weekend Brent ticked up to ~$72.6 and WTI ~$70. This is a two-sided gap-risk setup into Monday.

**The week itself (June 22–26) was the clearest "great rotation" of 2026: out of mega-cap AI/tech, into small-caps, value and defensives.** The Nasdaq Composite fell **−4.6%** to **25,297.62** (fifth straight down session Friday) while the **S&P 500 slipped −2.0% to 7,354.02**, the **Dow rose ~+0.6% to 51,876.11**, and the **Russell 2000 made new all-time highs** (~+21% YTD). Two AI-specific catalysts did the damage: a New York Times report that **OpenAI is leaning toward delaying its IPO to 2027** (rather than accept below a $1T valuation; SoftBank −12% in Tokyo), and ongoing "AI fatigue" amplified by **SpaceX's post-IPO collapse** (IPO'd 6/12, ran to ~$225, then gave it all back). Semiconductors fell **−7.9%** on the week (SOX's worst week since March 2025) — even a blowout Micron print (84.9% gross margin, HBM sold out through 2026, record ~$50B Q4 guide) couldn't lift the group. The standout *winners* were the rotation beneficiaries: cybersecurity (**CRWD +8.3%**, **PANW +8.3%**), defensives (XLV/XLP/XLU), and quantum names on a pair of Trump executive orders.

**Behind both stories sits the structural regime change: a hawkish Fed under new Chair Kevin Warsh.** The 10Y eased ~7 bps to **~4.38%** on the week as the Iran ceasefire pulled the inflation scare back, but the market is now pricing **rate HIKES, not cuts** — ~62% odds of a September hike, ~80% by December, with BofA calling for three 2026 hikes to 4.25–4.50%. That hawkish backdrop strengthened the dollar to a 13-month high (**DXY ~101.3**), pushed gold to a **fourth straight weekly decline** (~$4,074, −3%), and helped drive a brutal week in crypto — **BTC broke below $60K** to a ~20-month low on a record **$1.79B weekly spot-ETF outflow**. The setup into Monday: a complacent, risk-on-leaning tape (VIX just 18.4) facing a re-armed Hormuz oil premium and a Fed that has told you it would rather hike than cut. Any sustained oil spike feeds straight back into the inflation problem Warsh is fighting.
