## This Week's Take

**The lede is a war, not a rotation.** The 2026 US–Iran conflict re-erupted and escalated straight through the weekend: after a June 18 ceasefire and a brief Strait of Hormuz reopening, an Iranian drone strike on a Hormuz cargo ship reignited hostilities, the US expanded airstrikes (bridges in Hormozgan province, Chabahar port) on Jul 17, Iran retaliated against Gulf allies — striking a **Kuwaiti power/water desalination plant** and, per weekend reports, a **US base in Jordan** — and by early Sunday Jul 19 the IRGC had **re-closed the Strait of Hormuz**, the artery for ~20% of world oil and gas. Crude ran **~12% on the week (>14% intraweek)** to WTI ~$82.49 / Brent ~$88.10 (Jul 17 settles) and pushed higher again into Sunday. With OPEC+ spare capacity physically stranded behind the chokepoint and VLCC tanker rates spiking toward multi-hundred-thousand-dollar/day levels, oil carries real gap-up risk into Monday's open. This is the single biggest driver for the Jul 20 session.

**The week's other story was a $1T+ AI-semiconductor selloff.** Equities logged their **first weekly loss in nearly three months** (S&P 500 −1.6%, Nasdaq Composite −2.9%, Dow −0.9%) as the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) shed ~10% — roughly 20% off its June peak. The triggers were paradoxical: China's Moonshot AI open-sourced **Kimi K3** (Jul 16), a giant MoE model that stoked fears of cheaper AI compute, and **TSMC's record Q2** came with a **capex shock** ($60–64B, plus a fresh $100B Arizona commitment) instead of relief. NVIDIA fell ~3.9% on the week and briefly lost its most-valuable-company crown. Yet this was a concentrated crowded-trade unwind, not a breadth breakdown: ~69% of the S&P 500 held above its 200-day, small caps were resilient, VIX only rose to ~18.8, and cybersecurity actually rallied (CRWD +11%, PANW +7%).

**Underneath it all, the macro regime shifted hawkish.** New Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh** used his first semiannual testimony (Jul 14–15) to hammer an inflation-first, higher-for-longer message — even as a **surprisingly soft June CPI** (−0.4% m/m, +3.5% y/y, Jul 14) and a soft June PPI drove a midweek disinflation rally that briefly killed July-hike bets. The market has now flipped from pricing 2026 *cuts* to pricing *hikes*: the Jul 28–29 FOMC is ~87% priced to hold, but futures carry **>70% odds of a hike by year-end**. Tellingly, gold *fell* ~3% during a shooting war — markets are trading Hormuz as an **inflation/energy shock, not a flight-to-safety event**. Blockbuster bank earnings (JPMorgan's record **$21.2B** quarterly profit) set an upbeat tone before the chip and geopolitical shocks took the tape.

## Positioning & Thesis Update

**Actionable takeaways, weekend developments incorporated:**

1. **Oil/energy is the dominant near-term trade.** A re-closed Hormuz with stranded OPEC+ spare capacity skews crude to gap up Monday. Energy (XLE) and oil-levered names are the cleanest expression; **defense** benefits from a widening-war premium; **transports, airlines, and consumer discretionary** face the oil-cost headwind. Watch VLCC/tanker names on the freight spike.
2. **Trade Iran as an inflation shock, not a safe-haven event.** Gold *fell* during the escalation and Treasuries barely moved — the market is pricing higher-for-longer, not recession. That argues against reflexively buying gold on the war and for respecting the Fed's hike bias; a sustained oil spike that re-accelerates inflation is bullish USD and a headwind for rate-sensitive growth.
3. **AI-semi selloff: crowded-trade unwind, not thesis break — but respect the new risk.** Kimi K3 (cheap-compute fear) + TSMC's capex flip are genuine narrative shifts. The **picks-and-shovels power-demand thesis (CEG, VST, VRT, ANET, ETN, utilities)** held up and remains the higher-quality way to own AI; direct-silicon (NVDA, MRVL, ARM) is where the volatility and the Kimi/custom-silicon overhang sit. Tesla earnings (Jul 22) is the next megacap catalyst.
4. **Cybersecurity is showing genuine leadership** — CRWD/PANW rallied while chips fell. A defensible relative-strength pocket if the AI-semi wobble persists.
5. **Fed regime has flipped hawkish.** Position for **>70% year-end hike odds** and a July hold. Front-end anchored ~4.2%, ~5% long bond, credit calm — favor quality; the risk to watch is oil-driven inflation forcing the hike debate to a head by September.
6. **Nuclear/power and critical-minerals theses intact** — uranium range-bound, CEG/VST executing on data-center demand, MP advancing the rare-earth buildout ahead of the November 2026 US–China trade expiry. **Space** is repricing post-SpaceX IPO (RKLB weak); **quantum** sold off on beta, not fundamentals (IONQ RPO +554% YoY).
7. **Crypto: Ethereum over Bitcoin on the margin** — ETH outperformed on price and ETF flows (ETHA-led), snapping an 8-week outflow streak; BTC held $64K through the weekend but sits ~49% below its ATH. Stablecoin/GENIUS-Act rulemaking and CLARITY-Act progress are the structural catalysts; watch for weekend liquidation risk if Iran escalates further.
