## Market Overview

**The week was a risk-off reversal.** After the "Trump Pump" carried equities to fresh records — the Dow closed above 53,000 for the first time ever the prior week — stocks logged their first weekly loss in nearly three months. Two forces collided: (1) a global **semiconductor selloff** touched off by China's Moonshot AI releasing the open-source **Kimi K3** model (Jul 16) alongside TSMC's capex shock and the first wave of custom AI silicon shipping; and (2) the **re-escalation of the 2026 Iran War**, which sent oil up ~14% on the week and drove a defensive rotation into energy, staples and utilities.

## Weekend Developments (breaking through Sun Jul 19, 2026)

**1) 2026 Iran War re-escalates — the dominant weekend story.** About a week after the U.S. and Iran signed a preliminary deal to end the war, an **Iranian drone struck a cargo ship in the Strait of Hormuz**, triggering a chain of hostilities that has put the two countries back on a path toward all-out war. On Jul 15 the U.S. said fresh strikes were aimed at protecting vessels; by **Jul 18** NPR reported the two sides "blow past red lines" as strikes intensified — the U.S. hitting Iranian air-defense systems, radars and 60+ IRGC small boats. Iran said Friday that U.S. strikes have killed **at least 46 people and wounded 400+** since hostilities resumed. Over the weekend the U.S. said **a third American service member was killed in action** ([CNBC, Jul 19](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/19/stock-market-today-live-updates.html)). The Strait of Hormuz — ~one-fifth of the world's traded oil/gas in peacetime — has seen commercial traffic sharply curtailed.

**2) Oil pushing higher into Sunday night.** Brent closed ~**$88.10** and WTI ~**$82.49** on Jul 17 (both +~4.5% on the day), capping a **~14% weekly gain** ([Trading Economics via HDFCSky](https://hdfcsky.com/news/brent-crude-oil-prices-today-july-17-2026-us-iran-conflict-red-sea-supply-fears)). Kuwait said Iran attacked a desalination/power plant (Jul 17). Oil moved **higher again Sunday evening** as the conflict escalated — a live inflationary/energy-sector catalyst into the Jul 20 open.

**3) Sunday-night futures (as of Jul 19, current, not a Friday close):** S&P 500 futures **-0.1%**, Nasdaq-100 futures **~flat** ([CNBC, Jul 19](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/19/stock-market-today-live-updates.html)). Muted despite the geopolitics, with oil the main mover.

**Equity read-through:** bullish for energy (XLE) and oil-levered names; a headwind for transports, consumer, and rate-sensitive growth if the oil spike sticks and re-ignites inflation fears. Watch Tesla earnings (Jul 22) and the FOMC meeting (Jul 28–29) next.

## Breadth & Sentiment

- **Breadth stayed constructive despite the tape.** ~**68.6%** of S&P 500 members were above their 200-day moving average — the highest since February — with energy and semis having led the summer rally into the pullback ([Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4614620-s-and-p-500-stocks-above-their-200-day-ma-highest-since-february)). The damage was concentrated in mega-cap semis, not broad-based; small caps (Russell 2000) held up and are having their best year in ~23 years.
- **Volatility jumped but stayed moderate.** VIX closed **18.77 on Jul 17, +12.2% on the day** (from 16.73), reflecting the chip shock + geopolitics — elevated versus the sub-17 calm, but not stress levels. ([search: VIX 7/17/26](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/%5EVIX/history/))
- **Options positioning near-neutral.** CBOE **equity put/call ratio 0.73** on Jul 17 — just above the 0.70 "bullish" line, i.e., no panic hedging into the weekend. ([ycharts](https://ycharts.com/indicators/cboe_equity_put_call_ratio))
- **Takeaway:** classic crowded-trade correction in AI semis rather than a breadth breakdown; the bigger swing factor into next week is the oil/Iran path.
