**Bottom line up front:** The dominant rates story is the regime change under new Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh**, who chaired his first FOMC in June 2026, stripped the easing bias, and this week (semiannual Monetary Policy Report testimony, Jul 14–15) doubled down on an inflation-first, higher-for-longer message. A cooler-than-expected June CPI on Jul 14 (headline -0.4% m/m, +3.5% y/y) pulled July-meeting hike bets back down, but the market has decisively flipped from pricing cuts (start of 2026) to pricing **HIKES** — with >75% odds of at least one 25 bp hike by year-end. Treasuries rallied intra-week on the soft CPI even as the front end stayed anchored by hike risk.

## Fed Watch

**Regime frame — Warsh's hawkish Fed:** Kevin Warsh became Fed Chair and chaired his first FOMC on **Jun 16–17, 2026**. That meeting held rates at 3.50–3.75% but **stripped the easing bias** from the statement, and the June SEP dot plot showed **9 of 18 participants projecting at least one 2026 rate hike** — a dramatic reversal from the ~3 cuts markets had priced at the start of the year. On the June decision day the 2Y jumped 11 bps and the 10Y 4 bps.

**This week's Fed speakers:**
- **Gov. Christopher Waller (Mon Jul 13, 2026):** Hawkish appearance (scored +1.00 on the centralbank.watch hawk-o-meter), reinforcing the inflation-first tilt ahead of Warsh's testimony.
- **Chair Kevin Warsh — Semiannual Monetary Policy Report testimony (House Financial Services, Tue Jul 14; Senate Banking, Wed Jul 15):** Called the cool June CPI "one data point," warned against declaring "mission accomplished," and said inflation "remains too high." Consistent with his stated policy of giving *less* forward guidance, he declined to signal the July decision. Notably, on Jul 15 he **pushed back on the idea that the AI capex boom is inherently inflationary**, calling AI "the most striking feature of the economy right now" and saying the Fed is "monitoring the implications." He framed the internal policy debate as "one of the good family fights."

**FOMC minutes:** None released inside the Jul 13–19 window. The June 16–17 meeting minutes were released **Jul 8, 2026** (read hawkish, ~+0.80) — just prior to this window.

**Rates pricing (fed funds futures / CME FedWatch):**
- **Next FOMC (Jul 28–29, 2026):** **~87% priced for a HOLD** at 3.50–3.75% as of Jul 18, 2026 (~13% for a hike). July-meeting hike odds had spiked mid-week (from ~18% on Jul 2 → ~36% Mon Jul 13 → nearly 50% intraday Jul 14 pre-CPI) before the soft CPI knocked them back down.
- **September (Sep 15–16) and beyond:** meaningful hike probability retained; mid-week pricing implied roughly a coin-flip for a cumulative +25 bp by September.
- **Year-end 2026:** market prices **>75% odds of at least one 25 bp hike by year-end**, i.e., a terminal rate seen drifting *higher* toward ~3.75–4.00% rather than lower. The cut-cycle narrative of early 2026 is dead for now.

One month ago (Jun 16) IG was 75 bps and HY 271 bps, so spreads have barely budged; the higher-for-longer rate regime is not (yet) bleeding into credit risk premia.
- Implication: the market is absorbing the hawkish Fed shift through *rates* (higher yields, higher term premium), not through *credit* — consistent with a solid-growth / firm-inflation backdrop rather than a slowdown scare.

## Liquidity

- **TGA (Treasury General Account):** ~**$822.5B** closing balance on Jul 16, 2026 (Daily Treasury data via YCharts); H.4.1 Wednesday level ~$796.0B as of Jul 15. The TGA has been rebuilding to the ~$800B+ zone — a mechanical **drain on system liquidity/reserves** as Treasury issuance refills the account.
- **ON RRP (overnight reverse repo):** Effectively **drained / near zero** on most days as of early–mid July 2026 (per the Fed's July 2026 Monetary Policy Report), down from the $2.5T+ 2022 peak and <$200B by late 2025. With the RRP buffer gone, reserve scarcity is now the key liquidity variable to watch as QT/TGA rebuild continues.
- **Bank lending / SLOOS:** No new Senior Loan Officer survey in this window (next quarterly release is later). Credit availability is not flashing warning signs given still-tight market spreads.

- **Bloomberg, Sat Jul 18, 2026 — "Warsh Shows His Inner Hawk as Inflation Debate Heats Up":** The key weekend item. It frames the intra-Fed "good family fight" coming to a head in the meetings ahead amid **a renewed surge in oil prices** (tied to the 2026 Iran conflict) and an **AI buildout driving up costs for technology, equipment and power** — both cited as upside inflation risks that could tilt the Committee toward a hike in H2 2026. Reinforces, over the weekend, that the July 28–29 meeting is a likely hold but the *hike* risk is live into September/Q4.
- **No weekend policy change or emergency action.** No FOMC decision, no rate move, no surprise Fed statement between Friday's close and now. The net setup into the Jul 28–29 FOMC: hold expected (~87%), hawkish guidance, and a market carrying a genuine hike bias for later in 2026 — the opposite of the easing bias priced at the start of the year.
- **Geopolitical/rates linkage to watch:** elevated oil (Iran) + AI-driven power/equipment demand are the two supply-side forces Warsh and colleagues are leaning on to justify keeping — and potentially raising — rates.
