# Eagle Eye — Crypto & Alternative (Agent 4)

*Point-in-time snapshot as of Sunday, July 19, 2026. Covers the trading week Mon Jul 13 – Fri Jul 17, 2026 plus weekend developments through Sunday Jul 19, 2026.*

**One-line take:** A soft-inflation midweek rally lifted BTC above $65K and drove Ethereum sharply higher (ETH ETFs snapped an 8-week outflow streak), but escalating US–Iran / Strait of Hormuz strikes flushed risk assets on Friday and kept crypto pinned near $64K into the weekend. Ethereum was the clear relative winner; Bitcoin remains ~49% below its Oct 2025 all-time high.

## Bitcoin

**Weekly recap (Mon Jul 13 – Fri Jul 17, 2026):**
- The week opened with a risk-off leverage flush: BTC slipped below $63,000 in Monday's Asian session, and US spot Bitcoin ETFs bled **-$424.66M on Jul 13** (the largest single-day outflow of the month). [CoinDesk, Jul 13, 2026]
- Softer inflation data (June PPI -0.3% vs flat expected; core PPI +0.2% vs +0.4%) drove a midweek rally. BTC traded **$64,570 on Jul 15** and **~$64,600 on Jul 16**, tagging the **$65,000–$65,500** resistance zone; Fed July hike odds fell to zero and September cut/hold repricing followed. [CoinDesk, Jul 15–16, 2026]
- A sixth consecutive day of US airstrikes on Iran hit risk assets Friday. BTC opened **$63,788.52** on Jul 17, printed an intraday low of **$63,130** (Yahoo) / **$62,941** (Fortune 6am ET), and closed the week near **$63,900–$64,000**. [Yahoo Finance / Fortune, Jul 17, 2026]

**Friday Jul 17, 2026 close:** ~**$63,900** (24h ref ~$63,944; the day's decline was ~1.0–1.7%).
**Current weekend price (Sun Jul 19, 2026):** ~**$64,300** (CoinDesk showed **$64,417** as of Jul 18; Yahoo **$64,245**). BTC has held the low-$64Ks through the weekend despite continued Iran escalation.

**Weekly change:** Yahoo pegged BTC **+0.9% week-over-week** (as of Fri open); pre-Iran-selloff, CoinDesk cited **+4.2% over 7 days** as of Thursday. Net: a modestly green week that gave back gains on Friday's geopolitical shock.

**Key levels / context:**
- Resistance: **$65,000** (repeatedly rejected midweek), then $65,500.
- Support: **$63,000** (defended Monday and Friday); a break risks the June low near ~$60,000.
- **Monthly: -2.8%. Yearly: -46.3%.** BTC sits **~49% below its all-time high of $126,198.07 (Oct 6, 2025)**. [Yahoo Finance, Jul 17, 2026]
- BTC market cap ~**$1.28–1.33T**; total crypto market cap ~**$2.27T** (Jul 17). [Coingabbar / Fortune, Jul 17, 2026]
- **BTC dominance: 58.3%** (Jul 16) and holding — well inside "Bitcoin season." [CoinDesk, Jul 16, 2026]
- Sentiment: Crypto Fear & Greed **27 (Fear) on Jul 17**, drifting to **33–34 (Fear) on Jul 18**. [Milk Road / Alternative.me, Jul 17–18, 2026]

## Regulation & Institutional

**Regulation:**
- **GENIUS Act (stablecoins) — rulemaking deadline hit this weekend.** The GENIUS Act was signed Jul 18, 2025; its **rulemaking deadline fell on Jul 18, 2026** — the same week the Senate resumed post-recess floor work. A structurally important date for the $310B+ stablecoin market. [PYMNTS / Latham & Watkins tracker, Jul 2026]
- **CLARITY Act (Digital Asset Market Clarity Act, market structure):** A new combined Senate Banking/Agriculture draft was expected to drop for a **late-July push**, but still lacked bipartisan buy-in and missed the July 4 target. Three unresolved fights: (1) **ethics language on officials' (incl. the President's) crypto holdings** (White House opposes); (2) **Section 604 developer protections** (law enforcement objects); (3) **stablecoin yield** (Coinbase's ~$1.35B/yr USDC rewards revenue at stake vs GENIUS Act's issuer-interest ban). Senate returned Jul 13; ~20 working days to the Aug 7 recess; needs 60 votes. [CoinDesk Jul 9 / TechTimes Jul 4 / Yahoo, 2026]
- **Visa Stablecoin Platform launched Jul 16, 2026** — enterprise rails for institutions to mint/burn/hold/transfer/redeem stablecoins without building infrastructure. Initial asset: **Open USD**, issued by an Open Standard consortium backed by **BlackRock, Alphabet, and Coinbase**. Targets 200M+ merchants. [Bloomberg / Blockhead, Jul 16–17, 2026]

**Institutional adoption:**
- **Citadel Securities invested $400M in Crypto.com on Jul 16, 2026** — the exchange's first-ever institutional funding round, at a **$20B valuation**; capital earmarked for tokenized securities, derivatives, and institutional infrastructure. A notable TradFi-into-crypto-plumbing signal. [Bloomberg / Blockhead / Crypto Briefing, Jul 16–17, 2026]
- **Strategy (MicroStrategy):** Holds **843,775 BTC** (avg cost $66,384.56; ~$33.1B) as of Jul 6, 2026. Notably, Strategy has shifted to **selective selling** — it sold ~**3,588 BTC (~$216M) in early July** to fund dividends on its Digital Credit securities, part of a new monetization framework alongside its 42/42 capital plan. A behavioral shift worth flagging for the corporate-treasury trade. [Bitbo / BeInCrypto, Jul 2026]

## Weekend Developments

**The dominant weekend driver is geopolitical, not crypto-native: the US–Iran / Strait of Hormuz crisis escalated again over Sat Jul 18.**
- Per NPR (Jul 18, 2026), the US and Iran exchanged fresh strikes on infrastructure and military targets Saturday, with the US expanding strikes to northern Iran; shipping through the Strait of Hormuz has been largely blocked since Feb 28, 2026. This kept crypto in risk-off mode into Sunday. [NPR, Jul 18, 2026]
- **Friday's strike-driven flush** was the clearest crypto liquidation event of the window: BTC fell from a ~$65,500 pre-strike high to below $64,000, triggering **~$350M+ in crypto liquidations** across leveraged positions. [Crypto Briefing, Jul 17, 2026]
- **Current state (Sun Jul 19, 2026):** BTC ~$64,300, ETH ~$1,860–1,866, SOL ~$76.33, XRP ~$1.08 — broadly holding Friday's levels; no confirmed fresh weekend liquidation cascade or major hack surfaced as of this snapshot. Fear & Greed remains in "Fear" (33–34).

*Note on data hygiene: some aggregated search results conflated this event with a prior, unrelated Iran-linked selloff that referenced BTC at $77K–$80K and ~$800M in liquidations. Those figures are NOT from July 2026 (BTC is trading ~$64K) and were excluded.*
