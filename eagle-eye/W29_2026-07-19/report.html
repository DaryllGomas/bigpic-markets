This Week’s Take

Executive Summary

Ⅰ The Lede Is a War, Not a Rotation

The 2026 US–Iran conflict re-erupted and escalated straight through the weekend. After a June 18 ceasefire and a brief Strait of Hormuz reopening, an Iranian drone strike on a Hormuz cargo ship reignited hostilities; the US expanded airstrikes (bridges in Hormozgan province, Chabahar port) on Jul 17; Iran retaliated against Gulf allies — striking a Kuwaiti power/water desalination plant and, per weekend reports, a US base in Jordan — and by early Sunday Jul 19 the IRGC had re-closed the Strait of Hormuz, the artery for ~20% of world oil and gas. Crude ran ~12% on the week (>14% intraweek) to WTI ~$82.49 / Brent ~$88.10 and pushed higher again into Sunday. With OPEC+ spare capacity physically stranded behind the chokepoint and VLCC rates spiking toward multi-hundred-thousand-dollar/day levels, oil carries real gap-up risk into Monday’s open.

🛢️ This is the single biggest driver for the Jul 20 session — energy and defense biased higher, transports and discretionary pressured by the oil-cost drag.

Ⅱ The Week’s Other Story — a $1T+ AI-Semiconductor Selloff

Equities logged their first weekly loss in nearly three months (S&P 500 −1.6%, Nasdaq Composite −2.9%, Dow −0.9%) as the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) shed ~10% — roughly 20% off its June peak. The triggers were paradoxical: China’s Moonshot AI open-sourced Kimi K3 (Jul 16), a giant MoE model that stoked fears of cheaper AI compute, and TSMC’s record Q2 came with a capex shock ($60–64B, plus a fresh $100B Arizona commitment) instead of relief. NVIDIA fell ~3.9% and briefly lost its most-valuable-company crown. Yet this was a concentrated crowded-trade unwind, not a breadth breakdown: ~69% of the S&P held above its 200-day, small caps were resilient, VIX only rose to ~18.8, and cybersecurity actually rallied (CRWD +11%, PANW +7%).

📈 A crowded-trade unwind, not a thesis break — the picks-and-shovels power-demand names held while direct silicon took the brunt.

Ⅲ Underneath It All, the Macro Regime Shifted Hawkish

New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh used his first semiannual testimony (Jul 14–15) to hammer an inflation-first, higher-for-longer message — even as a surprisingly soft June CPI (−0.4% m/m, +3.5% y/y, Jul 14) and a soft June PPI drove a midweek disinflation rally that briefly killed July-hike bets. The market has now flipped from pricing 2026 cuts to pricing hikes: the Jul 28–29 FOMC is ~87% priced to hold, but futures carry >70% odds of a hike by year-end. Tellingly, gold fell ~3% during a shooting war — markets are trading Hormuz as an inflation/energy shock, not a flight-to-safety event. Blockbuster bank earnings (JPMorgan’s record $21.2B quarterly profit) set an upbeat tone before the chip and geopolitical shocks took the tape.

🏛️ The through-line: the tail risk under this Fed is a hike, not a cut — and a sustained oil spike only sharpens it.

Ⅳ The Setup — a Geopolitics-First Tape Into a Data Vacuum

The week ahead is a light data calendar — no FOMC, CPI, jobs or GDP — and that vacuum hands the wheel to Iran/oil headlines and Tesla earnings (Wed Jul 22, after close). Every day is a geopolitics-first tape until proven otherwise. With Sunday futures muted (~−0.1%), the overnight oil gap is the swing factor into Monday, and the key single-stock event is whether the first megacap tech report of the season validates or undercuts the AI-capex/robotaxi narrative into a stock already below its $391 support. Respect the hawkish regime, watch the oil open, and treat Tesla as the week’s single-name fulcrum.

🧭 Watch the oil open above all else; trade Iran as an inflation shock, not a safe-haven event; and respect that the Fed’s tail is now a hike.

Fed & Rates Outlook

Warsh’s Hawkish Regime • The Tail Is a Hike

🏛️ Higher-for-Longer — the Market Has Flipped From Cuts to a Hike

New Chair Kevin Warsh is running an inflation-first, higher-for-longer Fed. His first FOMC (Jun 16–17) held at 3.50–3.75% but stripped the easing bias; the June dot plot showed 9 of 18 officials projecting a 2026 hike. In Jul 14–15 testimony he called the soft June CPI “one data point,” warned against declaring “mission accomplished,” and said inflation “remains too high” — while pushing back on the idea the AI capex boom is inherently inflationary (“one of the good family fights”). A surprisingly soft June CPI (−0.4% m/m, +3.5% y/y) and soft PPI drove a midweek disinflation rally that briefly killed July-hike bets — but Warsh’s pushback, and now the oil shock, have reasserted the hawkish tilt.

📉 The early-2026 cut narrative is dead: Jul 28–29 FOMC ~87% hold; >70% odds of ≥one 25 bp hike by year-end (Dec-hike odds ~73%).
