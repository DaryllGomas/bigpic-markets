# Eagle Eye — Commodities & Forex (Agent 3)
**Week 29, 2026 | Snapshot as of Sunday, July 19, 2026**
**Report window: trading week Mon Jul 13 – Fri Jul 17, 2026 + weekend breaking news through Sun Jul 19, 2026**

> **TOP LINE:** The re-eruption of the US–Iran war and a renewed Iranian blockade of the **Strait of Hormuz** is THE commodities story. Oil surged ~12% on the week; over the weekend Iran's IRGC re-closed the Strait (early Sun Jul 19 local time) and US troops were killed in a strike on a base in Jordan (Sat Jul 18). This is a live, escalating event that will dominate Monday's open. Notably, gold FELL ~3% on the week — markets are trading this as an *inflation/rate-hike* shock, not a flight-to-safety event.

## Energy

**Crude oil — the week's dominant move.** Both benchmarks posted their biggest weekly gains in months as the June 18 US–Iran ceasefire collapsed and hostilities over the Strait of Hormuz reignited.
- **WTI (front-month):** settled **~$82.49/bbl Friday Jul 17, 2026**, +4.5% on the day, its highest level in a month. ([Fortune, Jul 17 2026](https://fortune.com/article/price-of-oil-07-17-2026/); [TradingEconomics](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/crude-oil))
- **Brent (front-month):** settled **~$88.10/bbl Friday Jul 17, 2026**, +4.6% on the day, also a one-month high. ([TradingEconomics — Brent](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/brent-crude-oil); [CNBC, Jul 17 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/17/oil-price-today-brent-wti.html))
- **Weekly change:** Both benchmarks rose **~12% on the week** (some outlets cited >14% intraweek), driven almost entirely by the geopolitical risk premium. ([CNBC, Jul 17 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/17/oil-price-today-brent-wti.html))
- Friday's fresh catalyst: **Kuwait said Iran struck a power and water desalination plant** (see Weekend Developments), adding a Red Sea/Hormuz double-chokepoint risk premium.

**EIA weekly inventories (week ending Jul 10, 2026; released Jul 15–16).**
- Commercial crude **−1.7 mmbbl** to **409.7 mmbbl**, ~6% below the 5-yr average. ([Rigzone, Jul 16 2026](https://www.rigzone.com/news/usa_crude_oil_stocks_drop_almost_2mm_barrels_wow-16-jul-2026-184143-article/); [EIA WPSR](https://www.eia.gov/petroleum/supply/weekly/))
- **SPR** at **316.5 mmbbl** after a further ~3 mmbbl release — cumulative post-war drawdown approaching **~100 mmbbl**.
- Gasoline **−1.5 mmbbl** (Gulf Coast stocks lowest since 2017); distillates **+4.6 mmbbl**; refinery inputs 17.1 mb/d.

**OPEC+ / supply backdrop.**
- On **Jul 5, 2026**, seven OPEC+ producers (Saudi, Russia, Iraq, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman) agreed to raise output **+188 kb/d from August** — the 5th consecutive monthly quota increase. ([Al Jazeera, Jul 6 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/6/opec-countries-say-they-will-expand-monthly-oil-production))
- **The spare-capacity paradox:** OPEC+ holds ~4 mb/d of spare capacity, but nearly all of it sits *behind* the Strait of Hormuz. Saudi's quota rose to ~10.35 mb/d, yet the Red Sea Yanbu terminal can only export ~4–5 mb/d, leaving a delivery gap of >5 mb/d when Hormuz is shut. ([IEA — Strait of Hormuz](https://www.iea.org/about/oil-security-and-emergency-response/strait-of-hormuz); [House of Saud analysis](https://houseofsaud.com/opec-hike-hormuz-delivery-gap/))
- IEA's July OMR noted global supply had rebounded +4.1 mb/d to 98.8 mb/d in June on the (now-reversed) Hormuz reopening. ([IEA Oil Market Report, Jul 2026](https://www.iea.org/reports/oil-market-report-july-2026))
- **Sell-side upside scenarios:** Barclays has flagged a $100/bbl 2026 Brent case tied to a Hormuz impasse (~6.6 mb/d deficit); EIA's April STEO had spiked to $96 Brent before cutting to ~$82 after the June reopening — a forecast now at risk of reversal again. ([Capital.com](https://capital.com/en-int/market-updates/crude-oil-price-forecast-19-05-2026); [EIA STEO](https://www.eia.gov/outlooks/steo/))

**Natural gas — the quiet corner.** Henry Hub **~$2.91/MMBtu (Jul 17, 2026)**, +1.85% on the day but near a two-month low. Weighed by rising US output, softer demand, and reduced LNG feedgas from scheduled **Freeport LNG maintenance**. US gas remains largely insulated from the Middle East crude shock. ([TradingEconomics — Nat Gas](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/natural-gas))

## Metals

**Gold — the counterintuitive story.** Gold **fell ~3% on the week** — its biggest weekly loss since early June — even as a shooting war escalated. Spot traded around **$3,985–$4,017/oz on Jul 17, 2026** (physical held below $4,000). ([CNBC, Jul 17 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/17/gold-on-track-for-biggest-weekly-loss-in-six-as-iran-war-fans-inflation-worries.html); [TradingEconomics — Gold](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/gold); [USAGOLD, Jul 17 2026](https://www.usagold.com/daily-precious-metals-market-report-july-17-2026/))
- **Why gold fell during a war:** markets are treating the Hormuz shock as an *inflation* event, not a safe-haven event. The oil spike revived Fed rate-hike bets, and non-yielding gold struggles when rate-hike odds climb. Traders now price a **73% chance of a December rate hike** (CME FedWatch); Dallas Fed's Logan publicly called for a hike. ([Bloomberg, Jul 16 2026](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/gold-heads-for-weekly-fall-as-hormuz-strikes-raise-fed-hike-bets); [USAGOLD, Jul 17 2026](https://www.usagold.com/daily-precious-metals-market-report-july-17-2026/))
- **Silver:** **~$55.20/oz (Jul 17, 2026)**, −0.6% on the day and lower on the week, tracking gold's rate-driven slide. ([Yahoo/Bullion, Jul 17 2026](https://finance.yahoo.com/personal-finance/investing/article/gold-prices-today-friday-july-17-2026-gold-nosedives-to-nov-25-levels-as-iran-airstrikes-intensify-121125037.html))

**Copper — demand-fear vs. supply-shock.** COMEX copper **~$6.22/lb (Jul 17, 2026)**, −1.2% on the day to a one-week low, and lower on the week. The US–Iran escalation triggered a broad metals selloff on demand-destruction fears, only partly offset by supply concerns after **a powerful storm struck top producer Chile**. ([TradingEconomics — Copper](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/copper); [Business Recorder, Jul 17 2026](https://www.brecorder.com/news/40430532/copper-falls-heads-for-weekly-dip-as-mideast-tensions-weigh-on-demand-outlook)) The pullback is a demand wobble within a structurally tight market (ICSG/JPM 150–330k t 2026 deficit per the critical-minerals thesis; Freeport Grasberg restart the key near-term catalyst).

**Uranium / nuclear fuel — range-bound, thesis intact.** U3O8 spot **~$85.70/lb (Jul 17, 2026)**, **~$85.74/lb as of Jul 19, 2026** — flat over the past month but +~21% YoY, holding a tight range since April. Little direct Middle East read-through; the nuclear-fuel thesis (30–40 mlb structural deficit, Russian-enrichment decoupling) remains the driver. Watch **SPUT/URA/URNM** as the liquid proxies; **CCJ, UUUU, LEU, UEC, NXE** are the thesis names. ([TradingEconomics — Uranium](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/uranium); [MetalCharts Uranium](https://metalcharts.org/uranium-price))

**Critical-minerals note:** No new China export-control headlines in-window. The structural catalyst remains the **November 2026 US–China trade-agreement expiry**. The Chile storm is a watch item for copper/lithium supply (SQM, ALB Atacama exposure).

- **CBOT wheat (Sep):** **$6.82¾/bu (Jul 17, 2026)**, +1.2% on the day; a bullish weekly close. Ukrainian drones reportedly hit ~116 Russian vessels in the Sea of Azov over nine days; Russia shut Kerch Strait navigation; Ukraine's Kernel halted operations at Chornomorsk port. **Euronext wheat jumped 7% on Jul 15 to €231.75/t, highest since Feb 2025.** ([Brownfield, Jul 17 2026](https://www.brownfieldagnews.com/market-news/closing-grain-and-livestock-futures-july-17-2026/); [Fastmarkets, Jul 2026](https://www.fastmarkets.com/insights/russian-attacks-disrupt-ukrainian-grain-trade-shipowners-shun-black-sea/); [Cyprus Mail, Jul 16 2026](https://cyprus-mail.com/2026/07/16/russia-and-ukraine-strike-vessels-in-black-sea-wheat-prices-jump))

## Forex

**Dollar — soft-CPI weakness vs. war-premium bid.** The DXY closed **~100.76 (Jul 17, 2026)**, on track for a **weekly decline**. The driver was a surprisingly soft June inflation set — **June CPI −0.4% m/m (sharpest drop in 6 years), 3.5% y/y; core CPI flat 0% m/m, 2.6% y/y; PPI unexpectedly fell** — which collapsed near-term Fed-hike odds. But the Hormuz oil shock reintroduced inflation risk, keeping a floor under the dollar and reviving a December-hike bid. ([TradingEconomics — USD](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/currency); [TechTimes, Jul 14 2026](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320507/20260714/stunning-cpi-miss-kills-july-rate-hike-hormuz-puts-september-back-table.htm))

**Major pairs (ECB reference rates / market, Fri Jul 17, 2026):**
- **EUR/USD ≈ 1.1435** ([ECB, Jul 17 2026](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/stats/shared/pdf/eurofxref.pdf))
- **USD/JPY ≈ 162.40** — yen remains deeply weak (BoJ still lagging), a level that keeps intervention risk on the radar. ([TradingEconomics — JPY](https://tradingeconomics.com/japan/currency))
- **GBP/USD ≈ 1.34** (implied from ECB EUR/USD 1.1435 and EUR/GBP 0.8510). ([ECB, Jul 17 2026](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/stats/shared/pdf/eurofxref.pdf))

**Read-through:** The dollar's failure to rally hard on a war week — held down by disinflation — is notable. A sustained Hormuz closure that re-accelerates inflation would flip the DXY narrative back toward "higher-for-longer/hike," a headwind for gold and EM. Petro-sensitive and EM FX (and the yen) are the pairs to watch on Monday's open.

## Weekend Developments (Fri Jul 17 close → Sun Jul 19, 2026) — MARKET-MOVING

This is a fast-escalating, live conflict. Key events *after* Friday's close that will drive Monday's open:

- **Fri Jul 17:** Iran struck a **Kuwaiti power + water desalination plant** (the second such facility hit within 24 hours). ~90% of Kuwait's drinking water comes from desalination, elevating this from an energy story to a humanitarian/infrastructure one. ([Bloomberg, Jul 17 2026](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/iran-attacks-kuwait-desalination-and-power-plants-as-hostilities-worsen); [PBS, Jul 17 2026](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/as-u-s-strikes-bridges-in-iran-it-targets-a-water-desalination-plant-in-kuwait))
- **Sat Jul 18:** US and Iran exchanged strikes (US hit bridges inside Iran). **Iran attacked a US base in Jordan — 2 US service members killed, 1 missing** (CENTCOM). Total US war dead reported at **17**. ([Fox News live, Jul 18 2026](https://www.foxnews.com/live-news/iran-war-trump-israel-hormuz-oil-july-18-2026); [Washington Post, Jul 18 2026](https://washingtonpost.com/world/2026/07/18/iran-us-hormuz-strait-war-july-18-2026/4c9781c2-827b-11f1-8a16-393bd03340b0_story.html); [NPR, Jul 18 2026](https://www.npr.org/2026/07/18/nx-s1-5898916/us-iran-escalate-strikes))
- **Early Sun Jul 19 (local):** **Iran's IRGC re-closed the Strait of Hormuz** after firing a warning shot at a vessel; the US launched multiple rounds of retaliatory strikes. **Bahrain, Qatar, Kuwait, Oman and Jordan all reported incoming attacks.** Iran threatened to halt all Middle East energy exports. ([CNN live, Jul 19 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/07/19/world/live-news/iran-war-trump); [The Hill](https://thehill.com/policy/defense/iran-threatens-middle-east-oil-gas-us-blockade/))

**Why it matters for Monday:** ~20% of global oil and gas transits Hormuz. With the Strait declared closed again, OPEC+ spare capacity stranded behind it, tanker rates already at multi-hundred-thousand-dollar/day levels, and US casualties raising the odds of a wider war, oil gapped-open risk is skewed sharply higher. Watch **crude, VLCC/shipping names, defense, and the gold-as-inflation-vs-safe-haven tug-of-war.** Because commodity futures and FX are largely closed over the weekend, all Friday levels above are last-print **closes for Jul 17, 2026**; the weekend escalation is not yet priced.
